# Endometriosis: Louise's Story

## Introduction

Louise describes her experience of endometriosis, which is the second most common gynaecological condition in the UK.

## Louise's Experience

My endometriosis started when I was around 15-16 years old. My periods had never been completely regular, but before then, the pain levels were steadily increasing. I worked at a chemist, and one day I was unable to go into work because my period pain was so severe. I remember my mum telling me that "you can't just take a day off work if you're in a little bit of pain." Shortly after, I vomited in the bathroom and collapsed on the cold tiles. I was bleeding huge blood clots, and I experienced searing pain running down my spine and thighs.

The bloating is also very real. I am larger as it is, but putting on 5-8 kgs every time before my period made me feel like a lumpy, frumpy balloon (I also have PCOS, which adds to this). This resulted in stretch marks, which can devastate your mental health as a teenager who has no idea what's wrong with her body.

I had to see 5-8 different doctors before one finally gave me a referral to see a gynecologist to get exploratory surgery. Every single time I went into a doctor's office, I heard comments like:
* "Oh, you're too young to have that."
* "Your periods will sort themselves out."
* "Just lose weight and your problems will go away."

Every single doctor told me that. I wanted to scream at them and insist that something was wrong with my body.

## Advice for Others

My advice for people with endometriosis or who are in the process of trying to get diagnosed is to:

* **Listen to your body.**
* **Don't give up.**

I've vomited and cried out of pain so many times, had excision surgery, and tried all the things. If you need to rest, then rest. If you need a heat pack and chocolate, treat yourself to a heat pack and a whole block of your favourite chocolate. Your mental health is more important. You are important.
