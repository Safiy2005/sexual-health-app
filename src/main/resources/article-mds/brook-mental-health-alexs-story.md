# Mental Health: Alex’s Story

Alex, 24, shares his experience of how mental health problems have impacted his life.

**Content Warning:** This page includes discussion of mental illness.

## Introduction
Alex first experienced mental health problems at nine years old and has managed them daily since. The impact and severity of these challenges vary significantly over time, affecting his education, social life, physical health, and family dynamics.

## Impact on Education
Mental health issues have had a major impact on Alex's academic journey:
* **Early Education:** Alex was out of school for nearly two years at age nine, missing the crucial transition from primary to secondary school, which set him back academically.
* **Life Skills:** Due to his absence, he missed out on essential sex education and other vital life skills.
* **Higher Education:** Despite these setbacks, he reached university with the support of a Special Educational Needs (SEN) department. However, a major relapse during his second year forced him to leave his course.

## Social Life and Relationships
Mental health problems, particularly anxiety, have influenced Alex's ability to navigate social environments:
* **Isolation:** For long periods during his teenage and young adult years, Alex felt isolated and lonely.
* **Building Connections:** Anxiety made creating and maintaining friendships and relationships incredibly difficult.
* **Recovery:** Through recent therapy, Alex has seen significant improvements, though he continues to treat new social situations as part of an ongoing learning curve.

## Health and Medication Side Effects
The treatment for mental health problems, specifically antidepressants, brought about various physical and sexual side effects:
* **General Side Effects:** Alex experienced dry mouth, tiredness, nightmares, weight gain, and insomnia.
* **Sexual Health:** Common but often stigmatized side effects included a loss of libido, erectile and ejaculation difficulties, and, for others, problems reaching orgasm or a lack of vaginal lubrication. 
* **Advocacy:** These experiences led Alex to become interested in sexual health advocacy and to begin volunteering for Brook. He is now on a combination of medications that effectively manage his symptoms with limited side effects.

## Impact on Family
Alex’s mental illness has affected his family life:
* **Caregiving:** His parents acted as full-time carers throughout his teenage years and during his recent relapse.
* **Family Dynamics:** The time and care required to support Alex impacted the entire household, including his sister. 

## Current Progress
Through a combination of intense specialist therapy and medication, Alex has developed strategies and tools to manage his mental health. While every day remains a challenge, he continues to make major progress in his recovery.

## Seeking Support
Approximately 1 in 4 people in the UK will experience a mental health problem each year (NHS England, 2020). If you are worried about your own mental health or that of someone else, support is available. You do not need a formal diagnosis to talk to someone or seek help.
