# Chronic BV and Thrush: Nadine’s Story

Nadine shares her personal experience living with chronic bacterial vaginosis (BV) and thrush, detailing the emotional and physical impact these conditions had on her life and the lessons she learned about management and self-acceptance.

## The Emotional Toll of Chronic Infections

Living with a body that feels like it is failing can be soul-destroying. Whether dealing with chronic pain or persistent infections, these conditions often leave individuals feeling powerless, frustrated, and hopeless. For Nadine, chronic BV and thrush led to a cycle of questioning why medications failed and why doctors couldn't provide definitive solutions.

## The Pursuit of a Cure

The journey to find relief often involves significant personal and financial costs. Nadine's experience included:
* Joining long waiting lists for specialists.
* Spending personal savings on private healthcare in London.
* Researching complex medical journals late at night.
* Following restrictive advice from online communities, such as extreme dietary changes.
* Investing in expensive, unproven probiotic subscriptions.

Desperation can make individuals vulnerable to companies that capitalise on the pursuit of "getting better," even when the solutions offered are not evidence-based.

## Impact on Quality of Life

Chronic infections often affect more than just physical health; they impact social and emotional well-being. Nadine experienced:
* Embarrassment regarding sexual intimacy.
* Avoidance of certain clothing (only wearing baggy clothes).
* Relationship strain and eventual breakdown.
* Social isolation and a constant fear that "wrong" choices would make the infection permanent.

## A Change in Perspective

A turning point occurred during a holiday when Nadine decided to stop her restrictive routines to be present with her loved ones. She discovered that:
* Eating a normal diet and participating in activities (like taking baths) did not necessarily worsen her symptoms.
* The stress of trying to control every variable was often more damaging than the condition itself.
* Honesty with friends and family about her health helped alleviate the emotional burden.

## Managing Instead of Curing

While Nadine still lives with the infections, her approach has shifted from an "agonising pursuit" of a cure to a focus on management and self-love. She emphasizes several key takeaways:
* **Focus on what you can control:** You can choose how you connect with others and how you treat yourself.
* **Self-compassion:** Treating yourself as someone deserving of care, regardless of physical health.
* **Resilience:** Accepting that there will be bad days, but also many good days.
* **Identity beyond illness:** Remembering that you are more than the "failing parts" of your body.

By choosing to manage her infections rather than being consumed by the need to cure them, Nadine found a way to move forward with her life.
