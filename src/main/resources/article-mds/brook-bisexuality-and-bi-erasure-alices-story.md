# Bisexuality and bi-erasure: Alice's story

For Pride month, Alice (she/her) shares her experience with her identity as a bisexual woman in a straight-presenting relationship and the challenges posed by bi-erasure.

## Identity in a Straight-Presenting Relationship

Being a bisexual woman in a relationship with a man often leads to the misconception that one's "queer journey" has ended. Alice notes that while she is in a supportive relationship with a partner who actively learns about LGBTQ+ culture, she often feels "outside of" the community. Because her partner is a cisgender man and they are in a monogamous relationship, her bisexual identity is frequently explained away or ignored by society.

## Understanding Bi-erasure

Bi-erasure is the tendency to ignore, remove, or explain away evidence of bisexuality in culture, media, or news. Society often views sexual orientation through a binary lens—labelling individuals as either straight or gay. This results in bisexual people being dismissed or facing biphobic tropes, such as:

* "It’s just a phase."
* "Bisexual people are just greedy."
* "Isn’t everyone a little bit bi?"

This lack of visibility can lead to internal conflict and feelings of invalidation. Despite being in a straight-presenting relationship, Alice maintains her queer identity through the media she consumes, the music she listens to, and her advocacy for the LGBTQ+ community.

## The Mental Health Impact of Bi-erasure

The consequences of bi-erasure extend beyond social exclusion. Research indicates that bisexual individuals experience higher rates of mental health issues—including depression, anxiety, and suicidal ideation—compared to other groups within the LGBTQ+ community. This is often attributed to the stress of having to hide or constantly defend one's identity.

## Combating Bi-erasure through Representation and Education

To challenge bi-erasure, there must be a concerted effort toward better representation and inclusive education:

* **Media Representation:** There is a need for more positive, accurate portrayals of bisexual individuals in media and online spaces to celebrate the identity rather than dismiss it.
* **Inclusive Education:** Relationship and Sex Education (RSE) should move beyond a binary focus on heterosexual marriage and penetrative sex. By discussing LGBTQ+ love and diverse experiences, education can validate the identities of young people.
* **Adopting an Inclusive Lens:** Approaching others with the assumption that they could identify in any way—rather than assuming heterosexuality—promotes kindness, respect, and connection.

## Tips for Celebrating Bisexual Identity

Alice suggests the following ways to embrace and champion a bisexual identity:

* **Find supportive people:** Surround yourself with individuals who champion your identity.
* **Keep learning:** Engage with books, TV, films, and podcasts that center bisexual voices in a positive way.
* **Celebrate bisexual voices:** Engage with the community to help others feel proud of their identity.
* **Visible advocacy:** Where safe, wearing the bi flag or speaking up can challenge erasure and set a positive example for others.
