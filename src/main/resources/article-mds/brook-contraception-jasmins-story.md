# Contraception: Jasmin’s Story

Jasmin, 23, shares her personal journey with contraception, from starting the pill at age 14 to finding a method that better suited her physical and mental health. Her story highlights the importance of finding the right fit and being heard by medical professionals.

## Starting the Contraceptive Pill

At age 14, Jasmin visited her GP with her mother to discuss contraception. Although she was not yet sexually active, the primary goals were to ensure future safety and to manage heavy periods.

The doctor prescribed **Rigevidon**, a combined oral contraceptive pill taken daily for three weeks, followed by a one-week break. Jasmin remained on this method until she was 17, but experienced significant side effects:

* **Weight gain**: Physical changes that occurred while on the pill.
* **Mental health impact**: A negative effect on her diagnosed anxiety disorder.
* **Emotional changes**: Feeling erratic, overly emotional, and "nasty" leading up to the withdrawal bleed.

Despite concerns from friends and family that these were "just natural hormones," Jasmin felt that the medication was contributing to her distress and decided to seek a change.

## Finding Support at the GP

Jasmin’s experience with her GP was positive and validating. During the consultation, she learned that:

* It is common for certain types of hormonal contraception to affect mood and anxiety.
* The specific pill she was taking secreted more hormones than some other methods, which can intensify side effects for some users.
* Patients should feel empowered to voice concerns and expect to be met with facts rather than ignorance.

## Transitioning to the Contraceptive Implant

After discussing various options, Jasmin chose the **contraceptive implant**. This is a small, flexible device inserted under the skin of the upper arm that provides protection for up to three years.

### The Procedure
The fitting involved a small procedure using a local anaesthetic to numb the area before the device was inserted. 

### Adjustment Period
The first six months presented some challenges as her body adjusted to the new hormone levels:
* Constant additional spotting.
* Initial concerns about whether the method was working correctly.

### Long-term Results
After the initial adjustment period, Jasmin found the implant to be highly effective for her body. She has now used the implant for six years (three rounds). Her results include:
* Significantly lighter periods lasting only 2–3 days.
* Irregular but manageable cycles.
* Improved mental and physical wellbeing compared to her time on the pill.

## Conclusion

Jasmin emphasizes that while the pill did not work for her, it remains a viable and effective option for many others. Contraception is highly individual, and what works for one person may not work for another. She encourages anyone who feels "something does not feel right" with their contraception to speak with a healthcare professional and explore alternative options.
