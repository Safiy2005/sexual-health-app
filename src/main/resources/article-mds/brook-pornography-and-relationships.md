# Pornography and Relationships

If you are over 18, you may choose to watch pornography, either alone or with a partner. Some people choose to watch porn frequently, whereas others may watch it occasionally or not at all. If you do decide to watch it, you should only watch what you feel comfortable with.

## Benefits of Open Communication
Research shows that when people in relationships have open and honest discussions about their individual pornography use, it can have positive consequences. Talking about your porn use or watching porn together can:

* Help you learn more about your partner’s likes and dislikes.
* Make your sex life more varied.
* Improve general communication because of the intimacy that comes from being open.
* Help manage differences in libido (desire for sex) between partners.

## Age and the Law
If you are under 18, it is not illegal to watch porn, but it is illegal to show porn to someone else who is under 18. This means that if a young person shows another young person a porn video, they could be committing a criminal offence.

## Challenges and Boundaries
Despite potential benefits, porn use is still a common subject of conflict in relationships. Studies show that if one person feels their partner’s porn use is problematic—perhaps due to its frequency or the type of content—it can negatively affect the relationship and the self-esteem of the person who does not watch porn.

The strongest relationships are built on communication and trust. Whether or not watching porn counts as "cheating" is something that only you and your partner can decide. Like sexting or interacting with others on social media, each relationship must establish its own boundaries and mutual agreements on what is acceptable.

## When Pornography Becomes Harmful
Pornography can become harmful and negatively affect your relationship if:

* You watch porn secretly and are not open about it with your partner.
* You compare your partner or yourself to the performers in porn and let it affect your expectations of real-life sex.
* You attempt things with your partner that you have seen in porn without asking for their consent beforehand.
* Your porn use affects your sex life, such as a decreased desire for sex with your partner or finding it difficult to get aroused without porn.

## Pornography vs. Reality
It is important to remember that pornography does not accurately represent sex. Elements of healthy, real-life sexual encounters are often missing from pornographic content, including:

* The use of condoms.
* Clear communication and consent.
* The use of lube.
* The natural "mess" of sex.
* A diverse range of body types and sexual acts.
