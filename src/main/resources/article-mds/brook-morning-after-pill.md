# Emergency Contraception: The Morning After Pill

If you’ve had unprotected sex, you may be able to prevent pregnancy by taking the morning after pill within five days. There are two types of emergency contraception: the morning after pill and the intrauterine device (IUD), also known as the copper coil.

The morning after pill works by delaying or preventing the release of an egg (ovulation). In the UK, there are two types of morning after pill. Both are less effective than the copper coil because they only prevent pregnancy if ovulation hasn’t already happened, whereas the copper coil can be used to prevent pregnancy at any time during your menstrual cycle.

**Note:** The morning after pill does not protect you from sexually transmitted infections (STIs). If you have had unprotected sex, it is a good idea to get tested.

## Types of Morning After Pill

The morning after pill is more effective the sooner you take it after unprotected sex.

* **Levonorgestrel (LNG):** Must be taken within 96 hours (four days) of unprotected sex, but is most effective within the first 72 hours (three days).
* **Ulipristal Acetate (UPA / ellaOne):** Must be taken within 120 hours (five days) of unprotected sex.

## Where to Get the Morning After Pill

You can get the morning after pill for free from:
* Pharmacies (often without an appointment or prescription)
* Brook centers
* Sexual health clinics
* Your GP

## What to Expect

When you seek emergency contraception, a healthcare professional will ask you questions to determine the most suitable method. These may include:
* When you had unprotected sex
* Where you are in your menstrual cycle
* Whether you are taking any other medications
* Whether you use a regular method of contraception

## Levonorgestrel (LNG)

This pill contains a progestogen hormone. 
* **Effectiveness:** Most effective within 72 hours, though it can be used up to 96 hours. It only works if taken before ovulation.
* **BMI:** Effectiveness may be reduced if the person has a high body mass index (BMI).
* **Brands:** Levonelle, Levonelle One Step, or Upostelle.
* **Future Protection:** It does not protect you from pregnancy if you have further unprotected sex.

## Ulipristal Acetate (UPA) / ellaOne

This pill works by stopping progesterone from working normally, which delays or prevents ovulation.
* **Effectiveness:** If taken within five days (120 hours), it is almost 98% effective. It only works if taken before ovulation.
* **BMI:** Effectiveness may be reduced for those with a high BMI.
* **Brand:** ellaOne is the only brand of this type available in the UK.
* **Future Protection:** It does not protect you from pregnancy if you have further unprotected sex.

## Factors That Can Reduce Effectiveness

Certain factors can make the emergency contraceptive pill less effective:
* **Delaying treatment:** LNG is most effective when taken within the first 12 hours.
* **Vomiting:** If you vomit within three hours of taking the pill.
* **Drug interactions:** Certain medicines for epilepsy, HIV, TB, or the herbal remedy St John’s Wort.
* **Timing:** If ovulation has already occurred.
* **Body Mass Index:** Having a high BMI.

## Side Effects

There are no serious short or long-term side effects, but some people may experience:
* Dizziness or tiredness
* Headaches
* Breast tenderness
* Abdominal pain
* Vomiting
* Disrupted periods or irregular bleeding

## Pregnancy, Birth, and Breastfeeding

* **After Birth:** You can get pregnant as soon as three weeks after giving birth. LNG can be used from day 21. While a small amount of hormone may enter breast milk, it is not thought to be harmful.
* **After ellaOne:** Avoid breastfeeding for one week after taking ellaOne.
* **Abortion/Miscarriage:** You can take the morning after pill if you have unprotected sex following an abortion or miscarriage.

## Restarting Regular Contraception

### After Levonorgestrel (LNG)
* **Hormonal methods (pill, patch, ring):** Start or restart within 12 hours of taking LNG.
* **Additional protection:** Use condoms for 7 days (9 days for Qlaira; 2 days for the progestogen-only pill).
* **Injections:** Use additional protection for 7 days after restarting.

### After ellaOne
* **Wait Period:** You must wait **five days** after taking ellaOne before starting or restarting any hormonal contraception.
* **Additional protection:** Use condoms for the 5-day waiting period, plus an additional 7 days once you start your method (9 days for Qlaira; 2 days for the progestogen-only pill).

## How Do I Know if it Worked?

If your next period arrives at the expected time and has a normal flow, the pill has likely worked. You should see a doctor or nurse if:
* Your period is lighter or shorter than usual.
* You miss your next period or withdrawal bleed.
* A pregnancy test is recommended three weeks after the unprotected sex to ensure accuracy.

## FAQs

### What counts as unprotected sex?
Unprotected sex includes sex without contraception, a condom breaking or coming off, or forgetting to take your regular contraceptive pill/change your patch or ring.

### What if I had unprotected sex more than five days ago?
The morning after pill will no longer work. You should take a pregnancy test as soon as your period is late, or three weeks after the unprotected sex.

### Can I take it during my period?
Yes. It is possible to get pregnant at any point in your cycle because sperm can survive for up to five days inside the body.

### How many times can I take it?
There is no limit to how many times you can take the morning after pill in your lifetime. You can take the same type of pill again in the same cycle if needed, but you should consult a professional before mixing different types of emergency pills in one cycle.

**Note:** Emergency contraception is intended for emergencies only and is not reliable enough to be used as a regular method of contraception.
