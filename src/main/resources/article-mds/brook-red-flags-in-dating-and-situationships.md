# Red Flags in Dating and Situationships

It can be hard to know if someone is right for you or if a situation could lead to a healthy relationship. When talking to someone new, as well as exploring compatibility, it is important to look for signs that getting involved might not lead to a healthy, fulfilling relationship. These signs are often referred to as ‘red flags’.

## Love Bombing

Although it can feel exciting or romantic, "love bombing"—receiving excessive love, compliments, gifts, and affection from a very early stage—can be a sign of problems later on.

### Signs of love bombing
* Showering you with affection, gifts, and compliments very early in the relationship.
* Declaring you are "the one" or their "soulmate" before they truly know you.
* Talking about a long-term future together after only a few dates.
* Wanting to spend all their free time with you and demanding you do the same.
* Demanding constant communication and immediate replies, even when you are busy.
* Moving too fast by wanting to define the relationship or make commitments prematurely.

### Why is love bombing a red flag?
While feeling desired is positive, feeling consumed by another person's interest can be problematic. Some people love bomb due to attachment issues, while others use it to gain control. It can cause you to lose your sense of self and become overly reliant on the other person for happiness. A partner should be a positive addition to your life, not your entire world.

### How to deal with love bombing
* **Set boundaries:** Explain that you need things to slow down.
* **Monitor their reaction:** If they do not respect your boundaries, it may not be worth continuing.
* **Manage your own attachments:** If you feel the urge to message constantly, try turning your phone off or planning activities to maintain your independence.

### What to look for instead:
* Someone who respects your space and sets their own boundaries.
* Someone with their own interests and a life outside the relationship.
* Gifts and affection that are proportionate to the stage of the relationship.
* Someone interested in getting to know you in the present rather than focusing solely on the future.

## Negging

‘Negging’ is a form of manipulation where someone tries to make you feel bad about yourself so you lose confidence and seek their approval.

### Signs of negging
* Acting as if they are superior to you or doing you a "favour" by spending time with you.
* Making negative comments about your appearance, intelligence, or life choices.
* Targeting your insecurities and dismissing it as "banter" or "flirting."
* Repeatedly making fun of you.
* Expecting you to prove your worth to them.

### Why is negging a red flag?
Being made to feel inadequate impacts your mental health and can bleed into other areas of your life, such as work or friendships. Healthy partners do not try to zap your happiness or make you feel worthless.

### How to deal with negging
In the early stages, it is usually best to stop talking to the person. You do not need to explain yourself or try to change them. If it happens in a long-term relationship, explain how it makes you feel and see if their behavior changes.

### What to look for instead:
* Someone who treats you as an equal.
* Someone who supports your growth and likes you as you are.
* Someone who does not make you compete for their time.

## Breadcrumbing

Breadcrumbing occurs when someone offers the bare minimum of attention to keep you interested without ever intending to commit.

### Signs of breadcrumbing
* Messaging randomly out of the blue, often when they are bored.
* Going days or weeks without replying.
* Sounding interested in meeting up but never committing to actual plans.

### Why is breadcrumbing a red flag?
If someone is genuinely interested, they will make a consistent effort. Breadcrumbing often means you are being kept as a "back-up" while they pursue other interests or avoid readiness for a relationship.

### How to deal with breadcrumbing
The most effective way to handle breadcrumbing is to block or unmatch. Save your energy for people who show genuine effort.

### What to look for instead:
* Good communicators who are clear about what they are looking for.
* People who follow through with plans and show consistent interest.

## Gaslighting

Gaslighting is a manipulation tactic that makes you question your own reality, feelings, or interpretations of a situation.

### Signs of gaslighting
* Dismissing your emotions with phrases like "calm down" or "it’s not that deep."
* Denying that events occurred or claiming they never said something that you know they did.

### Why is gaslighting a red flag?
It causes you to lose confidence in your own judgment and makes you feel like you are always in the wrong.

### How to deal with gaslighting
If someone makes you question yourself early on, it is unlikely to lead to a healthy relationship. Lean on friends and family for perspective and confidence. In long-term scenarios, communicate your feelings, but be prepared to walk away if the behavior persists.

### What to look for instead:
* Someone who listens to your feelings and validates your perspective.
* Someone who works with you to resolve issues rather than dismissing them.

## Jealousy

While some jealousy is normal, it becomes toxic when a partner makes their insecurity your problem.

### Signs of unhealthy jealousy
* Becoming upset when you spend time with friends or family.
* Reacting negatively when you receive attention from others.

### Why is jealousy a red flag?
Unchecked jealousy often leads to controlling behavior, such as telling you what to wear, who you can talk to, or what you can post online.

### How to deal with jealousy
If there is no foundation of trust, the relationship cannot be healthy. You can try to talk about the roots of their jealousy and set boundaries, but if the behavior becomes controlling, it is best to end the relationship.

### What to look for instead:
* Someone who is open and honest about their feelings without being controlling.
* Someone who trusts you and does not try to provoke jealousy.

## Coercive Control

Coercive control involves using a pattern of behavior to make you act a certain way or stay in a relationship. This includes controlling your appearance, your social circle, or your digital life. Coercive control is a sign of an abusive relationship. If you see these red flags early on, it is vital not to ignore them.
