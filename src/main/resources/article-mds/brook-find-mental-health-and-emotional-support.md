# Find Mental Health and Emotional Support

## Mental Health & Wellbeing

We all need help sometimes. These organisations are there to offer support with whatever you have going on. You don’t have to be diagnosed with a mental illness, you don’t even have to know exactly what’s wrong. Whenever you are ready to talk to someone, you can.

### Organisations Specifically for Young People

* **Brook:** Brook offers a range of wellbeing services for adults and young people.
 * Explore Brook’s wellbeing support.
* **Beat:** Beat is the UK’s eating disorder charity. They provide individuals experiencing an eating disorder, and their friends and family, a safe place where they feel listened to, supported and empowered to help them beat their eating disorders.
 * Call the adult helpline: 0808 801 0677
 * Call the student helpline: 0808 801 0811
 * Call the youth helpline: 0808 801 0711
 * Send them an email (adults)
 * Send them an email (young people)
 * Visit the Beat website
* **Campaign Against Living Miserably (CALM):** The Campaign Against Living Miserably (CALM) is leading a movement against suicide, the single biggest killer of men under 45 in the UK. They run a free and confidential helpline and webchat for anyone who needs to talk about life’s problems.
 * Call 0800 58 58 58
 * Talk to someone via webchat
 * Visit the CALM website
* **Samaritans:** Samaritans is a safe place for you to talk any time you like, in your own way, about whatever’s getting to you. They won’t tell you what to do – they listen and try to understand what you’re going through. It’s free and confidential for people of any age across the UK & Republic of Ireland.
 * Call 116 123 to speak to a Samaritan
 * Email a Samaritan
 * Visit the Samaritans website
* **SANE:** SANE provides emotional support, guidance and information to anyone affected by mental illness, including families, friends and carers. They provide this support through SANEline, the only national out-of-hours specialist helpline for people affected by mental illness.
 * Textcare: comfort and care via text message
 * Visit the peer support forum
 * Visit the SANE website
* **Shout:** Shout is the UK’s first free 24/7 text service for anyone in crisis anytime, anywhere. It’s a place to go if you’re struggling to cope and you need immediate help. If you are experiencing a personal crisis, are unable to cope and need support, text ‘SHOUT’ to 85258.
 * Visit the Shout website
* **Switchboard:** Switchboard is a confidential LGBT+ helpline there to listen and support you with anything that you want to talk about. All of their volunteers self-define as LGBT+ and they are trans-friendly and nonbinary-friendly.
 * Call 0300 330 0630
 * Email Switchboard
 * Visit the Switchboard website
* **Childline:** Childline is there to help anyone under 19 in the UK with any issue they’re going through. Whether it’s something big or small, their trained counsellors are there to support you. Childline is free, confidential and available any time, day or night.
 * Call 0800 1111 to speak to a counsellor
 * Visit the Childline website
 * Chat to a counsellor 1-2-1
 * Email Childline
* **HOPELineUK:** HOPELineUK is a confidential support and advice service offered by Papyrus for young people under the age of 35 across the UK who may be having thoughts of suicide, or anyone concerned that a young person may be having thoughts of suicide.
 * Call 0800 068 4141
 * Text 07860039967
 * Email: pat@papyrus-uk.org
 * Visit the Papyrus website
* **The Mix:** The Mix is for anyone under 25 living in any part of the UK. They are there to help you take on any challenge you’re facing – from mental health to money, from homelessness to finding a job, from break-ups to drugs and alcohol.
 * Call 0808 808 4994 to speak to a trained supporter
 * Chat 1-2-1 by texting THEMIX to 85258
 * Visit The Mix website
* **Youth Wellbeing Directory:** The Youth Wellbeing Directory provides a list of free local and national organisations for anyone up to the age of 25, along with important information you may find helpful. Created by the Anna Freud National Centre for Children and Families.
 * View the Youth Wellbeing Directory on the Anna Freud Centre website
* **Kooth:** Forum and online chat for young people that allows you to access support from other young people and professionals. Kooth is available in certain areas of the UK.
 * Visit the Kooth website
* **YoungMinds:** YoungMinds offers webpages with help and advice on mental health and wellbeing topics. Including a guide to help you if you’re thinking about reaching our for support but don’t know how to.
 * Visit the YoungMinds website
