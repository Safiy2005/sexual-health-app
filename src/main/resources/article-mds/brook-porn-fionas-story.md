# Porn: Fiona’s Story

Fiona shares her personal account of how pornography impacted her relationship, her mental health, and her self-esteem. Her story highlights the complexities of navigating boundaries and the emotional toll of one-sided sexual dynamics.

## The Beginning of the Relationship

Fiona began seeing her ex-partner when she was 22 and he was 32. Having known him since she was 14, she initially saw him as a supportive figure, particularly following the breakdown of her relationship with her daughter’s father.

However, pornography was introduced early in the relationship. What started as an occasional addition to foreplay soon became a central fixture of their sexual and daily lives.

## The Escalation of Pornography Use

As the relationship progressed, the use of pornography became pervasive and increasingly one-sided. Fiona observed several concerning behaviors:

* **Daily use:** Her partner would use the internet for pornography in the morning before she woke up.
* **Constant consumption:** He viewed men's magazines at work and used DVDs or the internet during intercourse.
* **Escalating demands:** He began introducing video recorders, various sex toys, and other objects into their sex life.
* **Boundary crossing:** He requested that Fiona expose herself to his friends, a boundary she firmly maintained.

## Impact on Self-Esteem and Mental Health

The constant presence of pornography led Fiona to feel that she was "not enough" and that her partner was always looking for something else. This resulted in:

* **Depression:** Fiona began to suffer from depression as a result of the relationship dynamics.
* **Feelings of worthlessness:** She frequently told her partner she felt worthless, but while he would pretend to understand, his behavior rarely changed for more than 24 hours.
* **Shame and isolation:** Fiona found it difficult to talk to others about her experience, fearing that the situation was a result of her own "failings" as a woman or an inability to satisfy her partner.

## Seeking Resolution

Despite her attempts to address the issue, her partner refused to acknowledge that pornography was affecting their relationship. He declined relationship counselling, insisting that there was no problem. 

Fiona notes that while she remains open-minded about pornography in a general sense—having used it occasionally when masturbating alone—she believes its role in this specific relationship was deeply unhealthy, both emotionally and sexually.

## Help and Support with Pornography

If you feel that pornography is becoming an issue in your relationship, it is important to seek support. There are several resources available to help you navigate these conversations and find professional guidance:

* **The Mix:** Provides advice on how to talk to your partner if you find pornography on their devices.
* **Relate:** Offers dedicated counselling for partners of pornography users via live chat, webcam, and telephone.
* **Thinkuknow:** Provides general advice for individuals who may need help addressing their own use of pornography.
* **Sexaholics Anonymous UK:** Offers free, confidential workshops throughout the UK for those struggling with pornography use.
