# Online spaces helped me discover my identity: Lois’ Story

Lois, 19, shares her experience of how online spaces and digital communities facilitated her journey of self-discovery regarding her sexuality and gender identity. For many young people, particularly Gen Z, the internet provides a unique environment to explore identity without the immediate scrutiny often found in physical environments.

## The Role of Online Communities

Growing up as part of the first generation raised with ubiquitous internet access, Lois found that online communities provided safe, accepting spaces that were unavailable to her in real life. These digital spaces allow individuals to:
* Connect with others who share similar experiences.
* Find representation that may be lacking in local communities.
* Access information that fills gaps left by traditional sex education.

## Navigating Change and Self-Discovery

One of the primary benefits of online spaces is the ability to evolve without the pressure of providing constant explanations. 
* **Fluidity:** Online platforms allow users to drift between communities as their understanding of themselves changes.
* **Privacy:** Exploring different labels or identities can be done at an individual's own pace.
* **Curating Content:** At age 15, Lois "cleansed" her social media by unfollowing accounts that caused anxiety and instead followed body-positive and sex-positive creators.

## Exploring Sexuality

Lois initially identified as cisgender and straight, partly due to a lack of comprehensive sex education and the presence of internalized homophobia. Her transition to understanding her own bisexuality and pansexuality was aided by:
* **Exposure:** Learning about different sexualities through online creators.
* **Normalisation:** Seeing others live authentically helped reduce feelings of shame and guilt.
* **Validation:** Realizing that romantic and sexual attractions outside of heteronormativity are valid.

## Understanding Gender Identity

Around 18 months ago, Lois began exploring her gender identity. The internet provided the vocabulary and representation needed to understand feelings that did not align with the binary "woman" label.
* **Non-binary identity:** Discovering this term allowed her to resonate with a middle ground that "tomboy" did not fully capture.
* **Aesthetics vs. Identity:** Lois experimented with her appearance, eventually shaving her head to separate her physical aesthetics from societal expectations of femininity.
* **Pronouns:** She currently uses she/they pronouns, embracing the idea that gender, like sexuality, can be fluid.

## The Impact of Online Representation

While the online world can sometimes feel vast or lonely, its primary value for the LGBTQ+ community lies in representation.
* **Finding Role Models:** Following advocates like Chella Man helped Lois realize she did not have to exist within traditional binaries.
* **Global Connection:** Knowing that similar people exist, even thousands of miles away, provides essential reassurance.
* **Individual Journeys:** Online spaces demonstrate that every person’s path to self-discovery is unique.

## Advice for Self-Exploration

Lois emphasizes that identity is often a fluid, ever-changing journey. Her advice for others includes:
* **Reducing Stress:** Do not feel pressured to have all the answers immediately.
* **Embracing Change:** It is okay for labels and feelings to shift over time.
* **Individuality:** You have no obligation to belong to a specific community; the most important thing is to be yourself.
* **Seeking Support:** There are communities and individuals online who offer love and acceptance for who you are.
