# Find the best condom for you

If you find condoms uncomfortable or think they ruin sex, you likely just haven’t found the right one for you yet. Finding the correct condom can help prevent them from breaking or slipping off, while also increasing intimacy and enjoyment.

Penises come in all shapes and sizes, and luckily, condoms do too. It is generally better for a condom to have more length or less width rather than being too wide or too short.

## Why size matters

Choosing the correct condom size is essential for both comfort and safety. Using the wrong size leaves you and your partner vulnerable to STIs and pregnancy.

* **Too big:** If a condom is too long or wide, it may roll up at the base, be saggy around the mid-section, or slip off during sex.
* **Too small:** A condom that is too tight is at a much higher risk of splitting.

Condoms generally come in three sizes: small, medium, and large. Because sizes differ between brands, always check the packaging measurements. If your measurements don't match a size exactly, choose the nearest size and experiment until you find the most comfortable fit.

## How to measure for a condom

To find the right fit, you need to measure your penis while it is erect (hard).

1. **Length:** Place a ruler or measuring tape at the base of the penis and measure to the very tip.
2. **Width (Circumference):** Wrap a measuring tape (or a piece of string to be measured against a ruler afterward) around the widest part of the penis to find the circumference.

Use these measurements to compare against condom packaging or online size charts.

## Condom Materials

Most condoms are made of latex, but several alternatives exist for those with allergies or different preferences.

### Latex
Latex is a natural material derived from rubber trees.
* **Pros:** Stretchy, easy to find, often available for free, biodegradable (if 100% latex).
* **Cons:** Some people are allergic; cannot be used with oil-based lubricants (which cause the material to break down).

### Polyurethane
A synthetic plastic material that often allows for a looser fit.
* **Pros:** Latex-free, thinner material, transfers heat well, can be used with oil-based lubes.
* **Cons:** Not biodegradable, less stretchy than latex, slightly more expensive.

### Polyisoprene
A synthetic rubber material that is softer than polyurethane and feels similar to latex.
* **Pros:** Latex-free, as stretchy as latex, cheaper than polyurethane.
* **Cons:** Cannot be used with oil-based lubricants, slightly more expensive than latex.

### Lambskin
Made from lamb intestines.
* **Warning:** Lambskin condoms **do not** protect against STIs. 
* **Pros:** Biodegradable, can be used with oil-based lube, latex-free.
* **Cons:** Expensive, hard to find, no STI protection.

## Varieties and Features

Beyond size and material, condoms come with various features to enhance experience:

* **Thickness:** Ranging from "extra thin" (for maximum sensation) to "extra thick" (often preferred for maximum reassurance).
* **Texture:** Ribbed or dotted condoms designed to enhance pleasure.
* **Flavoured:** Often used for oral sex (e.g., strawberry or chocolate).
* **Specialty Sensations:** Condoms with warming/cooling sensations or those designed to delay ejaculation.
* **Lubricated:** Pre-applied lube to ensure compatibility and ease of use.
* **Flared:** More room at the tip for comfort and ease of application.
* **Internal Condoms:** Inserted into the vagina or anus; a great alternative if external condoms do not fit well.

## Safety and Environmental Tips

* **Avoid Spermicide:** Avoid condoms containing spermicide. Evidence suggests they do not provide better protection against STIs and may actually increase your risk of infection by irritating the skin.
* **Eco-Friendly Disposal:** Never flush condoms down the toilet. Throw them in the bin.
* **Biodegradability:** 100% latex condoms are biodegradable and vegan-friendly. Synthetic materials like polyurethane and polyisoprene do not decompose.
* **Single Use:** Condoms are single-use only. Never reuse a condom.

## Finding what works

If you are uncomfortable using condoms, try practicing on your own while masturbating to get used to the sensation and the process of putting them on. Communicate with your partner about any concerns so you can find a solution together that ensures safe, protected sex.
