# My parents or carers are getting a divorce or separating

It can be difficult when your parents or carers separate or get a divorce, and it can bring many significant changes to your life. Sometimes people who are married or in long-term relationships decide to end their marriage (divorce) or separate.

There are many reasons for divorce and every situation is different. Some couples may grow apart, or there might be issues that cannot be resolved, such as cheating or abuse. The process of separation or divorce is different for everyone; sometimes it can take a long time and be very complicated, and other times the process is more straightforward and happens quickly.

The important thing to remember is that **it is never your fault** if your parents or carers decide to get divorced or separate.

## Your feelings

There are many things you might be feeling if you have just found out that your parents or carers are separating. You might feel:

* Sad
* Shocked
* Angry
* Relieved (especially if your parents were arguing a lot or had an unhealthy relationship)

You might feel a combination of all of these emotions at once. This can be confusing, but it is completely normal.

## Ways of coping

There are many different ways of coping that can help you work through your emotions and settle into a new routine.

### Talk about it
It is normal to feel hurt, betrayed, anxious, and lonely. Talking to a friend or another trusted adult is often a good way to deal with these emotions. If you have siblings, you may have different reactions to the news, but talking to each other can be a valuable way to provide mutual support. 

You can also contact organisations like **Childline**, who are there to listen and help you through any worries.

### Ask questions
Asking your parents or carers any questions you have can help reduce uncertainty and worries.

### Write down your thoughts
You could keep a mood journal or write down your worries. This allows you to reflect on how you are feeling and gives you the space to explore your emotions.

### Be kind to yourself
However you are feeling, you should never feel guilty about the divorce. The situation does not affect the way your parents or carers feel about you.

## Dealing with change

It can be hard to cope with the changes that might occur as a result of your parents or carers separating. These changes can include:

* Moving house
* Moving to an area away from your friends
* Moving schools
* Separating from a sibling or another relative
* Leaving a pet
* Leaving your belongings behind

## Living arrangements

Often, your parents or carers will decide who you live with based on what is best for you. This might mean you only see one parent at weekends or specific times. 

Sometimes it is too difficult for parents or carers to make that decision alone, and they will get help from a lawyer, social worker, or mediator. While this might seem scary, these professionals are there to ensure that the right decision is reached and that you remain safe.
