# Being a Teen Mum: Amber and Chloé’s Story

Amber and Chloé, stars from *Teen Mom UK*, share their personal experiences of discovering they were pregnant, telling their families and partners, and navigating the early stages of motherhood as teenagers.

## Discovering the Pregnancy

Both Amber and Chloé discovered their pregnancies when they were 16 or 17 years old. For both, the initial reaction was one of fear and shock.

* **Chloé:** Found out at 17. It was an unplanned pregnancy that felt like a "shock to the system," especially as she was transitioning from school to a life she expected to be free of major responsibilities.
* **Amber:** Found out the day before her 17th birthday. She felt overwhelmed by the massive commitment and the fact that there was no turning back once the decision was made. At the time, she was juggling work, college, and a difficult patch in her relationship.

## Telling Partners and Friends

The process of sharing the news was different for each of them, involving a mix of direct conversation and nervous hesitation.

* **Chloé:** After 10 positive pregnancy tests, she first called a friend who had experienced a similar scare. She eventually texted her partner, Jordan, as the news was too much to process over the phone immediately.
* **Amber:** Told her best friend first by leaving the positive test near the kettle. She waited to tell her partner, Ste, until her birthday. After an emotional moment at a theme park, she shared the news; his reaction was a mix of joy and fear regarding the future of the pregnancy.

## Breaking the News to Parents

Telling parents is often the most daunting part for young people. Both Amber and Chloé had varying experiences with their families.

* **Chloé’s Experience:** Her mother was with her when she found out and was immediately supportive. Her mother also handled telling her father, who initially reacted with frustration but quickly became supportive, emphasizing that it was Chloé's decision to make.
* **Amber’s Experience:** She chose to tell both parents at once during a lighthearted moment. While her mother initially needed space and didn't speak to her for the first night, she eventually approached Amber to offer full support, acknowledging Amber’s maturity and right to make her own decisions.

## Advice for Sharing the News

Based on their experiences, Amber and Chloé offer the following tips for other young people:

* **Choose the right person:** Break the news to the parent or guardian you are closest to first.
* **Stay calm:** Try to find a time when everyone is in a good mood and you are in a private, safe place.
* **Process it yourself first:** Take the time you need to understand your own feelings before you involve others.
* **Don't blurt it out:** Avoid sharing the news in public or stressful environments.

## Support Systems and Early Parenting

Support services are available for young parents to help them navigate the transition into parenthood.

* **Family Nurse Partnership:** Chloé utilized a family nurse, a service often offered to parents under 21, providing support during pregnancy and the first two years of the child's life.
* **Learning on the job:** Both mums emphasize that it is okay to make mistakes. Chloé recalls learning to change nappies through trial and error rather than relying solely on midwives.
* **Education:** Amber highlights that it is possible to continue education; she finished college with good grades while raising her son, Brooklyn.

## Dealing with Stigma and Judgment

Young mothers often face a fear of being judged by society or older parents.

* **Internalized Stigma:** Chloé notes that stereotypes about young mums can make them feel paranoid that others think they are incapable or lazy.
* **Confidence:** Amber shares that while she used to feel self-conscious when crossing paths with older mothers, she has grown more confident over time.
* **Shared Reality:** Young mums are trying to be the best parents they can be, just like parents of any other age.

## Tips for New Young Parents

* **Practice patience:** Both the parent and the child are learning at the same time.
* **Take it day by day:** Every day is a fresh start.
* **Cherish the moments:** Even on low sleep, try to enjoy the time with your child.
* **Don’t put yourself down:** Acknowledge that you are doing a good job under challenging circumstances.
