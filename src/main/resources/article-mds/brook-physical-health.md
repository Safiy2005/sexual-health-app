# Physical health

Looking after your physical health is essential for your general wellness. This involves doing regular exercise, eating a healthy diet, and drinking enough water. Maintaining your physical health can help prevent long-term or chronic illnesses, such as diabetes or cancer, and can significantly improve your mental wellbeing in both the short and long term.

## Benefits of regular exercise

Doing regular exercise is great for your body and mind. It offers numerous health benefits, including:

* Improving breathing and strength
* Boosting the immune system
* Preventing disease
* Increasing general energy levels
* Improving mental wellbeing by helping you relax and de-stress
* Releasing endorphins (natural chemicals that make you feel good)
* Building confidence and a sense of capability
* Providing social opportunities through team sports or group activities

## How much should I be exercising?

Exercise requirements vary depending on your age:

### Ages 5–18
Anyone in this age group should aim for **60 minutes of moderate intensity activity** daily across the week. This should include:
* **Aerobic exercise (cardio):** Anything that raises your heart rate.
* **Strengthening exercises:** Activities to build muscles and bones.

### Ages 18 and over
Adults should aim to be physically active every day. The NHS recommends:
* Strengthening activities that work all major muscles at least **2 days a week**.
* At least **150 minutes of moderate intensity activity** a week, OR **75 minutes of vigorous intensity activity** a week.
* Reducing time spent sitting or lying down and breaking up long periods of inactivity.

## Understanding intensity

### Moderate intensity activity
This is activity that raises your heart rate and makes you breathe faster and feel warmer. A simple test is that you can still talk but cannot sing. Examples include:
* Walking
* Cycling on level ground
* Dancing

### Vigorous intensity activity
This makes you breathe hard and fast. At this level, you will not be able to say more than a few words without pausing for breath. In general, 75 minutes of vigorous activity provides similar benefits to 150 minutes of moderate activity. Examples include:
* Jogging or running
* Swimming fast
* Riding a bike fast or on hills
* Team sports like football and hockey

## Strengthening exercises
Muscle-strengthening activities help maintain physical health and can be done at home, in a gym, or in a class. Examples include:
* Carrying heavy shopping bags
* Yoga
* Lifting weights
* Bodyweight exercises like push-ups

## Finding what exercise is right for you

The best way to stay active is to find something you enjoy. If you like the activity, you are more likely to stick with it.
* **Social:** Try climbing or joining a sports team with a friend.
* **Outdoors:** Try running, kayaking, or swimming.
* **Creative:** Find a dance class near you.

## Fitting exercise into your daily life

Exercise doesn’t have to be a separate chore. You can "sneak" activity into your day:
* Walk or cycle to school or work.
* Get off the bus a stop early or park further away to get more steps.
* Opt for collection rather than delivery when ordering food.
* Walk around while talking on the phone.
* Do stretches during TV commercial breaks or credits.
* Do bicep curls with tin cans while waiting for the kettle to boil.
* Dance while tidying your room.

### The ‘one minute movement’ challenge
Try taking a one-minute movement break every hour. Do a different activity each time, such as star jumps, push-ups, or lunges. This is particularly helpful if you spend a lot of time working at a screen. You can gradually increase the time by 10 seconds each week until you reach 3 minutes.

## Eating a healthy, balanced diet

A balanced diet provides the energy you need and helps you feel your best. According to the Eatwell Guide, you should try to:
* Eat at least five portions of various fruit and vegetables every day.
* Base meals on higher-fibre starchy foods (potatoes, bread, rice, or pasta).
* Include dairy or dairy alternatives (like soya drinks).
* Eat protein sources such as beans, pulses, fish, eggs, and meat.
* Choose unsaturated oils and spreads in small amounts.
* Drink plenty of fluids (at least six to eight glasses a day).

### Moderation
A balanced diet is about moderation. Unless advised otherwise by a doctor, it is okay to eat foods high in fat, salt, and sugar occasionally. These foods aren’t "bad," but they should not make up the majority of your diet as they do not provide the same benefits as other food groups.
