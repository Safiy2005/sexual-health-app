# Penises and testicles – do I need to see a doctor?

If you notice any changes or anything unusual about your penis, testicles, or anus, you should see your GP or visit a sexual health clinic.

## Symptoms to look out for
It is important to seek medical advice if you experience:
* Unusual discharge
* Tenderness
* Any lumps or bumps that you are worried about

These can be signs of infections that require treatment, such as:
* Thrush
* Cystitis
* Urethritis
* Proctitis

## Understanding your body
Knowing what is normal for you is the best way to identify when something is wrong. 
* **Foreskin health:** It is helpful to understand how a healthy foreskin should function and how to recognize signs that something isn’t right.
* **Checking for lumps:** You should regularly check your genitals to stay familiar with how they look and feel, making it easier to spot new lumps or bumps.

## What to expect at your appointment
If you need to see a doctor about your genitals, it is natural to feel a bit nervous, but remember:
* Doctors are professionals who have seen many penises and testicles; they will not judge you.
* The appointment will take place in a calm, professional environment.
* The doctor will likely ask to look at the area you are concerned about and may need to touch it to perform an examination.
* The examination will not take any longer than is strictly necessary.

Seeking help early is the best way to get peace of mind or receive necessary treatment.
