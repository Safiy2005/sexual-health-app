# Sex and Consent: What is it and why is it important?

Consent means agreeing to do something. When it comes to sex, this means agreeing to have sex or engage in sexual activity. It is important that everyone involved in sexual activity is consenting at all times—no one should ever feel they have to do something they are not comfortable with or don’t want to do.

## What is sexual activity?
Sex or sexual activity can include:
* Kissing
* Sexual touching
* Oral, anal, and vaginal sex with a penis or any other type of object

Just because you have consented to one thing doesn’t mean you have consented to something else. It is completely okay to say no or stop at any point if you don’t want to continue.

## Consent and the law
The Sexual Offences Act 2003 (England and Wales) defines consent as when a person "agrees by choice and has the capacity to make that choice." 

In the eyes of the law, all participants must have the freedom and full capacity to make that decision. Engaging in a sexual act without the person’s consent is sexual violence and is a criminal offence.

### Freedom and capacity
"Freedom" and "capacity" mean being able to agree to sexual activity with a full understanding of what is happening, without any pressure to say "yes." This is sometimes called "enthusiastic consent"—someone saying "yes" only when they really mean it because it is something they genuinely want to do.

## Situations where consent cannot be given
There are specific situations where a person does not have the freedom or capacity to consent:

* **If the person is too drunk or high:** Being under the influence of drugs or alcohol may mean someone lacks the mental capacity to understand what they are agreeing to.
* **If the person is asleep or unconscious:** Someone who is unaware of what is happening cannot consent.
* **If a person is pressured or coerced:** If someone is being manipulated, threatened, or pressured into sexual activity, they do not have the freedom to consent. Threats can include physical violence or emotional threats, such as breaking up or sharing private images.
* **If a person isn't able to withdraw consent:** If someone cannot communicate clearly or is unable to say "no," they cannot say "yes."
* **If someone is under the age of consent:** In the UK, the legal age of consent is 16. Legally, a person under 16 is seen as not having the capacity to consent to sexual activity.

## What is sexual violence?
Sexual violence is a general term for any unwanted sexual act. This includes:
* **Rape:** Penetration of the vagina, anus, or mouth with a penis without consent.
* **Sexual assault/abuse:** Any unwanted sexual contact, including online grooming and sexual exploitation.
* **Sexual Harassment:** Unwanted behavior of a sexual nature, such as comments or jokes that cause distress or humiliation.
* **Female Genital Mutilation (FGM).**

## Navigating consent
Everyone needs to understand consent to keep themselves and others safe. It is important to remember:
* You always need consent from all people involved, every time and throughout every encounter.
* You have the right to withhold or withdraw consent at any time.
* Consent requires verbal and physical communication before, during, and after sexual activity.
* It doesn’t matter what your relationship is or how far you have gone before; you always have the right to change your mind.

## Getting help and support
If someone forces you to do something sexual that you do not want to do, it is **never your fault**. You should speak to someone you trust to get help.

### Trusted adults
This could be a family member, teacher, social worker, or a friend’s parent—someone who has your best interests in mind.

### Sexual health services
Brook services offer non-judgmental advice and support regarding sex and relationships. If there isn't a Brook service nearby, any sexual health service can provide help.

### Other sources of support
* **Childline:** Call 0800 1111 (for those under 19).
* **Victim Support or Rape Crisis:** For those affected by sexual abuse or violence.
* **NHS 111:** For medical advice.
* **Emergency Services:** Call 999 if you are in immediate danger.
