# How to build intimacy in a relationship

Intimacy is more than simply being around your partner; it is about feeling understood and connected on a deeper level. Over time, work, stress, and daily routines can make it easy to lose that feeling of closeness. Whether you are in a new relationship or a long-term one, there are ways to build and maintain this connection.

## Quality time over quantity

It is common to mistake simply being in the same room as intimacy, but quality time matters more than the quantity of time spent together. If you are often around each other while scrolling on phones or doing separate activities, you are not building the relationship in the same way as when you share a focused experience.

To build a deeper connection:
* **Be intentional:** Set aside time to be fully present without distractions.
* **Communicate your needs:** Be open about your desire to connect differently. To avoid placing blame, frame your request around what you would like rather than what your partner is doing wrong.

**Examples of how to start the conversation:**
* “I love you and want to feel close, so I want to make sure we’re spending more quality time together.”
* “I’d love to have some time this week to share more of what’s on my mind about work and friends. When would work for you?”
* “I feel I haven’t seen you much recently; do you fancy doing something on Friday?”

## Learning and evolving together

Even in long-term relationships, there is always more to learn about one another as individuals change over time. You may develop new hobbies, your goals might shift, or your sexual preferences may evolve. Asking thoughtful questions allows you to understand yourself and your partner better.

**Try asking:**
* “What excites you about our sex life or physical intimacy?”
* “What am I doing right in our relationship that I should keep doing or do more of?”
* “What makes you feel loved?”
* “What turns you on or gets you in the mood?”
* “Is there anything we could do to set the mood even more, such as sending sexy texts during the day or ensuring chores are done?”

## Openness and vulnerability

The more you and your partner can share your feelings and practice active listening, the closer you will feel. Active listening involves repeating back what you heard to ensure understanding before adding your own perspective.

When your partner shares an issue, clarify what they need from you:
* Do they want your opinion or advice?
* Do they simply want to be listened to and comforted?

Openness and vulnerability create trust and a deeper bond. These conversations should always have the goal of strengthening the team and learning how to make each other feel good.

## Engaging in shared activities

Shared activities can spark new conversations and experiences. Consider the following:
* **Take a quiz:** Explore tools like the "5 Love Languages" or "Erotic Blueprints" and discuss your results. Note that you do not need the same answers to be compatible.
* **Consume media together:** Read a book or watch a film or video together and discuss it.
* **Try something new:** Engage in a new activity you are both interested in, such as an art class, a sport, or a unique experience like axe throwing.

## Building sexual intimacy

You can spend time building sexual intimacy by removing the pressure of performance. Try setting aside time to be naked together and appreciate each other’s bodies with no expectation that it will lead to intercourse. Activities such as body painting, life drawing of one another, or massage can be excellent ways to connect.

By taking the pressure off physical intimacy always leading to sex, you create space to connect in ways that are fulfilling on their own. Intimacy becomes the goal, rather than just a step towards sex.

## The importance of space

While quality time is vital, having space to yourself is equally important for a healthy relationship. Maintaining your own identity, hobbies, and friendships allows you to be yourself away from your partner. 

A healthy bit of separation can actually improve your sex life. If you are always together, it can reduce the sense of mystery and the build-up of sexual tension. Maintaining independence allows you to bring new energy back into the relationship, creating more opportunities for flirting and romance.
