# Homesickness at University: Jasmin’s Story

Jasmin shares her personal journey of moving away for university, discussing the challenges of homesickness and the eventual benefits of gaining independence.

## Facing the Fear of Moving Away

Moving to university is a significant life transition that can be daunting. Combining the academic pressure of university with the experience of moving away from home can feel overwhelming. For many, the prospect of managing daily tasks like cooking, cleaning, and laundry is manageable, but the loss of a primary support system—such as being able to ask a parent for immediate help—can create a high level of anxiety.

The initial hours in new accommodation, surrounded by strangers, often lead to feelings of being out of one's depth. However, it is important to remember that:
* Most students feel exactly the same way during the first few days.
* Homesickness is a completely normal reaction to a major life change.
* Feeling "strange" or worried is a common experience for everyone starting university.

## Normalising the Experience

While it may feel like you are the only one struggling, homesickness and anxiety are widespread among new students. Over time, most people learn to adapt. As the term progresses, the relationship with "home" often changes. Returning to a parents' house becomes a treat and a welcome break from assignments, rather than a constant necessity.

## Embracing Independence and Adulthood

Moving away from home serves as a "safety net" for adulthood, allowing students to test their independence without total permanent commitment. There are several benefits to this transition:
* **Building Confidence:** Managing your own schedule and environment boosts self-esteem.
* **Personal Freedom:** Students enjoy the autonomy to make their own choices, such as staying out late or setting their own routines, without parental oversight.
* **Life Skills:** Living away provides a practical understanding of what is required to maintain a household, such as cleaning, washing up, and understanding the commitments of renting.
* **Readiness for the Future:** It helps individuals realise when they are truly ready to leave their childhood bedroom behind and begin their own journey.

## Managing Your Emotions

It is vital to acknowledge that missing loved ones—including family, school friends, and pets—is a natural part of being human. Whether you have moved three hours away or only thirty minutes, your feelings are valid. 

To manage these emotions, consider the following:
* **Accept your feelings:** It is okay to cry and feel sad about the transition.
* **Stay connected:** Do not hesitate to call, FaceTime, or visit home if you need to.
* **Be patient:** Give yourself time to adjust to your new environment.

## Different Reactions to Moving Out

It is also important to recognise that everyone has a different relationship with their home and family. Not everyone will experience homesickness; some students may feel immediate excitement and joy about their new-found independence. Both reactions are perfectly acceptable. Whether you are missing home or fully embracing your new life, your individual experience is valid.
