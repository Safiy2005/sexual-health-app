# Condom Excuses (and Comebacks!)

Some people don’t use condoms because they think they don’t need to or because they don’t want to. However, condoms are the only method of contraception that protects you against both pregnancy and sexually transmitted infections (STIs), making them a vital habit for sexual health.

Many condoms are designed to increase pleasure and sensation, meaning they can actually make sex more enjoyable.

## "They look healthy" or "I know who they’ve slept with"

You cannot tell if someone has an STI by the way they look, behave, or by knowing their sexual history. 
* Many STIs have no symptoms.
* You only need to have sex once to contract an STI.
* The only way to know for sure is to get tested.

## "Condoms don’t feel good"

Safer sex can still be good sex. If sensation is an issue, the type of condom you choose matters:
* **Size:** Condoms that are the wrong size can be uncomfortable or slip off. Experiment with different sizes for the best fit.
* **Thinner condoms:** These are designed to increase sensation and pleasure.
* **Non-latex condoms:** Ideal for those with allergies, these are often thinner and allow for better heat transfer, feeling more natural.
* **Flared condoms:** These offer more room at the tip, making them easier to put on and more comfortable for some.
* **Stimulating condoms:** Ribbed or textured condoms, or those with warming/tingling lubricant, are designed to enhance pleasure.
* **Flavoured condoms:** These improve the taste of oral sex. *Note: Do not use flavoured condoms for vaginal sex, as the flavouring can irritate and upset the vagina's PH balance.*
* **Extra lubricant:** Adding a few drops of lube inside the tip or on the outside of the condom can increase pleasure. Avoid using too much inside, as it may cause the condom to slip off.

## "STIs can be cured anyway"

While many STIs are treatable, not all can be cured. Furthermore, many STIs are asymptomatic. If left untreated for a long time, they can lead to serious health issues, including infertility. Testing is the only way to ensure you are clear.

## "We can just pull out"

The "withdrawal method" (pulling out before ejaculation) is not a reliable form of contraception.
* **Pre-ejaculate (pre-cum):** This fluid can contain sperm from previous ejaculations and can cause pregnancy.
* **Timing:** It is easy to get the timing wrong or lose control in the moment.
* **STIs:** Pulling out provides zero protection against STIs.

## "I trust you" or "I don't have anything"

Trust does not prevent infection. Many people carry STIs like chlamydia and gonorrhoea without knowing it because they have no symptoms. Unless you are in a monogamous relationship and both partners have recently received clear STI test results, condoms are necessary.

## "We're using another method of contraception"

Other methods, such as the pill, protect against pregnancy but do not protect against STIs. Additionally, no method of contraception is 100% effective, so using condoms alongside another method provides extra security.

## "I lose my erection"

Losing an erection while using a condom is very common and nothing to be ashamed of.
* **Check the size:** A condom that is too small can be constricting.
* **Practice:** Practice putting on a condom during masturbation to get used to the sensation.
* **Try internal condoms:** These can be inserted into the vagina ahead of time, are latex-free, and protect against both pregnancy and STIs.
* **Explore other touch:** Great sex can happen without an erection. If this is a persistent concern, you can speak with a professional at a sexual health clinic.

## "It ruins the moment"

It can be difficult to stop when things are heating up, but preparation helps:
* **Talk beforehand:** Discussing condoms before sex makes you more likely to use them.
* **Make it sexy:** Incorporate putting the condom on into foreplay.
* **Keep them nearby:** Have condoms easily accessible in a bedside drawer or your bag.
* **Storage note:** Do not store condoms in a wallet or pocket for more than a month, as friction and heat can damage them.

## "I’ve been drinking or taking drugs"

Alcohol and drugs can impair your judgment and your ability to consent.
* **Consent:** Any sexual activity without clear consent is illegal. If someone is too intoxicated to consent, do not engage in sex with them.
* **Performance:** Alcohol often reduces sensation, causes vaginal dryness, and makes it harder to maintain an erection or reach orgasm.

## "Condoms are too expensive"

Condoms are free on the NHS. You can get them from:
* Sexual health clinics (including Brook clinics).
* The C-Card scheme (available in many local areas).

## "They're too small"

Condoms are designed to stretch significantly. While a tight fit can be uncomfortable, there is a wide variety of sizes available. There is a condom for every penis; it is just a matter of finding the right variety.

## "We can just use emergency contraception"

Emergency contraception (the "morning-after pill") should only be used in emergencies, such as when a condom breaks.
* It does not prevent pregnancy if ovulation has already occurred.
* It has a higher hormone dosage than regular contraception, which can impact mood and your period.
* It provides no protection against STIs.
