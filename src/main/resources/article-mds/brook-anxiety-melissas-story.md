# Anxiety: Melissa’s Story

Melissa, a Brook volunteer from Manchester, shares her personal journey of living with and managing anxiety.

> **Content Warning:** This article contains discussions of mental illness, including depression, anxiety, and suicidal thoughts.

## Understanding the Journey

At 17, after years of learning to cope with anxiety, the exact cause remains unclear. However, the focus has shifted from finding a "why" to developing effective coping mechanisms. Living with anxiety involves identifying new challenges daily while simultaneously discovering techniques to manage them, ensuring the condition does not prevent a fulfilling life.

## The Impact of Puberty and School

For Melissa, anxiety began at approximately 14 years old, coinciding with the physical and emotional changes of puberty. The experience was overwhelming, described as a "tape over the mouth" that restricted the ability to communicate and act.

The school environment proved particularly challenging:
* **Fear of Panic Attacks:** A pervasive fear of having a panic attack made attending regular classes impossible.
* **Lack of Institutional Support:** The school lacked the resources or expertise to properly support students with mental health challenges.
* **Isolation:** Melissa was often placed in a separate room with limited supervision, which contributed to a sense of disconnection from the educational process.
* **Emotional Distress:** The pressure of school led to deep unhappiness and depression, requiring significant support from family to manage daily attendance.

Despite these obstacles, Melissa completed several GCSEs, noting that her greatest achievement from that period was not the grades themselves, but the realization that she could learn to cope with her anxiety.

## Navigating Education and Mental Health

Following school, Melissa transitioned to college to pursue A Levels. However, the move highlighted the importance of checking in with one's own wellbeing. After five weeks, she realized that remaining in a traditional educational environment was detrimental to her happiness.

She chose to leave college to focus on a personal project: writing a book about coping with mental health. This decision marked a shift toward prioritizing passion and personal recovery over conventional expectations.

## Managing Crisis and Recovery

In late 2015, while struggling to find employment and staying at home, Melissa experienced a severe depressive episode and suicidal ideation. 

Key factors in her recovery included:
* **Seeking Presence:** Recognizing that even simply sitting with a family member can help interrupt dangerous thoughts.
* **Open Communication:** Working through depression by talking openly with family over several months.
* **Perspective:** Realizing that feelings are transient and that situations can change rapidly.

By February 2016, life had improved significantly. Melissa secured a job, met new people, and gained tangible proof that recovery is possible.

## Success and Reflection

In June 2016, Melissa published her first book, *COPE*. Her experience taught her several vital lessons about mental health:
* **The Unpredictability of Emotions:** Feelings can shift from despair to hope in as little as ten minutes.
* **Persistence:** It is essential to keep going, as "feelings pass" and "it does get better."
* **Sharing Lived Experience:** By sharing her story, she aims to help others feel less alone in their struggles.

## Seeking Support

Approximately 1 in 4 people in the UK experience a mental health problem each year. If you are worried about your mental health or the wellbeing of someone else, support is available. You do not need a formal diagnosis to reach out for help. 

Melissa is a writer and volunteer at Brook. Her book, *COPE*, and her blog offer further insights into her experiences with mental health.
