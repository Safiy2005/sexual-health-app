# The Contraceptive Injection

The contraceptive injection contains the hormone progestogen, which is similar to the progesterone naturally produced in the ovaries. It is a form of long-acting reversible contraception (LARC).

## Key Information

* **Effectiveness:** More than 99% effective.
* **Duration:** Lasts for 8, 12, or 13 weeks depending on the type.
* **Hormones:** Contains progestogen only (no oestrogen).
* **Visibility:** No one else can see it.
* **STIs:** Does not protect against sexually transmitted infections; use condoms for STI protection.
* **Maintenance:** Requires remembering to attend repeat appointments or self-injecting on schedule.
* **Starting:** Requires an initial appointment with a specialist nurse or doctor.

## How the Injection Works

The injection prevents pregnancy in three ways:
* It stops ovulation (the release of an egg).
* It thickens the mucus around the cervix, making it difficult for sperm to enter.
* It thins the lining of the womb so a fertilised egg cannot implant.

## Types of Injection

| Name | Frequency | Injection Site |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Depo-Provera** | Every 12 weeks | Buttocks or arm |
| **Noristerat** | Every 8 weeks | Buttocks |
| **Sayana Press** | Every 13 weeks | Front of the thigh or abdomen |

While most injections must be administered by a healthcare professional, **Sayana Press** is a self-injection option. A doctor or nurse can teach you how to use it at home, and you will typically be given a one-year supply.

## Pros and Cons

### Pros
* Over 99% effective.
* Does not interrupt sex.
* Lasts for several weeks at a time.
* May reduce heavy periods and period pain.
* Suitable for those who cannot use contraception containing oestrogen.
* Effectiveness is not affected by vomiting, diarrhoea, or antibiotics.

### Cons
* No protection against STIs.
* Can cause side effects such as headaches, mood swings, breast tenderness, and acne.
* Once injected, it cannot be removed; side effects may last as long as the injection and for some time afterward.
* Can cause slight thinning of the bones (bone mineral density), which usually recovers after stopping.
* Some people may experience weight gain (specifically with Depo-Provera).
* Fertility can take up to a year to return after stopping.

## Impact on Periods

Your periods may change significantly:
* They may become irregular, lighter, or stop altogether (amenorrhea).
* Some people experience spotting, particularly when first starting.
* It may take some time for periods to return to their natural cycle after you stop the injections.

## Starting the Injection

You can start the injection at any time during your menstrual cycle if you are certain you are not pregnant.

* **First 5 days of period:** You are protected against pregnancy immediately.
* **Any other time:** You will not be protected for the first seven days and must use an additional method, like condoms.

## At the Appointment

When you attend a clinic for the injection:
* A professional will ask about your medical and family history.
* They will check your blood pressure and weight.
* You do **not** need a vaginal or breast examination.
* You will be advised when to return for your next dose (8, 12, or 13 weeks).

## Use After Pregnancy, Abortion, or Miscarriage

### After Giving Birth
* If not breastfeeding: Can be used any time. If started within 21 days, protection is immediate.
* If breastfeeding: Usually started after 6 weeks.
* Starting in the first few weeks after birth may increase the likelihood of heavy or irregular bleeding.

### After Abortion or Miscarriage
* Can be administered immediately.
* If given within 5 days of an abortion or miscarriage, protection is immediate. If later, use backup contraception for 7 days.

## Late Injections and Fertility

It is vital to have follow-up injections on time. If you miss your window, use condoms and seek medical advice, as you may no longer be protected.

If you are planning to become pregnant in the near future (within the next year), the injection may not be the best choice because it can take up to 12 months for your fertility to return to normal after the last shot.
