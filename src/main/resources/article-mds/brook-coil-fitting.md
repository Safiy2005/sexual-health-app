# Coil Fitting: What You Need to Know

Please read this information carefully when you book your coil fitting. It is important that you read this information when you book your appointment and then again before you attend your appointment to have a coil fitted.

If you are worried or have questions, you can call the clinic or speak to the nurse or doctor before the fitting. It is okay to change your mind at any time. If you need to change or cancel your appointment, please contact the clinic as soon as possible.

## Contraception, Pregnancy, and STIs

It is essential that there is no chance of pregnancy when you have your coil fitted or re-fitted.

* **Pre-appointment contraception:** Use condoms every time you have sex for three weeks before your coil fitting.
* **Unprotected sex:** If you have had sex without a condom or contraception in the three weeks before your appointment, the nurse or doctor will not be able to fit your coil.
* **Replacements:** If you are replacing an existing coil, use condoms for one week before the appointment (in case the new coil cannot be inserted).
* **Method validity:** The "withdrawal method" and natural family planning do not count as contraception for the purposes of a coil fitting.
* **Existing methods:** If you are already using a method of contraception (pill, patch, injection, or implant), keep using it until your fitting. It is safe to have both methods at the same time. You may need to continue your current method for up to seven days after the fitting.
* **Pregnancy tests:** You may be asked to provide a urine sample for a pregnancy test at the clinic. If you do not provide one when asked, the fitting may not proceed.
* **STI screening:** You should not have a coil fitted if you are at risk of a sexually transmitted infection (STI). If you have had a new sexual partner since your last test, perform an STI test before your appointment.
* **Post-fitting effectiveness:** 
 * **Hormonal coil (IUS):** May not be effective immediately. You may need to avoid sex or use condoms for seven days.
 * **Non-hormonal coil (IUD/Copper coil):** Effective immediately.

### Pre-Fitting Confirmations
At the start of the appointment, you will be asked to confirm:
* I have followed the advice about contraception and have not had unprotected sex within the last three weeks.
* I understand the coil will not be fitted if there is a concern that I may be pregnant.
* I understand I should not have the coil fitted if I am at risk of an STI.
* (If having a hormonal coil) I understand it might not prevent pregnancy immediately and agree to use back-up contraception for seven days if advised.
* I understand the coil does not protect against STIs.

## Risks of Coil Fitting

There are risks involved in all medical procedures. Please ensure you understand the following:

* **Failure rate:** Both hormonal and non-hormonal coils have a small risk of failure (less than 1 in 100 chance of pregnancy).
* **Ectopic pregnancy:** In the unlikely event of pregnancy with a coil, there is an increased risk of it being ectopic (outside the womb).
* **Perforation:** There is a 1 in 1,000 risk of the coil making a hole in the womb at the time of insertion.
* **Expulsion:** There is a 1 in 20 chance of the coil falling out.
* **Infection:** There is a 1 in 100 risk of infection after the fitting.
* **Bleeding patterns:** Your periods may change. You may experience no periods, heavier bleeding, irregular bleeding, or prolonged bleeding.
* **Fainting:** On rare occasions, it is possible to faint (vasovagal syncope) during the procedure.

## On the Day of Your Appointment

* **Nutrition:** Ensure you have had breakfast or lunch and are well-hydrated. If you are fasting, rearrange the appointment for a time when you can eat.
* **Sobriety:** Do not drink alcohol or take drugs before your appointment.
* **Pain management:** You can take a painkiller (like paracetamol) about an hour before your appointment. While local anaesthetic can be requested, not all services offer it; check with your clinic beforehand.
* **Support:** You can request a second member of staff to be present for support. If you bring a friend, they may be asked to wait in the waiting room during your initial assessment.
* **Children:** It is advised not to bring children. If you must, you must bring another adult to look after them in the waiting room.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

### Does the coil fitting hurt?
The experience varies. It can feel invasive or uncomfortable, and some find it painful. Practitioners are experienced and will check on your comfort throughout. To help, use relaxation techniques and consider ibuprofen afterward for cramping.

### Do I need to take time off work or school?
Many people find it helpful to take the afternoon off to rest. You might experience cramping or bleeding similar to a heavy period.

### Do I need to be on my period?
No. A coil can be fitted at any point in your cycle as long as you are not pregnant. However, fitting during a lighter flow day can sometimes be more comfortable as the cervix is slightly more open.

### When can I have sex?
It is generally recommended to wait at least three days after the fitting to allow the cervix to heal and reduce infection risk. Remember that hormonal coils may require seven days to become effective if not fitted on the first day of your period.

### What should I do if I have pain after the fit?
Rest, use over-the-counter pain relief, and apply heat to the lower abdomen. Contact a doctor immediately if you experience severe cramping, fever, unusual discharge, or heavy bleeding.

## How to Check Your Coil

You should regularly check that your coil is in the right place:
1. Wash your hands thoroughly.
2. Squat or raise one leg on a stool.
3. Insert a finger into the vagina to find the cervix (it feels firm, like the tip of your nose).
4. Feel for the threads. It is normal to feel one or two threads.
5. **Do not pull the threads.** 

If you feel the hard tip of the coil itself against the cervix, it may be misplaced. Use condoms and seek a check-up immediately.

## How Long Does a Coil Last?

* **Copper Coil (Non-hormonal):** Lasts 5 or 10 years. If fitted at age 40 or older, it can stay in until age 55.
* **Hormonal Coil:** 
 * Mirena/Levosert/Benelexa: Currently licensed for 8 years (if used for contraception). If fitted at age 45 or older, it can stay in until age 55.
 * Other brands: May last 3 or 5 years.
* **HRT:** If using the coil for Hormone Replacement Therapy, consult your GP for specific expiration advice.
