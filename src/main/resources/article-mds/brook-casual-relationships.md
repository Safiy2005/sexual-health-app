# Situationships & Casual Relationships: Do they work?

Casual relationships can take many different forms, but generally, they are relationships with less commitment or are seen as ‘less serious’ than an exclusive romantic relationship.

## Types of casual relationships

There are several common types of casual arrangements:

* **Casual dating:** Meeting new people and going on dates without an expectation or desire for a committed relationship. This may or may not involve sex.
* **Casual sex:** Seeking purely physical intimacy. These arrangements are often called ‘friends with benefits’, ‘sex buddies’, or ‘sneaky links’. Some people choose this with someone they know well but have no romantic feelings for, while others only meet specifically for sex.

Relationships based on sex might involve:
* Last-minute plans to meet up.
* Little conversation beyond communicating about sex.
* Irregular communication.
* Not staying over after sex.
* Keeping the relationship a secret.

## Make sure you’ve got someone to talk to

Regardless of your situation, you should ensure at least one friend knows about your casual relationship so you are safe and supported. Having someone to talk to who isn't involved in the relationship can help you process your feelings.

## Situationships

A "situationship" is a grey area where a relationship goes beyond sex but isn't committed, official, or exclusive. People may choose them intentionally, fall into them out of convenience, or find themselves in one when they actually want more.

Situationships might look like:
* **Time limited:** Avoiding labels because you know the relationship will have to end soon due to external changes.
* **Less committed:** Meeting less often than a standard relationship to save time or energy.
* **More independent:** Not introducing them to friends or family, or not posting about them on social media.
* **Not exclusive:** Agreeing that both parties can see other people.

## How to make casual relationships work

For casual relationships to be healthy, certain elements are necessary:

### 1. Be honest
Be up front about what you want before the first date. Explain your reasons for wanting a casual relationship and what that looks like for you. If using dating apps, look for people seeking the same thing.

### 2. Make sure you know where you stand
Talk about whether you are seeing other people, especially before having sex. This helps define boundaries and allows you to protect yourselves from STIs and unwanted pregnancy. If having sex with others:
* Use condoms every time.
* Use condoms on sex toys and wash them between partners.
* Test for STIs every three months or every time you have a new partner.
* Maintain clear communication regarding consent and pleasure.

### 3. Respect each other and each other’s time
You always owe the other person respect. Do not string someone along or make false promises just to keep the arrangement going. Value their time by avoiding last-minute cancellations.

### 4. Set boundaries
If you are spending significant time together, you might develop an attachment. If there is no romantic future, set clear boundaries so both parties know where they stand.

### 5. Monitor your feelings
Sex involves emotions and hormones that can affect how you feel. Regularly check in with yourself to see if you are "catching feelings" and talk to a friend about your emotional state.

### 6. Know your worth
If your needs aren’t being met, or if the other person is a poor communicator who disrespects you, the arrangement isn't working. You do not have to put up with something that doesn't make you feel good just because it "isn't that deep."

## Why do people want a casual relationship?

Casual relationships can offer intimacy, friendship, and fun without the expectations of a long-term monogamous commitment. They can be beneficial if:
* You want affection but lack the time or headspace for a full relationship.
* You enjoy someone's company but neither of you wants a partner right now.
* You want to explore your sexuality and preferences.
* You want to get to know someone slowly before deciding on a serious relationship.

## Why they do not work for some people

Casual relationships aren't for everyone. If you want a committed, long-term relationship, a casual one might not be right for you. Signs that a casual relationship isn't working for you include:
* **Jealousy:** Feeling upset when they see other people.
* **Obsessing:** Spending too much time thinking about them or checking their social media.
* **Seeking attention:** Posting online or going to specific places just to get their notice.
* **Disappointment:** Feeling bad after spending time with them.
* **Low self-worth:** Questioning why they don't want a "real" relationship with you.

If your needs for exclusivity or emotional connection are not being met, it is best to end things. Continuing in an arrangement that doesn't serve you can hurt your mental health.

## Ending the relationship

Ending a casual relationship can be difficult because you are letting go of a "potential" future. To move on effectively:
* Delete their number and block them on social media if necessary.
* Write a list of reasons why they weren't right for you.
* Avoid waiting for them if they suggest they "might" want more in the future; this is often "breadcrumbing" (giving just enough attention to keep you interested without committing).
