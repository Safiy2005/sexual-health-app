# Fertility Awareness Methods

Fertility awareness methods, also known as natural family planning, involves working out when you are in the most fertile phase of your monthly cycle and avoiding unprotected sex during this time.

## Key Features
* **Hormones:** No hormones
* **Mood/Emotions:** No effect on mood
* **STIs:** No protection against STIs
* **Visibility:** Invisible but works best with clear communication with your sexual partner(s)
* **Periods:** Periods will stay the same
* **Acne/Skin:** Skin will stay the same
* **Effectiveness:** Depends on how well you follow the method, if you’re taught by a practitioner, and a number of other factors.
* **Starting:** It can take up to six menstrual cycles to know your fertile days. You will need to use another form of contraception in the meantime.
* **Maintenance:** Daily monitoring and recording of fertility signs.

Fertility awareness requires a high level of commitment and is only recommended for people with regular cycles who would be prepared to manage an unplanned pregnancy if it happened.

## How it Works
The method works by observing and recording your body’s natural signs each day of your monthly cycle. The main indicators are:
* Recording your body temperature
* Monitoring cervical secretions (cervical mucus)
* Calculating the length of your menstrual cycle

During your fertile time (which generally lasts around 8-9 days), you avoid having sex or use additional contraception such as condoms. This window accounts for the fact that eggs live for 24 hours and sperm can live inside the body for up to seven days.

## Pros and Cons

### Pros
* Suitable for people who cannot or do not want to use hormonal contraception.
* Can be used to plan pregnancy as well as avoid it.
* Up to 99% effective with motivation and training by experienced practitioners.

### Cons
* No protection against STIs.
* Requires avoiding sex or using condoms during fertile days (often when libido is highest).
* Failure rates can be as high as 24 in 100 for those who are untrained or use only one indicator.
* Relies on proper teaching; information alone is not enough for full effectiveness.
* Effectiveness is impacted by illness, stress, travel, or changes in routine.
* Requires a high level of commitment from both partners.

## Monitoring Techniques
It is recommended that you use all three of these methods together to maximize reliability.

### 1. Menstrual Cycle Length
Measure and record the length of your cycle (from the first day of one period to the first day of the next). While this gives a rough idea of ovulation, it is not reliable enough to be used on its own.

### 2. Temperature Method
There is a small rise in basal body temperature (temperature at rest) following ovulation.
* Take your temperature every morning at the same time before getting out of bed, eating, or drinking.
* Use a digital thermometer designed for family planning.
* Look for 3 days in a row where your temperature is higher (usually by about 0.2°C). This indicates the end of your fertile window.
* Note: Temperature can be affected by alcohol, lack of sleep, illness, and travel.

### 3. Cervical Mucus
Cervical mucus changes in colour, consistency, and quantity throughout your cycle.
* **After your period:** Vagina feels dry; little to no mucus.
* **Before ovulation:** Mucus is white, sticky, moist, and creamy. This is the start of the fertile period.
* **Immediately before ovulation:** Mucus is wet, clear, and slippery (like raw egg white). This is peak fertility.
* **After ovulation:** Mucus becomes thicker and stickier or disappears. After 3 days of this change, you are no longer fertile.

## Fertility Awareness Apps
While phone apps can help record data and reduce human calculation errors, their reliability is not always known. Apps cannot always account for sudden lifestyle changes like stress, illness, or travel which might shift your ovulation. It is best to understand your own cycle indicators rather than relying on technology alone.

## Suitability
This method is best suited to someone who:
* Is organized and motivated.
* Has a regular routine and doesn't do shift work.
* Has regular menstrual cycles.
* Wakes up at the same time every day.
* Does not drink much alcohol (to avoid hangovers affecting temperature).
* Is not nearing menopause or has not recently started their period.
* Would not be devastated by an unplanned pregnancy.

## Starting the Method
You can start at any time, but it takes 3–6 months to fully understand your cycle. Use alternative contraception during this learning phase.

If you have recently used hormonal contraception, wait at least three complete cycles until your periods are regular before relying on this method.

## After Pregnancy, Abortion, or Miscarriage
* **After giving birth:** You can get pregnant 3 weeks after birth. The method is unreliable until your cycle is regular again. 
* **LAM (Lactational Amenorrhoea Method):** Only effective if you are breastfeeding fully (day and night, no bottles/dummies), the baby is under six months old, and your period hasn't returned.
* **After abortion or miscarriage:** You can become pregnant 2 weeks later. Use other contraception until your cycle returns to a normal pattern.

## Important Considerations
* **Irregular periods:** If your cycles are irregular, it is best to use a different, more reliable method of contraception.
* **Fertile days:** Always use condoms or avoid vaginal sex during fertile windows.
* **Pulling out:** Withdrawal is not a method of contraception and is not effective at preventing pregnancy.
* **Training:** It is highly recommended to be taught by a specialist teacher. Visit to find a clinic.
