# Single by Choice: Hannah’s Story

Hannah, 20, shares her experience of choosing to remain single and explains the importance of maintaining independence and prioritizing friendships, even when in a relationship.

## Breaking the Cycle of Codependency

Many people experience a cycle of intense, short-term relationships that lead to personal burnout. Common signs of this pattern include:
* Messaging a partner every day to the exclusion of others.
* Spending all available time with a partner.
* Losing contact with friends and family during the relationship.
* Feeling like a "package deal" where one person cannot exist without the other.

Recognizing these patterns is the first step toward developing a healthier approach to dating. It is essential to work through "separation issues" and understand one's own emotional capacity before committing to someone else.

## Maintaining Identity Outside of a Relationship

It is vital to develop as an individual outside of a romantic partnership. Being known only as "so-and-so's partner" can lead to an unhealthy level of dependency. Healthy relationships require that both individuals remain "enough of a person" to have their own lives, interests, and conversations.

When interacting with friends, remember that they value you for who you are, not just who you are dating. They are often interested in:
* Your career or university progress.
* New hobbies or sports teams you have joined.
* Updates on your family and other mutual friends.
* Upcoming events, like concerts or trips, that you are planning for yourself.

## Practical Tips for Balancing Friendships and Relationships

Finding a balance between a partner and a social circle requires intentionality. Your friends should not have to compete with your partner for your time.

* **Set specific time aside:** Dedicate clear blocks of time for friends and separate time for date nights. 
* **Stick to your commitments:** If you are out with friends, avoid leaving early just because a partner is home. Similarly, if you are on a date, avoid taking non-urgent calls from friends.
* **Ask before bringing a partner:** If you want to bring a partner to a group hangout, check with your friends first. This shows respect for the group dynamic and ensures everyone feels comfortable.

## Choosing Singlehood for Personal Fulfillment

Choosing to be single is a valid and healthy life choice, especially when focusing on personal goals. Many people choose singlehood to focus on:
* **Education:** Pursuing degrees or certifications.
* **Career and Activism:** Dedicating time to work or causes they are passionate about.
* **Self-Discovery:** Learning how to be happy and fulfilled without relying on a partner.

Being in a relationship when you do not have the time or emotional energy can be unfair to a partner, who may end up feeling like a secondary priority.

## Conclusion

Being single does not make you "less of a person." Relationships may come and go, but the time spent building a fulfilled life—pursuing your interests and nurturing your friendships—cannot be replaced. Prioritizing your own growth ensures that you live a life without regrets.
