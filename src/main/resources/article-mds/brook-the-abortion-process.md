# The Abortion Process

Abortion, sometimes called a ‘termination’, is when someone chooses to end a pregnancy. Understanding the procedure from start to finish can help make the process feel less overwhelming.

## Your first contact and consultation

Your first contact with an abortion service is often by phone. Depending on how many weeks pregnant you are, you may have a telephone consultation or be asked to attend a clinic appointment.

If you attend a clinic, you may be offered:
* A finger prick blood test.
* STI tests.
* An ultrasound scan to determine exactly how far along the pregnancy is and the best procedure to use.

If you require a vaginal examination or ultrasound and have not had one before, inform the nurse or doctor so they can explain what to expect. During this time, you may also discuss different types of contraception.

Finally, you will be asked to provide formal consent. You can change your mind about having an abortion at any point, even after you have signed the consent form.

## How and where an abortion takes place

The type of abortion and the location will depend on how many weeks pregnant you are.

### Medical abortion at home
An early medical abortion takes place at home or another comfortable location. It involves two stages of medication:
1. **Mifepristone:** After taking this first pill, you can usually continue your normal activities.
2. **Misoprostol:** Taken 24-48 hours later, this medication causes the abortion to begin.

### Abortion at a clinic
If you are over 10 weeks pregnant or having a surgical procedure, the abortion will typically take place at a clinic.
* **What to bring:** Bring all relevant documents (GP letters, etc.), any personal medication (inhalers, EpiPens), sanitary towels, and a clean pair of underwear.
* **Support:** You can usually bring one person for support in the waiting room. If you are having a general anaesthetic, an adult must accompany you home.
* **Preparation:** For some surgical abortions, you may be required to fast (not eat) beforehand. Your provider will give you specific instructions.
* **Clinic safety:** Some people protest outside clinics. If you are worried, you can call the clinic to ask for an escort inside or inquire about alternative entrances.

## Physical recovery

Immediately after an abortion, you may feel "woozy" or "wobbly." It is important to rest and relax.

* **Pain management:** You can take painkillers like paracetamol or aspirin. Ask the clinic for specific recommendations.
* **Bleeding:** Bleeding is normal. Use sanitary towels rather than tampons until your next period.
* **Activity:** It is advised to avoid penetrative sex until you feel physically recovered.
* **Contraception:** You can get pregnant immediately after an abortion. Discuss contraception options with a professional if you do not wish to become pregnant again.

### When to seek medical advice
Contact your clinic, a doctor, or call NHS 111 if:
* Bleeding has not stopped after two weeks.
* The pain becomes severe.
* You are bleeding very heavily.
* You have a high temperature/fever.

## Emotional well-being

The majority of people feel a sense of relief after an abortion. However, everyone’s experience is different.
* **Mixed feelings:** If you feel sad or upset, these feelings usually fade in time. Studies show abortion has no long-lasting effect on mental health for most people.
* **Watch out for "crisis pregnancy centres":** Some centres may offer help but aim to make people feel guilty. Seek support from regulated, non-judgmental services instead.
* **Ongoing support:** If sadness persists, see a doctor or counsellor, as you may be experiencing depression or other mental health issues.
