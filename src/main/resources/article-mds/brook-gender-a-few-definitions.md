# The A-Z Of Gender: A Few Definitions

The language we use when talking about gender is very important because it can have an effect on people’s wellbeing. Language changes with time and is also personal; your meanings or use of words may be different from how other people use them. 

Not everyone will want to be known by the same terms, so the most important thing is to be respectful of someone else’s identity and the labels they choose to use. In turn, you deserve that respect back.

This glossary explores some of the main words related to gender, though it is not an exhaustive list.

## A

* **AFAB**: The acronym for ‘Assigned female at birth’. People are assigned female at birth usually based on the appearance of external genitalia (a vagina and vulva).
* **AMAB**: The acronym for ‘Assigned male at birth’. People are assigned male at birth usually based on the appearance of external genitalia (a penis and testicles).

## B

* **Binding**: The action of tightly wrapping the chest with garments or materials in order to minimise the appearance of breasts. Binding is one of the many actions which trans people, particularly those assigned female at birth, sometimes choose to undertake to feel more comfortable.

## C

* **Cis or cisgender**: This describes a person whose gender identity is the same as the gender they were assigned at birth.
* **Cis man**: A man who was assigned male at birth.
* **Cis woman**: A woman who was assigned female at birth.
* **Closeted / In the Closet**: A term used to describe someone who identifies as LGBT+ in some way, but has not openly told anyone about this aspect of their identity.
* **Coming out**: When someone tells someone else about their LGBT+ identity. Coming out is rarely a once-in-a-lifetime event, as many LGBT+ people may need to come out to each new person they meet or may realise different aspects of their identity over time.

## D

* **Deadnaming / deadname**: This usually refers to intentionally or maliciously using a trans person’s previous name (e.g., the name they were given at birth) rather than the name they use currently. This can also happen by accident.

## F

* **Feminine**: A set of ideas and behaviours that are associated with women due to a society’s cultural ideas about gender.

## G

* **Gender**: The socially constructed ideas about human behaviour, actions, and roles in relation to ideas of ‘masculinity’ and ‘femininity’. The word gender is sometimes also used to refer to how a person sees or feels about themselves (gender identity).
* **Gender affirmation surgery**: An umbrella term used to describe various surgical procedures which some trans people may undergo as part of their transition.
* **Gender binary**: The conventional idea that everyone identifies as one of two genders (i.e., is either a boy or a girl). The word ‘binary’ comes from Latin and means ‘duality’ or ‘a pair’.
* **Gender dysphoria**: The distress or discomfort someone might feel when their sex assigned at birth doesn’t match their gender. Trans people may experience different levels of dysphoria, and some do not experience it at all.
* **Gender expression**: The outward behaviours and choices that a person makes in relation to their gender identity, including clothing, hairstyles, behaviour, and mannerisms.
* **Gender fluid**: Someone whose feelings about the relationship between their body and gender identity changes over time or is fluid.
* **Gender identity**: How a person sees or feels about themselves. It describes the personal sense of feeling or knowing that you are a man, woman, or non-binary person.
* **Gender neutral**: If something is gender neutral, it is not associated with a specific gender and has no limitations to use based on the gender of the person using it.
* **Gender non-conforming**: Someone whose gender expression doesn’t align with, or who rejects, the social expectations of their gender or the gender binary.
* **Gender roles**: The behaviours, mannerisms, personality traits, and interests that society expects men and women to have. These are socially constructed and change over history and across cultures.

## I

* **Intersex**: Some babies are born with external genitalia that don’t appear to be clearly either male or female. This may mean the baby has one of a group of conditions called differences or variations in sex development (VSD) or variations in sex characteristics (VSC). Some people with VSDs/VSCs use the word intersex to describe themselves.

## L

* **LGBT+**: The acronym for lesbian, gay, bisexual, and transgender. The ‘+’ is used to be inclusive of other identities beyond those in the acronym.

## M

* **Masculine**: A set of ideas and behaviours that are associated with men due to a society’s cultural ideas about gender.
* **Misgender**: The act of referring to someone as the wrong gender or using the wrong pronouns. This is often used to describe intentional or malicious acts but can also happen by accident.
* **Misogyny**: Prejudice and discrimination towards someone who is female or assumed to be female, or of items and behaviours associated with femininity.

## N

* **Non-binary**: An umbrella term for people whose gender identity isn't simply ‘man’ or ‘woman’. Non-binary people may feel their identity involves being both, neither, in between, or something else entirely.

## O

* **Outing / Out**: To ‘out’ someone is to tell someone about someone else’s gender identity or sexual orientation without their consent.

## P

* **Passing**: Refers to whether someone is perceived as the gender they identify with.
* **Pride**: Having a positive view of being part of the LGBT+ community. It is also a worldwide event celebrating LGBT+ cultures and protesting discrimination.
* **Pronouns**: Words used instead of names, such as ‘he’, ‘she’, or ‘they’. You should always use the pronouns that people ask you to use. Gender-neutral pronouns include ‘they/them’, ‘ze/zem/zirs’, and ‘xe/xem/xeir’.

## Q

* **Queer**: An umbrella term used by some to describe members of the LGBT+ community or an identity that rejects specific labels. Historically used as a slur, the term has been reclaimed by many, though some still find it offensive.
* **Questioning**: A term used to describe people who are in the process of exploring their sexual orientation or gender identity.

## S

* **Sex**: Biological sexual characteristics, including external genitals, internal reproductive organs, chromosomes, and hormones.
* **Sex assigned at birth**: The sex that a person is given when they are born, usually based on the appearance of external genitalia.

## T

* **Trans / transgender**: When a person's sex assigned at birth doesn’t match or sit easily with their gender identity. Trans is an umbrella term describing various ways of not belonging to the gender aligning with the sex assigned at birth.
* **Transfeminine / transfemme**: A broad term to describe trans people whose gender or expression is mostly feminine.
* **Transitioning**: The process of changing your gender expression to match your gender identity. This can involve social, legal, and/or medical changes.
* **Transmasculine / transmasc**: A broad term to describe trans people whose gender or expression is mostly masculine.
* **Transphobia**: Prejudice and discrimination towards someone who is trans based on their trans identity. This can include bullying, harassment, and the denial of someone's gender identity.
* **Trans man**: A man who was assigned female at birth.
* **Trans woman**: A woman who was assigned male at birth.
