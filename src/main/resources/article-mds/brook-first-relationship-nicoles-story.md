# First Relationship: Nicole’s Story

Nicole, 21, shares how her first relationship was a positive and formative experience, while exploring the reasons why she is now happy to be single.

**Content warning:** This article includes discussions of anxiety.

## Growing Up Together

Nicole’s first relationship began at school when she was 14. Following their first kiss at 15, she and her partner stayed together for five years, transitioning from mid-teens into young adulthood.

During the early years, the relationship was built on a deep friendship and shared simple activities, such as:
* Watching movies
* Cooking and eating together
* Playing games
* Walking the family dogs

Nicole notes that the simplicity of these early days, where they didn't have specific plans but just enjoyed each other's company, remains a cherished memory.

## A Positive Introduction to Sex

Nicole considers herself fortunate to have had a positive introduction to sexual experiences. Because the couple loved and trusted each other, they prioritized mutual pleasure and had access to a safe environment at home.

After hearing about the negative early experiences of many of her peers, Nicole realized that her positive experience was not the universal norm. This realization sparked a passion for sex education, highlighting the importance of trust and safety in early sexual encounters.

## Emotional Dependency and Changing Perspectives

As the couple grew older, they began discussing marriage. However, looking back, Nicole reflects that her desire to marry was driven more by a fear of losing her partner than a genuine desire for marriage at that age. She also felt a conflicting fear regarding the prospect of being with only one person for her entire life.

A significant turning point occurred during a gap year before university. Spending a month apart allowed Nicole to experience:
* Life as a single person
* Personal independence
* Freedom from emotional reliance on another person

During this time, Nicole realized she had become emotionally dependent on her boyfriend. While he provided safety and support for her anxiety, she recognized the need to learn how to be happy and stable on her own.

## Transitioning to Independence

The relationship became strained after the gap year. Although they remained together for another year, the shift in Nicole's perspective made it difficult to maintain the bond. When they moved to different universities, the challenges of a long-distance relationship ultimately led to their breakup.

Nicole describes the breakup as a difficult but necessary decision. While she grieved the end of the relationship, she also felt a sense of relief.

## Life as a Single Person

Nearly two years after the breakup, Nicole remains single and reports being happier than ever. Her time spent alone has allowed her to:
* Focus on personal growth and independence
* Rely on herself for happiness
* Nurture and grow her friendships
* Explore new passions and adventures

She emphasizes that the relationship wasn't "holding her back" in a traditional sense, but that she needed the space of singlehood to develop her own identity.

## Support and Mental Wellbeing

It is estimated that approximately 1 in 4 people in the UK will experience a mental health problem each year. If you are worried about your mental health or the wellbeing of someone else, support is available.

You do not need a formal diagnosis to seek help. If you are struggling with your emotions or the end of a relationship, reaching out to friends, family, or professional services can provide the necessary support to help you move forward.
