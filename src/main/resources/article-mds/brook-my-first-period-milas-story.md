# My First Period: Mila's Story

## Introduction

Mila, aged 15, shares her experience of getting her first period and the emotions she felt.

## Mila's Experience

* Mila first got her period at 11 years old while on holiday in Scotland at her cousin's house.
* She was initially scared and confused, and used toilet roll as a makeshift pad.
* The experience was particularly challenging as she was wearing shorts during a walk up a mountain, and she experienced heavy bleeding with fevers.
* Her mother noticed she was uncomfortable, and although nothing embarrassing happened, Mila felt she was hiding something.

## Challenges and Feelings

* Mila describes her periods as progressively worsening over the years, often leaving her feeling ill, similar to having the flu.
* She recently started taking the pill for acne, but it has not helped her period symptoms.
* Her first experience using a tampon was uncomfortable because she was not taught to insert it fully at school, leading to it falling out during a 6-hour street dance class.
* She felt unprepared for tampon use, noting that the way it was demonstrated in primary school (inserting it into blue liquid) did not reflect the reality of its use and the absence of visual cues like the liquid turning blue.
* Mila highlights that educational resources often omit side effects like cramps.
* She experiences significant depression a week before her period and continues to feel depressed throughout it, which impacts her monthly life.
* Severe acne and associated insecurity are also common issues.
* Mila believes educational programs do not adequately address the reality of periods, especially for early bloomers. She was one of the first in her class to wear a training bra.
* The "becoming a woman" aspect was daunting, and the thought of potential pregnancy at 11 was a significant concern.

***Note:*** *It is possible to become pregnant before your first period, as ovulation may have already begun. Please refer to the periods page for more information.*
