# Vasectomy (Male Sterilisation)

A vasectomy is a permanent form of contraception in which the tubes that carry sperm from the testicles to the penis are tied, cut, or sealed. This prevents sperm from being present in the semen during ejaculation, meaning an egg cannot be fertilised.

## Quick Facts
* **Effectiveness:** More than 99% effective.
* **Hormones:** Contains no hormones and does not affect sex drive or erections.
* **STI Protection:** Does not protect against sexually transmitted infections (STIs).
* **Permanence:** Designed to be permanent and very difficult to reverse.
* **Visibility:** Invisible once healed, apart from temporary stitches.
* **Requirements:** Requires more than one consultation. You must use other forms of contraception until tests confirm the procedure was successful.

## Pros and Cons

### Pros
* Over 99% effective at preventing pregnancy.
* Once confirmed as working, you do not have to think about contraception again.
* Long-term health effects are rare.
* No impact on hormones or sex drive.

### Cons
* Not easily reversed; reversals are rarely funded on the NHS and are expensive privately.
* Provides no protection against STIs.
* Alternative contraception is required for several months until follow-up tests are clear.
* Small risk of complications during or after the procedure.
* Minor temporary side effects such as swelling, bruising, or mild discomfort.

## The Procedure
A vasectomy is a quick surgical procedure usually performed under local anaesthetic. Most people can return home the same day. There are two main types:

### Conventional Vasectomy
* The scrotum is numbed with local anaesthetic.
* The doctor makes two small cuts on each side of the scrotum to reach the tubes (vas deferens).
* Each tube is cut, and a small section is removed.
* The ends of the tubes are closed by tying them or sealing them with heat.
* The cuts are closed with stitches (usually dissolvable).

### No-scalpel Vasectomy
* The scrotum is numbed with local anaesthetic.
* The doctor makes a tiny puncture hole in the skin of the scrotum instead of a cut.
* The tubes are closed in the same way as a conventional vasectomy.
* This method involves less bleeding and no stitches, and it is generally thought to be less painful with fewer complications.

## After the Operation
* **Recovery:** You may need a couple of days off work. You should avoid sex for at least 7 days.
* **Side Effects:** You may experience a swollen or bruised scrotum or blood in your semen for a few days.
* **Backup Contraception:** You must continue using another method of contraception for 8–12 weeks after the procedure.
* **Semen Testing:** About 12 weeks after the procedure, you will need to provide a semen sample to confirm it is sperm-free. You are only considered "sterile" once these tests are successful.

## Accessing a Vasectomy
In most parts of the UK, vasectomies are available for free on the NHS, though waiting lists can be long. You can also choose to pay for the procedure privately. 

While there is no legal minimum age, doctors may advise against it if you are under 30. A doctor may refer you for additional counselling to ensure you are 100% certain about the decision, as it is intended to be a permanent choice.

## Confidentiality
Contraception and sexual health services like Brook are free and confidential, including for those under the age of 16. Health professionals will not tell anyone about your visit unless they believe you are at serious risk of immediate harm.
