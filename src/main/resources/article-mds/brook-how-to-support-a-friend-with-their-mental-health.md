# How to support a friend with their mental health

It can be difficult to know what to say to a friend who is struggling with their mental health. If you’ve noticed a change in your friend—perhaps they are staying in more, cancelling plans, or no longer enjoying things they used to—it is a good idea to talk to them.

Even if they aren't ready to talk, knowing that someone cares can make a significant difference. You might start the conversation by saying: “I’ve noticed recently that you’ve not been yourself, what’s up?” or by asking, “Really, how are you?” if they give a standard "fine" response. Being honest about your own stresses, such as exam pressure or work, can also create a safe space for them to share.

## Tips for talking about mental health

When preparing to support a friend, keep the following principles in mind:

* **Make sure you have the headspace:** Only start the conversation when you can give your friend your full attention. You need to look after your own wellbeing first.
* **Go somewhere quiet:** Choose a private place where you won’t be overheard. Walking side-by-side can often feel less intense than sitting face-to-face.
* **Validate their feelings:** Let them know their feelings make sense. Avoid saying things like "everyone feels like that," which can be invalidating.
* **Be patient and actively listen:** Allow for silences and don't rush them. Use eye contact and nodding to show you are listening.
* **Don't offer solutions unless asked:** Often, people just want to be heard. If you aren't sure what they need, ask: "How can I help you with this?"
* **Avoid toxic positivity:** Phrases like "look on the bright side" or "everything happens for a reason" can make a person feel pressured to hide their true emotions.
* **Don't force them to talk:** If they aren't ready, respect their space but remind them that you are there when they are ready.
* **Don't make it about you:** While empathy is good, avoid over-comparing their situation to your own experiences unless they specifically ask.

## Looking after your own wellbeing

Supporting someone else can be emotionally draining. It is essential to maintain your own mental health while helping others.

* **Set boundaries:** If a topic is triggering for you, or if you don't have the headspace at that moment, it is okay to say so.
* **Communicate your own needs:** A friendship should not be one-sided. Share your own struggles and let your friend support you too.
* **Encourage professional support:** Ensure your friend is not relying solely on you. Encourage them to seek help from family, school/work, or professional support organisations.
* **Address safety concerns:** If you are worried about your friend’s safety, talk to a trusted adult or professional. While you should ask for their permission to share information, their safety is the priority.
* **Maintain the friendship:** Remind your friend they are more than their mental health. Continue to arrange fun activities, like grabbing coffee or exercising together, to keep the bond strong.
* **Provide practical support:** If emotional support feels overwhelming, offer practical help with tasks like homework or household chores.
