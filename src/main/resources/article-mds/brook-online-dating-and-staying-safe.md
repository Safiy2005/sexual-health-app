# Online Dating: 7 Safety Tips

Dating apps are wildly popular and provide an instant, low-effort way to flirt and meet new people. In fact, a quarter of people now meet their partners online. While dating apps are useful tools, there are several safety considerations to keep in mind when messaging people and transitioning to first dates.

Most dating apps are restricted to people over the age of 18 to help keep young people safe.

## 1. Take your time before meeting in person
If you match with someone you really like, you might be tempted to meet up immediately. However, it is important to take the time to get to know the person first. Don’t feel pressured to meet before you’re ready. Getting to know their interests and personality beforehand is not only safer but can save you time and effort if they aren't a good match.

Try to have a conversation and get a feel for who they are and what they do before accepting a date.

## 2. Verify their identity
Not everyone on dating apps is who they say they are; these individuals are often referred to as "catfishes." Verifying someone's identity is a sensible precaution. You can:

* **Video call before the date:** This can ease nerves and confirm they match their profile.
* **Check for verified profiles:** Use apps that have verification features to ensure images are genuine.
* **Check social media:** Look for tagged photos and established accounts. Scammer accounts often have very few followers, no tagged photos, and were recently created.
* **Google reverse search images:** Use Google to see if their profile pictures appear elsewhere under different names.
* **Request a specific photo:** Ask them to take a photo doing something random (like holding a spoon) to prove they are real.

## 3. Protect your personal information and money
Never send money or share sensitive personal details—such as your home address or surname—with someone you haven't met. Many scammers use dating apps specifically to solicit money. If someone asks for money, report them to the app and **ActionFraud**.

It is safer to keep your messaging within the dating app until you have met and established trust. If someone pressures you to move to WhatsApp or another platform, you can politely decline.

## 4. Trust your instincts
Your gut feeling is a powerful tool. Be wary if someone:
* Asks too many personal questions while revealing little about themselves.
* Declares "undying love" before you have even met.
* Makes you feel uneasy in any way.

If you are on a date and feel uncomfortable, you have every right to end it. You can make an excuse or have a friend call you. Many venues also support the **‘Ask for Angela’** scheme, where you can alert staff that you feel unsafe, and they will help you get home safely.

## 5. Be aware of nudes and cyberflashing
Be cautious if someone asks for nude photos before you have met. Once you share images online, you lose control of them. If someone pressures you to send nudes, this is unacceptable behavior and you should block them.

Sharing naked images without consent—known as **cyberflashing**—is a form of sexual harassment. If you receive an unwanted nude, report the user to the app and block them. Sending or receiving naked images of anyone under 18 is illegal. If this happens, contact a trusted adult or organizations like **Brook** or **Childline**.

## 6. Plan a safe first meeting
Choosing the right location and time is essential for safety:
* **Meet in a public place:** Ensure there are plenty of people around and good phone signal.
* **Choose a familiar location:** This will help you feel more comfortable.
* **Avoid home visits:** Never go to their house or invite them to yours for a first meeting.
* **Arrange your own transport:** Don't let a date pick you up. Ensure you can get to and from the date independently.
* **Keep it simple:** A quick coffee or drink is better than a long meal. It's easier to leave if things aren't going well.
* **Limit alcohol:** Be mindful of your alcohol intake so you can get yourself home safely. If a date pressures you to drink more than you want, they may not be a good match.

## 7. Keep others informed
Always ensure a friend or family member knows where you are going and who you are meeting. 
* Share a screenshot of the person’s profile.
* Keep your phone charged.
* Update your friend throughout the date.
* Consider sharing your live location via your phone.
* Plan your route home in advance and let someone know when you have returned safely.
