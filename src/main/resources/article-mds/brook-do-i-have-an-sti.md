# Do I Have an STI?

If you’ve had unprotected sexual contact, there is a chance you could have a sexually transmitted infection (STI). Anyone can get an STI, regardless of how many times they have had sex or how many partners they have had.

It is important to remember that you may or may not have symptoms; physical signs are not a reliable way of knowing if you have an STI. Without being examined and tested by a health professional, you cannot know for certain what is causing your symptoms.

## Common Symptoms of STIs

### Unusual Discharge
Discharge is fluid or mucus that comes out of the body. While some discharge is normal, changes can indicate an infection:
* **Chlamydia:** Unusual discharge from the vagina, penis, or rectum.
* **Gonorrhoea:** Unusual green or yellow discharge from the vagina; or green, yellow, or white discharge from the tip of the penis.
* **Trichomoniasis:** Frothy, yellow-green, and/or fishy-smelling vaginal discharge; or thin, white discharge from the penis.
* **Urethritis (can be caused by an STI):** White or cloudy discharge from the tip of the penis.
* **Thrush (not an STI):** Thick, white vaginal discharge (like cottage cheese) with no smell; or cottage cheese-like discharge under the foreskin.
* **Vaginitis (not an STI):** General unusual discharge.

### Stinging, Tingling, Burning, or Itching
* **Chlamydia:** Burning and itching in the penis area.
* **Genital Herpes:** Stinging, tingling, or itching in the genital or anal area.
* **Trichomoniasis:** Itching, swelling, and soreness of the penis or vagina; itchy inner thighs in women.
* **Pubic Lice:** Itching, irritation, or inflammation; you may also see the lice or their eggs.
* **Thrush (not an STI):** Itching and soreness around the vagina; stinging when urinating; sore/itchy penis.
* **Vaginitis (not an STI):** Irritation or itching of the vagina.

### Painful Swelling
* **Chlamydia or Gonorrhoea:** Painful swelling of the testicles (rare).
* **Gonorrhoea:** Painful swelling of the foreskin.
* **Trichomoniasis:** Swelling and soreness of the penis or vagina.
* **Urethritis (not an STI):** Irritation or soreness at the tip of the penis.

### Pain During Urination
* **Chlamydia:** Pain when peeing.
* **Gonorrhoea:** Burning feeling when peeing.
* **Trichomoniasis:** Pain while urinating; needing to urinate more often (in people with penises).
* **Cystitis (not an STI, but can result from one):** Frequent need to urinate, only passing small amounts, pain/stinging, or dark/cloudy/smelly urine.
* **Urethritis (not an STI):** Pain, burning, or frequent need to urinate in people with penises.

### Irregular Bleeding and Abdominal Pain
* **Chlamydia:** Irregular bleeding; bleeding during or after sex; pain/tenderness in the lower abdomen; pelvic pain.
* **Gonorrhoea:** Pain or tenderness in the lower abdomen.
* **Trichomoniasis:** Lower abdominal pain (in people with vaginas).
* **Vaginitis (not an STI):** Light bleeding/spotting; pain during sex; strong unpleasant smell from the vagina after sex.

### Blisters, Sores, and Other Signs
* **Genital Herpes:** Small fluid-filled blisters in the genital/anal area, buttocks, or thighs that burst to leave painful red sores.
* **Syphilis:** A small painless sore on the vagina, penis, or anus.
* **Molluscum:** Blisters or sores.
* **Pubic Lice:** Black powder in underwear (lice droppings), brown eggs on body hair, or sky-blue spots/tiny specks of blood on the skin from bites.

## Asymptomatic STIs (No Symptoms)
Many people will never experience symptoms but can still pass on an infection. Symptoms can also take months to appear.
* **Chlamydia:** 75% of women and 50% of men show no symptoms.
* **Gonorrhoea:** 50% of women and 10% of men show no symptoms.
* **Genital Herpes:** Many people have no visible signs.
* **HIV:** 80% experience a short flu-like illness shortly after infection, which is often confused with a cold.
* **Trichomoniasis:** 50% of men and women have no symptoms.
* **Genital Warts:** Can take months or years to appear.

## Getting Tested
STI tests are usually quick and simple. They may involve a genital examination, a swab (from the vagina or tip of the penis), and a urine or blood sample.

* **If you have symptoms:** Contact a sexual health clinic (GUM), a GP surgery, or a Brook service immediately.
* **If you don't have symptoms:** You can order a home testing kit to complete and return via post.
* **Point of Care Testing:** Some locations offer rapid HIV testing with results available in less than 30 minutes using a saliva or finger-prick blood sample.

## Treatment and Protection
Most STIs can be treated with a short course of antibiotics. While some infections like HIV cannot be cured, they can be managed with treatment to prevent them from getting worse. 

If you test positive, your clinic will help you with "partner notification" to ensure previous and current partners are tested.

### Prevention
The best way to avoid STIs is to use a condom or dam every time you have sex (vaginal, anal, or oral). Contraceptive methods like the pill or implants do not protect against STIs.

### When to Test
If you do not have symptoms, it is best to wait **6-7 weeks** after unprotected sex to get a fully accurate result.
* **Chlamydia and Gonorrhoea:** Usually show up after 2 weeks.
* **HIV:** Usually shows up after 6-7 weeks.
Testing too early may result in a false negative. If you have symptoms, do not wait; contact a professional immediately.
