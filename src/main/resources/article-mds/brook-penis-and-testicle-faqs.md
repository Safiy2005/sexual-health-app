# Penis and Testicle FAQs

## How do I know if my penis is normal?
Penises come in many different shapes, sizes, and colours. There is a wide range of "normal" when it comes to appearance.

## Do I need to see a doctor about my penis?
If you have noticed changes or are experiencing symptoms, it is recommended that you see a doctor. Symptoms to look out for include:
* Itchiness
* Dryness
* Discharge
* Soreness of the penis or testicles

## Does size matter?
Penises come in various shapes and sizes, and all are valid. The size of your penis has no connection to how much pleasure you can bring a partner. Communication is key; check with your partner(s) about what they enjoy during sex.

Size does matter when choosing a condom. A condom that is too big might fall off, and one that is too small might break. It is important to find the right fit for your safety.

## Can I do anything to change my penis if I am unhappy with how it looks?
Many products marketed for penis enlargement have little to no evidence of working. While surgery is an option, it can come with complications and is considered cosmetic, as there is usually no medical justification for it. It is important to celebrate body diversity and learn to value your body for the pleasure it can provide.

## Should I remove my pubic hair?
Whether you leave your pubic hair, trim it, or remove it entirely is a personal choice. However, remember that pubic hair serves a purpose: it provides protection from bacteria and reduces friction during sex. Removing it can sometimes lead to soreness and ingrown hairs.

## What contraception can I use?
Condoms are a primary method of contraception that protect against both pregnancy and sexually transmitted infections (STIs). They work by stopping sperm from entering the vagina and preventing the transfer of sexual fluids. When used correctly, condoms are 98% effective. They are available for free at Brook services, contraception clinics, and GUM clinics.

## Will something happen to my penis if I masturbate?
No. Myths about masturbation causing long-term damage are false. If you masturbate frequently in a short period, the skin may become temporarily irritated, but this is not permanent.

## Are blue balls a real thing?
"Blue balls" is the informal term for epididymal hypertension. It refers to aching or painful testicles caused by blood buildup during prolonged sexual arousal that does not result in orgasm. While uncomfortable, it is not harmful and will subside as blood flow returns to normal. 

To help ease the discomfort, you can try:
* Ejaculation (through masturbation or consensual sex)
* Distracting yourself with a puzzle or problem
* Taking a cold shower to restrict blood flow
* Lying down to move blood flow away from the testicles
* Exercising to encourage general circulation
* Applying a warm compress

**Note:** You should never use "blue balls" as a reason to pressure someone into having sex.

## Can a penis break or tear?
Yes, it is possible to "break" a penis (a penile fracture) if it is dramatically bent or twisted while erect. This is a rare emergency that requires surgical treatment. 

Additionally, the frenulum (the skin connecting the foreskin to the shaft) can tear during rough sex or vigorous masturbation. This can be painful and cause bleeding. If this happens:
* Avoid all sexual activity until it has fully healed.
* Use lubricant in the future to prevent re-tearing.
* See a GP or visit a sexual health clinic if it does not heal.

## I’m trans, how will medically transitioning affect my penis and testicles?
Medical transitioning through hormone therapy or gender-affirming surgery may change the size, shape, and functionality of the penis and testicles. There are many ways to transition, and some methods do not affect the genitals at all.

## What should I do if I find a lump on my penis or testicles?
Lumps can occur for many reasons; some are benign, while others require medical attention. You should check your penis and testicles regularly. If you find a new lump, visit a doctor to have it checked.

## Is it a bad sign if my penis smells bad?
Genitals generally have a minimal smell. A strong odor might be due to hygiene. You should wash the penis and testicles daily with warm water and soap, ensuring you wash under the foreskin to prevent the build-up of smegma. If you have bad-smelling discharge, it could be a sign of an infection or STI.

## Why does it burn when I wee?
A burning sensation during urination can indicate inflammation of the bladder (cystitis) or urethra (urethritis). It is also a common symptom of STIs such as chlamydia, gonorrhoea, or trichomoniasis.

## Why is my penis and/or testicles itchy?
Itchiness can be a sign of skin conditions or STIs. You should consult a doctor or visit a sexual health clinic to determine the cause.

## I’ve had sex without a condom, do I need to test for STIs even though I have no symptoms?
Yes. Many STIs do not show symptoms. If left untreated, they can cause serious problems like infertility.
* For most STIs, it is best to wait two weeks after unprotected sex for an accurate test result.
* For HIV testing, the window period is six weeks.
