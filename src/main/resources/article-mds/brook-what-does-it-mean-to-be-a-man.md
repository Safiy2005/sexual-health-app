# What does it mean to be a man?

Stereotypical masculinity can be harmful for everyone, including men and boys. Masculinity and femininity are often seen as opposites—the idea that to be masculine, you must not be feminine. This can make it seem like having feminine traits is a bad thing, which it isn’t. Everyone should be able to show traits of both masculinity and femininity without feeling like they don’t fit in.

Gender stereotypes are ideas created by society about how we should act based on our gender. While these roles may have roots in the past (e.g., men as providers and women as caregivers), they no longer apply to our modern society where individuals can choose their own roles.

## Emotions and Mental Health

> “To be masculine is to not cry and suck it up if upset” – Young person

Gender stereotypes often tell men they shouldn't express emotions other than anger or jealousy. This can have severe negative impacts, causing men to:

* Feel they can't speak out about their feelings.
* Bottle up their emotions.
* Lack meaningful connections in relationships.
* Feel unable to ask for help.

This has real-world consequences. Boys are far less likely than girls to ask for help or call support services like Childline. In 2021, three out of four suicides in England were males, and twice as many boys aged 10-19 die from suicide each year compared to girls. It is important to remember that emotions don't have a gender; paying attention to them is a key step in understanding yourself and getting support.

## The Myth of "Alpha" and "Beta" Males

> “To be a man in the world is like always being prepared for battle… you need to battle for the house, money, status and woman you want… if you don’t have grind mentality, you’ll fail.” – Young person

The terms "alpha," "beta," "sigma," and "chad" are often used to categorise men based on strength, status, and charisma. However, there are several problems with this:

* **There is no such thing as an "alpha" male:** Humans have different strengths and weaknesses; being a leader in every situation is impossible.
* **Diversity of personality:** Categorising men ignores the vast range of personalities and strengths that boys and men possess.
* **Insecurity:** Constant comparison to others can lead to a trap of never feeling "enough."
* **Negative behavior:** Trying to be an "alpha" can lead to putting others down, making sexual or homophobic comments, or criticising others for not being "man enough."

True leadership involves empowering others and standing up for people, rather than dominating them.

## Body Image and Physical Appearance

Social media and film often promote a "perfect" male body—tall, handsome, and muscular. This lacks realism and can harm mental and physical health.

### Height
The average height for men is 5ft 9 inches, despite the pressure often felt on dating apps to be over 6ft. The idea that a man must be taller than his partner is a dated stereotype of dominance.

### Muscle and Body Dysmorphia
As many as a third of people with eating disorders are boys and men. Stereotypes can lead to:
* **Bigorexia:** An obsession with muscle mass, diet, and fitness, leading to guilt over rest days or specific foods.
* **Body Dysmorphia:** An anxiety disorder where someone becomes obsessed with perceived flaws in their appearance.

**Body neutrality** is a helpful alternative to these pressures. It focuses on accepting your body as it is without judgment and being thankful for what it allows you to do.

## Relationships and the "Manosphere"

Stereotypical masculinity often associates "manliness" with the ability to attract women (sometimes called "rizz").

* **Rejection:** If self-worth is tied to popularity with women, rejection can be devastating. This insecurity can lead people toward the "manosphere"—online spaces that promote harmful stereotypes and blame women for men's frustrations.
* **Objectification:** Seeing women as "achievements" to gain status among peers leads to unfulfilling and unhealthy relationships.
* **LGBT+ Experiences:** These stereotypes often exclude or marginalise gay, bi, and trans men. Trans people may feel pressure to perform these stereotypes or hide masculine traits to fit societal expectations.

## Sexuality and Consent

Stereotypical masculinity incorrectly suggests that men always want sex and do not value emotional connection. This contributes to:

* **Sexual Violence and Harassment:** The "boys will be boys" myth suggests men cannot control themselves, leading to misogynistic abuse and online harassment.
* **Pressure from Pornography:** Porn often promotes unrealistic expectations of how men should act during sex.
* **Personal Readiness:** Every person’s sexuality is unique. Men are allowed to want intimacy, dislike certain types of sex, or not want sex at all. The "right time" for sex is whenever you feel ready, and your decision should always be respected.

## Challenging Masculinity

Challenging these outdated ideas allows everyone to be more accepting of themselves. By moving away from restrictive stereotypes, men and boys can lead healthier, more fulfilling lives.
