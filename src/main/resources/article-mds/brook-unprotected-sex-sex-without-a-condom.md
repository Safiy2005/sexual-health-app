# Unprotected sex: Sex without a condom

Unprotected sex is any sort of sex where protection isn’t used. It is often used to mean having penetrative vaginal or anal sex without a condom, or when a method of contraception or barrier method has failed (for example, a split condom or a missed pill).

Having unprotected sex can put you at risk of sexually transmitted infections (STIs) or unplanned pregnancy. It’s important to take action soon after it happens to keep yourself and your partner safe. If you are worried, remember that your local sexual health service or doctor will be able to help you.

## Emergency Contraception

If you have the ability to become pregnant but do not want to be, you have options following unprotected sex. You have up to **5 days** to take action:

* **Emergency contraceptive pill:** Available from pharmacies, sexual health clinics, and GPs.
* **IUD (intrauterine device):** This is the most effective form of emergency contraception and must be fitted by a healthcare professional.

The sooner you access emergency contraception, the more effective it is. It is recommended that you do not wait longer than necessary.

## STI Testing

Regardless of pregnancy risk, everyone who has had unprotected sex should consider taking an STI test. Getting tested regularly is the best way to protect yourself and others. If an infection is found, it should be treated as soon as possible to avoid health complications and prevent it from being passed on to partners.

* **When to test:** It is best to wait at least **two weeks** after unprotected sex before getting tested. It can take this long for certain STIs to show up on a test.
* **Where to go:** You can find STI testing and treatment at local sexual health services, or you may be eligible for free STI home testing kits.

## Summary of Risks
* **Unplanned pregnancy:** Risk is present during vaginal sex if no contraception is used or if a method fails.
* **Sexually Transmitted Infections (STIs):** Risk is present during both vaginal and anal sex without a barrier method.
