# Dating Apps: Chloe’s Story

Chloe, 22, shares their experience of online romance along with some tips for navigating dating apps as a Queer Non-Binary Disabled Person.

## Early Experiences with Online Dating

I remember downloading my first dating app when I was around 14, primarily because everyone else was doing it and I felt the need to be involved. It wasn’t until I was around 17 that I started wanting a more genuine romantic relationship. Looking back, much of that desire was driven by peer pressure and the feeling of being the "odd one out" in school. I also longed for the type of love portrayed on the big screen.

## Navigating Apps as a Queer Non-Binary Disabled Person

Navigating dating apps with these intersecting identities can be tedious. It is often difficult to find someone genuine who matches my preferences, maintains a good conversation, and understands my needs as a disabled person.

I used to struggle with the feeling that I couldn't expect someone to accommodate my needs since they didn't know me yet. However, I now believe that discussing these needs is a necessary part of dating. It allows the other person to understand my situation and helps me see if they are willing to be accommodating.

## The Reality of Online Connections

I have had dating apps on my phone for the past four years, but I haven’t had much luck finding lasting real-life connections. Online romances often feel superficial and rarely last. Personally, I find that I need to connect with someone face-to-face rather than solely online.

However, going on a date as a disabled person can be daunting due to support needs, the inaccessibility of certain spaces, and the potential need for a companion. For a long time, I felt I could never go on a date because I struggle to go outside alone. Once I accepted that the right person would understand these requirements, it opened many doors.

### Using a Dating Companion

While I haven't set up many dates through apps, I have gone on dates arranged via social media. When I do, I prefer to have a companion accompany me. They help me get to the venue and meet my date, then they sit a couple of tables away—readily available if I need support.

## The Psychology of Dating Apps

One thing I struggled with early on was the addictive nature of online connections. This feeling is similar to "doom-scrolling" because we receive dopamine hits for tasks like scrolling, swiping, and matching. 

The initial stages of dating release high levels of dopamine, creating an "online honeymoon phase." Quite often, the connection fades once this dopamine level decreases and one or both people become "bored" of the purely online interaction. This is why I believe real-life connectivity is so vital.

## Tips for Navigating Dating Apps

Based on my experiences, here are some things that have helped me navigate the dating world:

* **Be honest about your needs:** If you need a companion to join you on a date, be upfront about it.
* **Communicate openly:** Have honest communication from the start; the right person will stick around.
* **Be unapologetically yourself:** Express yourself fully. You are more likely to find someone who accepts you for who you are if you don't hold back.
* **Focus on quality, not quantity:** Don’t worry about the total number of matches you have.
* **Set time limits:** Try to limit yourself to 15 minutes of swiping at a time and perhaps an hour or two a day on the apps to avoid burnout.
* **Explore local events:** If you feel comfortable, try attending local in-person events.
* **Prepare conversation starters:** If you’re worried about what to say, make a note of questions or topics you’re happy to talk about.
* **Acknowledge nerves:** Keep in mind that your date will likely be nervous too.
* **Prioritize self-care:** Remember that dating isn’t everything; treat yourself and take yourself on dates too.
