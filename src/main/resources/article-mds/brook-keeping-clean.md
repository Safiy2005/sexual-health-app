# Keeping Clean

Puberty causes various changes in your body, and you may need to adjust how you keep yourself clean and take care of your physical health. During this time, your skin and scalp may become greasy, new hair grows in different places, and you might notice increased sweating or new body odours.

## Managing Oily Hair
The same hormones that cause spots and acne also affect your hair. During puberty, the sebaceous glands (mostly found on the face and scalp) produce extra oil, which can make your hair look shiny or greasy. 

To manage this:
* Wash your hair more frequently.
* Use hair products specifically formulated to control oily hair.

## Managing Sweat and Body Odour
During puberty, sweat glands become more active and secrete different chemicals, leading to a stronger odour. You may notice this particularly in your armpits, feet, or genitals.

To keep unpleasant smells away:
* Bathe or shower every day using warm water and a mild soap or shower gel to wash away bacteria.
* Wear clean clothes, socks, and underwear daily.
* Choose clothing made from cotton or other natural materials, as these absorb sweat more effectively and are less likely to smell than man-made fibres.
* Consider using a deodorant or antiperspirant. Deodorants cover the smell of sweat, while antiperspirants help stop or dry up perspiration.

## Body Hair
Regardless of gender, you can expect hair to start growing on your body, particularly under your arms and on your legs. Whether you choose to remove this hair or leave it is entirely up to you—your body belongs to you.

If you choose to remove hair, common methods include:
* Shaving
* Waxing
* Electrolysis
* Sugaring
* Lasering

## Facial Hair
### Shaving for Men
Facial hair typically starts growing around age 16 or 17, though it can begin earlier or later. If you choose to shave, you can use:
* **Traditional razors:** Use a new, sharp blade to prevent cuts. Use shaving cream or gel rather than soap, as they make it easier for the razor to glide over the skin.
* **Electric razors:** A popular alternative to traditional blades.

Always follow instructions carefully and take your time, especially around curves and angles of the face, to avoid nicks and cuts.

### Facial Hair for Women
Some young women may find they have more facial hair than expected, which can be a source of worry. There are several ways to manage this:
* **Bleaching:** Lightens the hair to make it less visible.
* **Hair removers:** Products specifically designed for facial hair removal.
* **Moisturisers:** Oil-free facial moisturisers can contain substances that make facial hair softer and less noticeable.
