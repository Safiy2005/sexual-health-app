# Love Your Penis

Like every other part of your body, everyone’s penis and testicles are different. It is common to worry about what is considered "normal," but understanding the diversity of bodies can help you appreciate your own.

## Size

Many people worry that their penis is too small, often because average sizes are frequently exaggerated or overestimated. 

* **Growth:** During puberty (usually between ages 11-18), the penis and testicles will grow. They typically do not stop growing completely until around age 21.
* **Average Size:** The average adult penis is usually about 13–18cm (5 to 7 inches) when erect.
* **Flaccid State:** When soft (flaccid), the length of a penis can vary significantly depending on external factors such as temperature.

## Shape

Penises come in many different shapes. 
* **Width:** Some are the same width from base to tip, while others may be wider or narrower at certain points. 
* **Curve:** When erect, penises can curve up, down, left, or right. Having a curved penis is not unusual.

## Colour

The colour of a penis may not be consistent and can change when it is erect due to increased blood flow. 
* **Changes:** While most colouration is normal, some changes can be related to health conditions like STIs or skin issues. 
* **Advice:** If you notice unusual or persistent discolouration, it is worth consulting your GP to rule out anything serious.

## Understanding Erections

Your body may behave in unexpected ways, particularly during puberty. Erections are not always connected to arousal or physical stimulation.
* **Inconsistency:** You may have an erection when you aren’t aroused, or you might not have an erection even when you are. This is completely normal and affects most people throughout their lives.

## When Erections Don’t Last

There can be significant pressure to maintain an erection during sex, but in reality, penises are soft most of the time. Erections can fluctuate regardless of how aroused you are.

* **Normalcy:** It is natural for an erection to subside during sexual activity. There are many ways to have sex that do not require an erect penis.
* **Erectile Dysfunction:** If you regularly have difficulty maintaining erections and it affects your mental health, you may want to seek help. This can be caused by medical or psychological factors. Speaking to a GP, counsellor, or trusted adult can help reduce the anxiety and shame often associated with this issue.

## Involuntary Erections

Involuntary erections happen without warning and often at awkward moments, such as in the morning or when you are feeling relaxed. These are very common during puberty.

### Managing Involuntary Erections
If you experience an erection at an inappropriate time, remember that it is natural and likely less noticeable to others than it feels to you. You can try the following:

* **Stay calm:** Breathe slowly; the erection will eventually go away on its own.
* **Rearrange clothes:** Baggy clothing can help, or you can discreetly tuck the penis into the waistband of your underwear.
* **Use cover:** Place a bag or jumper on your lap.
* **Muscle tension:** Tense your glutes or quads for about thirty seconds to redirect blood flow away from the penis.
* **Meditation:** Focus on your breath or repeat a calming phrase to distract your mind.
* **Mental distraction:** Try solving a maths equation or recalling song lyrics.

Never do anything to conceal an erection that is painful or uncomfortable.

## Orgasm and the Refractory Period

Stimulation of the penis during arousal often leads to orgasm. 
* **Timing:** The average time of stimulation before reaching orgasm is between 5–10 minutes, though this varies greatly.
* **Refractory Period:** After orgasm, the penis usually becomes softer, and most people experience a period where another erection is not immediately possible.
* **Focus on Intimacy:** Sex is about intimacy and pleasure, not just how long you "last" or reaching a specific outcome like ejaculation.

## Porn and Reality

Pornography often portrays unrealistic body shapes and sizes. 
* **Distortion:** Actors in porn are often chosen because they are much larger than average, and videos may give the impression that people can sustain erections for unnaturally long periods.
* **Self-Esteem:** Comparing yourself to porn actors can be harmful to your self-esteem and your understanding of healthy relationships. Porn is often a fantasy and does not reflect how most people's bodies function.

## Masturbation

Masturbation is the act of stimulating yourself for sexual pleasure. It is a safe and healthy way to get to know your body and understand what feels good to you. 
* **Frequency:** You can masturbate as often or as little as you like. 
* **Preference:** Some people choose not to masturbate at all, which is also perfectly normal.
