# The Contraceptive Implant: Annie’s Story

Annie, 22, shares her experience of getting the contraceptive implant and why it has proven to be a successful long-term birth control option for her.

## Choosing the Right Contraception

In October 2020, Annie decided to get the contraceptive implant. Having used the same implant for two years, she highlights several reasons why this method suited her lifestyle:

* **Long-term effectiveness:** The implant remains fully effective for three years, meaning there is no need to think about replacement until that time has passed.
* **High reliability:** It is more than 99% effective at preventing pregnancy, providing a high level of protection and peace of mind.
* **Convenience:** Unlike the pill, which requires a daily routine, the implant is a "set and forget" method, making it ideal for those who may be forgetful.

## The Fitting Experience

Annie describes her appointment as being set up quickly and the procedure itself as less painful than anticipated. While the implant is inserted into the upper arm, the discomfort was minimal.

Shortly after the insertion, she experienced a brief emotional reaction:
* **Initial hormone adjustment:** Following the fitting, Annie experienced a period of emotional sensitivity (crying for about an hour), which she attributed to the initial flood of hormones.
* **Quick recovery:** After this initial adjustment period, she felt completely normal and has had no further complaints regarding the physical presence of the device.

## Changes to the Menstrual Cycle

Before booking her appointment, Annie researched how hormonal contraception might affect her body. She was aware that the implant could rearrange her menstrual cycle and patterns. Her experience included:

* **Initial adjustment:** For approximately eight weeks after insertion, she experienced constant menstruation.
* **Long-term amenorrhea:** Following the initial eight-week period, she has not had a single period for two years. 

For Annie, the cessation of her period was considered a significant positive outcome of choosing the implant.

## Advice for Others

It is essential to research different methods before choosing a hormonal contraceptive. Everyone’s body reacts differently, and what works for one person may not work for another. 

Key takeaways for those considering contraception:
* **Do your research:** Explore the various options available rather than simply choosing the first one offered.
* **Listen to your body:** Think about your specific needs, such as whether you can remember a daily pill or if you prefer a long-acting method.
* **Consult professionals:** Use available tools and clinical services to find the right fit for your health and lifestyle.

Overall, despite common "horror stories" regarding hormonal birth control, Annie’s experience has been very positive, providing her with effective protection and a method that works well for her body.
