# How to Get Over a Breakup

Breakups are tough, and it’s completely normal to feel sad or unsure about yourself. While every situation is unique, there are steps you can take to make the process of getting over someone easier.

## Give yourself space
Where possible, give yourself some space away from your ex. Avoid seeing them for a while to allow yourself time to adapt to the changes. 

* **Limit contact:** It can be tempting to get in touch, especially if you are thinking about them frequently. Remember that this urge is usually temporary and fades with time.
* **Social media boundaries:** It is often helpful to delete or block an ex on social media to prevent the temptation to monitor their life or initiate contact.

## Can you stay friends?
There is no right answer to whether you can stay friends, as everyone is different. 
* Some people find they can maintain a friendship without harm, while others find it impossible to move on while staying in contact.
* If you were friends before the relationship, transitioning back to a friendship might be easier, but it is not guaranteed.
* If you share mutual friends, you may need to stay in touch to some extent, but do not be afraid to prioritise your own needs and avoid certain social gatherings if necessary.
* Even if you intend to stay friends, taking an initial break from each other is crucial.

## Managing anger
Anger is a common emotion after a breakup. It is important to express this anger in healthy ways.
* **Acknowledge your feelings:** Do not try to suppress your anger; allow yourself to feel it.
* **Find healthy outlets:** Use exercise, journaling, or talking to a trusted friend to release frustration.
* **Avoid destructive behaviours:** Resist the urge to lash out at your ex or engage in self-destructive actions.
* **Seek professional help:** If you are struggling to manage your emotions, consider talking to a therapist or counsellor.

## Respecting the decision
If someone breaks up with you, you must respect their decision, regardless of how difficult it feels.
* **Consent:** Trying to force someone to change their mind is not consent. While media often portrays "chasing" as romantic, it is problematic in real life.
* **Acceptance:** If a relationship has ended, it is usually because it wasn't working for at least one person. Trying to force it back together rarely works.
* **If you ended it:** Feeling pain or heartbreak does not mean you made the wrong choice. It is normal to feel sad or regretful while still knowing that ending the relationship was the right decision for you.

## Sex with your ex
While it happens frequently, having sex with an ex can cause significant heartache for one or both parties.
* **Emotional setbacks:** Every time you see an ex, it can set back your recovery and keep you fixated on the past.
* **Unhealthy motivations:** Avoid trying to use sex to prove you are doing "better" without them.
* **Physical health:** Remember that STIs and unplanned pregnancy are still risks if you have sex without protection.

## Prioritise self-care
Focus on your own health rather than fixating on your ex. 
* **Physical health:** Eat well, get enough sleep, and stay active.
* **Avoid destructive habits:** Use this time to build healthy routines rather than turning to negative coping mechanisms.

## How long does it take?
There is no standard timeline for getting over a breakup. It depends on the individual, the nature of the relationship, and how it ended. 
* It may take a few months, or you may still think about them years later. 
* The key is that it does get easier over time. The more time you spend apart, the more you can focus on your own life.

## Seek support
Beat the loneliness by spending time with friends you trust.
* **Perspective:** Sharing your feelings can provide a helpful outside perspective.
* **Avoid gossip:** Resist the urge to badmouth your ex, and avoid airing your grievances publicly on social media.

## Embrace your feelings
Dealing with feelings of grief is challenging but necessary. 
* **Reality of heartbreak:** Heartbreak can be painful and confusing, sometimes even causing physical pain like a "pang" in the chest or stomach.
* **Numbness:** It is also normal to feel relieved or even numb. Sometimes the brain shuts down emotions when they become too overwhelming.
* **Creative outlets:** Activities like art, journaling, and sports can serve as therapeutic physical outlets for your feelings.

## Moving forward and growth
Breakups can propel you towards personal growth and new opportunities.
* **Find happiness:** Use your newfound freedom to find a new hobby or plan events with friends.
* **Avoid rebounds:** Be cautious about jumping into new sexual relationships just to feel good. Taking time to be on your own can be very beneficial.
* **Avoid drastic changes:** Try to avoid the "post-breakup haircut" or other drastic changes to your appearance. Focus on feeling like yourself rather than trying to manufacture a "new" version of yourself.
