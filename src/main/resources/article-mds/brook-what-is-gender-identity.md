# Gender Identity: What Is It?

Gender identity refers to how a person sees or feels about themselves. It describes the personal sense of feeling or knowing that you are a man, a woman, or a non-binary person. This identity can be the same as or different from the gender assigned to a person at birth.

Ultimately, gender identity is the set of feelings and beliefs a person has about their gender, regardless of their physical body or the sex assigned to them at birth.

## Sex and Gender

The word ‘sex’ refers to biological sexual characteristics, including external genitals, internal reproductive organs, chromosomes, and hormones. Although these factors contribute to someone’s sex, people are typically defined as male or female at birth based on the observation of external genitals.

While sex is biological, gender identity is personal and internal. Many people find labels helpful in understanding their experiences, while others prefer not to use labels at all.

## Understanding Identity Terms

### Cisgender
For most people, the sex they were assigned at birth is the same as their gender identity. This is called being ‘cisgender’ or ‘cis’. The term comes from a Latin word meaning ‘on this side of’.

### Transgender
When a person's sex assigned at birth does not match or sit easily with their gender identity, they may use the term ‘trans’ to describe themselves. ‘Trans’ is an umbrella term that describes many different ways of not belonging to the gender assigned at birth. Examples include:

* **Trans men:** People assigned female at birth who recognize themselves to be men.
* **Trans women:** People assigned male at birth who recognize themselves to be women.
* **Non-binary:** People who recognize their gender as being beyond or between the categories of man or woman.
* **Gender fluid:** People who experience different genders at different times.
* **Agender:** People who do not experience a sense of gender at all.

### Non-Binary and the Gender Binary
Non-binary is an umbrella term for gender identities that are not simply ‘man’ or ‘woman’. Non-binary people may feel their identity involves being both, neither, or something else entirely.

This exists in contrast to the **gender binary**, which is the conventional idea that everyone identifies as one of two genders (boy or girl). The word binary comes from Latin and means ‘a pair’ or ‘duality’.

## Gender Expression

Gender expression is how a person chooses to outwardly express aspects of their gender. This can include:

* Clothing and hairstyles
* Behavior and mannerisms
* Choice of name and pronouns

While society often associates certain appearances with specific genders, gender expression does not have to "match" gender identity. People may choose masculine, feminine, or neutral forms of expression regardless of how they identify.

## Misgendering

Misgendering is the act of referring to someone as the wrong gender or using the incorrect pronouns. While this is sometimes done by accident, it can also be done intentionally. Because a person’s individual identity is often very important to them, being misgendered can feel uncomfortable or distressing.

## Support and Inclusion

Understanding gender can sometimes feel complicated, and you may worry about getting things "wrong." A good starting point is to avoid making assumptions based on how people look and to strive for sensitivity and inclusion.

Being kind and respectful is the best approach. Any harassment or bullying of individuals based on how they identify or present—whether they do not conform to gender stereotypes or because of their gender identity—is wrong.

For further information, organizations such as Gendered Intelligence and Stonewall provide extensive resources regarding trans and non-binary identities.
