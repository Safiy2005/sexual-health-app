# What is Sexuality?

Sexuality describes how you express yourself in a sexual way. It is a broad term that encompasses your sexual behaviors, attractions, likes, dislikes, kinks, and preferences. 

Your sexuality can be expressed through your emotional and physical desires, behaviors, attitudes, and relationships. These feelings and experiences are often influenced by factors such as your friends, culture, and religion. It is important to remember that:
* **Sexuality is unique:** Your experience is personal to you.
* **Sexuality can be fluid:** It may change day to day or in different situations throughout your life.
* **Exploration takes time:** You do not need to understand everything immediately. You can explore your sexuality through your own feelings and behaviors, such as masturbation, without engaging in sexual activity with another person.

## Sexual Orientation vs. Sexuality

While "sexuality" refers to what you enjoy and how you enjoy it, **sexual orientation** refers specifically to who you are attracted to, want to have sex with, and fall in love with. Sexual orientation makes up a part of your overall sexuality, but they are not the same thing.

No one knows exactly what influences sexual orientation, but it is not something a person can choose or control. Common sexual orientations include:
* **Gay, Lesbian, or Queer:** People who are attracted to others of the same gender.
* **Straight (Heterosexual):** People who are attracted to people of a different gender (e.g., a man attracted to women or a woman attracted to men).
* **Bisexual:** People who are attracted to more than one gender.
* **Asexual:** People who do not experience sexual attraction to anyone.

## Understanding LGBT+

LGBT+ is an acronym for lesbian, gay, bisexual, and transgender. 
* The **‘+’** is used to be inclusive of other identities beyond those in the main acronym. 
* Sometimes it is written as **LGBTQ+**, where the ‘Q’ stands for ‘queer’ or ‘questioning.’

It is important to note that sexual activity is not always representative of sexual orientation. For example, some people may have sexual relations with someone of the same gender but may not identify as LGBT+.

## Coming Out

In a society where many people automatically assume everyone is straight (heterosexual) or cisgender, the process of "coming out" exists. Coming out is when a person tells someone else about their LGBT+ identity.

* **It is a personal choice:** Telling others about your sexual orientation is entirely up to you.
* **Different experiences:** For some, coming out is not an issue; for others, it can be very difficult.
* **Selective disclosure:** Some people choose to come out to friends but not to family, while others may choose not to come out to anyone at all.

Regardless of how you choose to identify or who you choose to tell, your sexual orientation is valid.
