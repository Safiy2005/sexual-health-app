# Living away from home: Advice for freshers

Starting university is a major life transition that brings both new opportunities and unique challenges. Staying healthy and safe during your first few months as a fresher is essential for a successful university experience.

## Sexual Health Tips

Practicing safer sex and managing your reproductive health is an important part of student life.

* **Practice safer sex:** Use condoms consistently. They are the only method of contraception that also protects against STIs. Many Students’ Unions provide them for free, or they can be purchased affordably.
* **Manage your contraception:** If you use the contraceptive pill, patch, or ring, ensure you have a sufficient supply or a new prescription before moving. This prevents you from running out during the busy first weeks of term.
* **Get tested regularly:** It is important to get tested for STIs every time you have a new sexual partner. Many infections, such as chlamydia and gonorrhoea, often show no symptoms.
* **Emergency contraception:** If you have had unprotected sex or your usual contraception has failed, do not delay seeking help. The effectiveness of emergency contraception depends on how quickly you access it.
* **Know where to go:** Familiarise yourself with the location of local sexual health services, walk-in centres, and A&E departments. You do not need to be registered with a clinic to use their services.

## Accessing Healthcare

Understanding how the NHS works while you are at university ensures you get the right care when you need it.

* **GP Registration:** While you can receive emergency care from any GP surgery, you should register with a local GP if you are living in student halls.
* **Temporary vs. Permanent Registration:** You can register as a temporary resident if you are staying for up to three months. If you are living in halls for longer, you must register as a permanent resident.
* **Sexual Health Clinics:** You do not need to register to visit a sexual health clinic. Many universities host their own dedicated clinics on campus.

## Sexual Assault, Abuse, and Harassment

Sexual violence includes any unwanted sexual contact, such as rape, non-consensual touching, online grooming, domestic abuse, and sexual exploitation.

If you experience sexual harassment in student halls or on campus, it is never your fault. Consider the following steps:
* **Ask them to stop:** If you feel safe and comfortable, tell the person their behaviour is unwanted and must stop.
* **Keep a record:** Note the date, time, and location of incidents, and keep any unwanted messages, emails, or texts.
* **Speak to someone:** Reach out to a trusted friend, family member, or professional service like Victim Support or Rape Crisis.
* **Report it:** You can report harassment to your university or college, which has a duty to investigate. In an emergency, always call 999.

## Mental Health and Wellbeing

Living in a new environment can be difficult. It is vital to prioritise your mental health and seek help if you are struggling. Most universities offer internal support services, such as counseling or student support groups.

Additional resources include:
* **Samaritans:** Confidential support available 24/7 for anyone going through a difficult time.
* **YoungMinds Crisis Messenger:** A free 24/7 text service for those experiencing a mental health crisis.
* **On My Mind (Anna Freud Centre):** A directory of mental health services and self-care ideas.
* **Child Bereavement UK:** Support for young people experiencing the loss of a loved one.

## Physical Health and Vaccinations

The combination of close living quarters, socialising, and academic stress often leads to the spread of illnesses, commonly known as "freshers' flu."

* **General Health:** Stay hydrated, eat a balanced diet, and try to get enough sleep and exercise to support your immune system.
* **Meningitis Awareness:** It is crucial to recognise the symptoms of meningitis, which can include a stiff neck, seizures, vomiting, light sensitivity, drowsiness, or a rash. If you suspect meningitis, call 999 immediately.
* **Vaccinations:** Before starting university, ensure you are up to date with:
 * **MenACWY vaccine:** Protects against meningitis and septicaemia.
 * **MMR vaccine:** Two doses protect against measles, mumps, and rubella.
