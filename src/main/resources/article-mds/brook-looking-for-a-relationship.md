# Looking for a Relationship

Before you start looking for a relationship, it is important to take a minute to ensure it is really what you want and that the timing is right. Here are several things to bear in mind if you are looking for a relationship or want to ask someone out.

## Are You Ready for a Relationship?

There is no "normal" or "right" age to have your first relationship. Not having a relationship can be a perfectly good choice at any age. Before diving in, consider the following:

* **Why do you want a relationship?** If you are looking for one just because friends are in relationships, you feel like the odd one out, or you have free time you don’t know how to fill, these may not be the best reasons. You might find better fulfillment by meeting new people, making new friends, or trying new hobbies.
* **Do you have the time and effort?** If you are "emotionally unavailable"—meaning you are unable to offer time and emotion to another person—it is very difficult to build a healthy connection.
* **Do you know what you’re looking for?** You don’t need an exact checklist, but being honest about whether you want something serious or casual helps ensure you and potential partners are on the same page.
* **Do you know your worth?** Your self-esteem affects who you date. If you don't feel good about yourself, you may put up with unacceptable behavior or become overly dependent on a partner for validation. A partner should complement your life, not fill a void.

## Where to Meet People

There are many ways to meet potential partners, each with its own benefits and considerations:

* **Through friends:** Meeting via mutual friends often feels safer and more comfortable because there is a pre-existing level of trust and shared social circles.
* **Groups and clubs:** Joining a sports club or starting a new hobby is an excellent way to meet people with shared interests.
* **Dating apps (Over 18s only):** While apps are a common way to meet people, they carry risks. Always prioritize your safety when meeting someone you don't know in person.
* **Social media:** Starting conversations via direct messages (DMs) can be less intimidating, but the same safety rules for online dating apply.

## Identifying a Healthy Relationship (Green Flags)

Everyone has different ideas of an ideal partner, but healthy relationships share key characteristics often called "green flags." Look for someone who:

* Makes you feel good about yourself
* Respects your boundaries
* Is a good communicator
* Listens and responds to how you want to be treated
* Brings out a version of yourself that you like
* Asks questions and is interested in getting to know you
* Makes you feel safe and comfortable
* Likes you for who you are and doesn't try to change you
* Shows you respect and compassion

## Asking Someone Out

Before asking someone out, try to gauge if they are interested. Look for signs of "fancying" you back or ask mutual friends. If they show clear signs of not being interested, it is best to respect that.

If you decide to ask them:
* **Offer your number:** Giving them your number rather than asking for theirs gives them the choice to message you without pressure.
* **Be prepared for "no":** Respond kindly and move on. Praise yourself for being brave.
* **Recognize "pressure yes":** Sometimes people say yes because they feel awkward saying no. Give them space and understand they may change their mind.
* **Respect their privacy:** They do not owe you a reason for why they don't want to go out with you.

## The First Kiss and Consent

The first kiss should only happen when both people are comfortable and have given consent. Consent means a clear agreement without pressure.

* **Ask for permission:** Simple phrases like "I’d really like to kiss you" or "Can I kiss you?" are effective.
* **Read body language:** If they are leaning away or have their arms crossed, they likely do not want to be kissed. During a kiss, check if they are responding and leaning in.
* **Start slow:** You don't have to get it perfect immediately. Pay attention to how the other person responds and adjust accordingly.
* **Talk about it:** Discussing preferences and boundaries beforehand can make the experience better for both of you.

## When Things Don't Work Out

Relationships require effort, communication, and commitment, but they don't always last.

* **Things fizzle out:** Sometimes your mental image of someone doesn't match their actual personality. It is better to move on than to settle for a relationship that doesn't make you happy.
* **The "Friendzone":** You or the other person may decide that you are better off as friends. If you still have romantic feelings, it is okay to take space or set boundaries to protect your own wellbeing.
* **Breakups:** People grow and change, and sometimes they grow in different directions. While upsetting, a breakup is not the end of the world, and you will have other opportunities for relationships in the future.
