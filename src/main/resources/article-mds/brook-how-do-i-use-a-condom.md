# How to use a condom in 5 simple steps

Using a condom correctly is essential to maximise its effectiveness in preventing unplanned pregnancy and STIs. While putting on a condom can feel a bit awkward at first, it becomes easier with practice. You may find it helpful to practice on your own in private so that you feel comfortable before using one during sex.

## Before you start: 4 things to check
Before opening the condom packet, check for the following:
* **Expiry date:** If the condom has expired, the material may be weakened and might not work properly.
* **Rips or holes:** If the packaging is damaged, the condom itself may be compromised.
* **European CE mark or the UKCA mark:** These marks indicate that the condom has been tested to high safety standards.
* **Size:** Ensure you have the correct size for the penis or toy. If it is too large, it may fall off; if it is too small, it may tear.

## Step 1: Open the packet carefully
Before opening, feel for the "rib" (the rolled ring) of the condom inside the packaging. Push this to one side so that you do not tear the condom when opening the packet. Never use scissors or your teeth to open the packet, as sharp edges can easily damage the latex.

## Step 2: Check the direction
Check which way the condom rolls down before it touches the penis. Unroll it slightly to ensure it is the right way round. If you accidentally place a condom on the penis the wrong way round, discard it, get a new one, and start again.

## Step 3: Put the condom on
Pinch the tip of the condom between your thumb and forefinger to squeeze out any trapped air. This leaves space for the semen. Place the condom on the tip of the erect penis. Using your other hand, roll the condom all the way down to the base. Once it is on, gently slide your fingers along the side to smooth out any air bubbles.

## Step 4: During sex
The condom should be put on as soon as the penis is erect and before it comes into contact with anyone’s mouth, vagina, or anus. This helps protect against both pregnancy and STIs.

### Using lube with condoms
* **Water-based lube:** If you are having anal sex or want extra lubrication, use water-based or silicone-based lube.
* **Avoid oil-based products:** Never use products like Vaseline, baby oil, or coconut oil with latex condoms, as they cause the material to break down and tear.
* **Avoid spermicide:** Avoid condoms lubricated with spermicides like nonoxynol-9, as they do not protect against STIs and may increase the risk of infection by irritating the skin.

## Step 5: After sex
After ejaculation, the penis should be withdrawn while it is still erect. Hold the condom firmly at the base against the penis as you pull out to ensure it does not slip off. 

Once withdrawn, take the condom off away from your partner. Wrap the used condom in a tissue and throw it in the bin. Do not flush condoms down the toilet.

## Accessing free condoms
The **C-Card scheme** allows young people to collect free condoms and lube from local outlets, including pharmacies, youth services, and some shops. You can find your local C-Card provider through the Brook website or local health services.
