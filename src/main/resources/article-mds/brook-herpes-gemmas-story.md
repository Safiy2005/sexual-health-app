# Herpes: Gemma’s Story

Gemma, 19, shares her experience of contracting and managing genital herpes during her first year of college. Her story highlights how the virus is transmitted, the reality of the symptoms, and the importance of seeking professional medical help.

## Transmission and Initial Symptoms

Despite consistently practicing safe sex to prevent pregnancy, Gemma contracted genital herpes through oral sex. Her partner at the time had a cold sore, which they both mistakenly assumed was a regular spot.

A few days after the encounter, Gemma developed a rash between her legs. While it initially caused mild stinging, the condition progressed rapidly:
* The rash turned into blisters.
* The blisters were extremely painful, which Gemma compared to the sensation of "cigarette burns."
* Movement and urination became agonizingly difficult.

## Seeking Diagnosis

Due to the severity of the pain and discomfort, Gemma visited a sexual health clinic. Although she was nervous about the visit and the potential cost of transport, she opted for a full STI screening to be thorough.

Upon examination, clinicians confirmed a diagnosis of genital herpes. They noted it was a particularly severe initial outbreak. Gemma chose to complete all necessary tests and examinations in one visit to ensure she received immediate care.

## Treatment and Management

The clinic provided a combination of medical treatment and practical self-care advice:
* **Antivirals:** Prescribed to help manage the viral infection.
* **Pain Relief:** Standard over-the-counter painkillers were recommended.
* **Salt Water Baths:** Bathing in salt water was suggested to soothe the area.
* **Urination Tip:** Pouring salt water over the area while urinating helped dilute the urine, significantly reducing the stinging sensation.

## Living with Herpes and Recurrences

Gemma experienced a few subsequent outbreaks. She learned to identify the "tingling" sensation that precedes an outbreak, allowing her to prepare. While the second outbreak was still difficult, subsequent occurrences became increasingly less painful and easier to manage.

While Gemma notes that she felt some embarrassment due to the social stigma surrounding herpes, she emphasizes that being open with her partners and being extra cautious about her sexual health became her new priority.

## Advice for Others

Gemma encourages anyone who suspects they might have an STI to seek testing immediately:
* **Don't delay:** Professional diagnosis is essential for proper treatment.
* **Clinic staff are supportive:** Despite any nerves or embarrassment, sexual health clinic staff are professional, non-judgmental, and there to help.
* **Manage the pain:** Practical tips, like using salt water, can be "lifesavers" during painful outbreaks.
