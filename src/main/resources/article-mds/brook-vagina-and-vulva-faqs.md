# Vagina and Vulva FAQs

Common questions and answers about vaginas and vulvas. This content was developed in partnership with Canesten.

## What is the vulva?
The vulva is often mistakenly referred to as the vagina. 
* **The vagina** is the muscular tube inside your body. You cannot see it, but it is where you might insert tampons during your period. 
* **The vulva** is the visible, external part of your genitals.

## How do I know if my vulva is normal?
Vulvas come in many different shapes, sizes, and colours. There is no single "normal" look; diversity is natural.

## Do I need to see a doctor about my vulva?
It is recommended that you see a doctor if you notice changes or experience symptoms such as:
* Itchiness
* Dryness
* Abnormal discharge
* Soreness in the vagina or vulva

## Why do I have discharge?
Discharge is the vagina’s natural way of cleaning itself. Healthy discharge is normal and can vary depending on your menstrual cycle:
* It can be thick, watery, white, pink, or brown.
* It may have a faint smell.
* **When to seek help:** If discharge causes itching, has an unpleasant smell, or has a texture similar to cottage cheese, you should speak to a doctor or nurse.

## Can I get surgery if I am unhappy with the way my vulva looks?

## Penetrative sex hurts. Is there something wrong with me?
Pain during sex can happen for several reasons, including:
* Lack of lubrication
* Sexual position
* Emotional factors or stress
* Infections
If sex is painful, stop or try alternatives like using lubricant or changing positions. If pain is consistent, consult a medical professional.

## Should I remove my pubic hair?
The choice to trim, remove, or keep pubic hair is entirely personal. However, pubic hair serves a biological purpose:
* It provides protection from bacteria.
* It reduces friction during penetrative sex.
* **Note:** Removing hair can sometimes lead to soreness or ingrown hairs.

## Is it a bad sign if my vulva smells bad?
Every vulva has its own natural aroma. However, certain smells can indicate an issue:
* **Thin, grey-white discharge with a fishy smell:** May indicate bacterial vaginosis (BV).
* **Yellowy-green frothy discharge with a fishy smell:** Suggests trichomoniasis (an STI).
* **Strong, unpleasant smell after sex:** Can suggest vaginitis.

## Can tight trousers cause vulva infections?
Wearing tight, restrictive, or synthetic clothing does not cause infections directly, but it can irritate the vulva and make existing symptoms worse.

## Why does it burn when I wee?
A burning sensation or pain during urination could indicate:
* **Cystitis:** Inflammation of the bladder.
* **Vaginitis:** Inflammation of the vagina.
* **Urethritis:** Inflammation of the urethra.
* **STIs:** Such as chlamydia, gonorrhoea, or trichomoniasis.

## Why am I suffering from vaginal dryness?
Vaginal dryness can be caused by:
* Certain types of contraception.
* Medications, such as antidepressants.
* Using perfumed products around the vagina.
* Lack of arousal during sex.
* Menopause (typically occurring between ages 44 and 56).

## Why is my vulva and/or vagina itchy?
Itching is a common symptom of **thrush**, a yeast infection caused by a fungus that lives naturally in the body. Thrush often involves a white, cottage-cheese-like discharge. Itching can also be a symptom of various STIs, so testing is important.

## I have had unprotected sex but have no symptoms. Do I need to get tested?
Yes. Many STIs do not show symptoms. It is important to get tested for peace of mind and to prevent long-term health issues like infertility. Testing is quick, easy, and usually painless.
