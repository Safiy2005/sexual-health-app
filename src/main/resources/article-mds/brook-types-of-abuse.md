# Types of Abuse

Abuse can be verbal, emotional, physical, financial, or sexual. It can also be less obvious and take the form of controlling behaviour. It is important to recognize that some people may use a combination of these types or progress from one type to another—for example, starting with emotional abuse before leading to physical violence.

Abuse can happen in any type of relationship and is not limited to romantic partners. It can occur face-to-face or online, and within a relationship or a household.

## Verbal Abuse
Verbal abuse occurs when someone uses language to harm or intimidate you. Signs of verbal abuse include:
* Threatening you.
* Calling you nasty names.
* Shouting at you constantly.
* Saying cruel and hurtful things to make you feel bad about yourself.

## Emotional Abuse
Emotional abuse involves someone using their power to manipulate and control you. This often results in you feeling scared to do something in case it upsets them. Key characteristics include:
* Constantly checking on you or demanding to know your location at all times.
* Criticising your choices, such as what you wear.
* Making you feel worthless to diminish your strength to leave the situation.
* Making excuses for their behaviour, often claiming they act this way out of "love" or "concern."

## Coercive and Controlling Behaviour
Coercive and controlling behaviour is illegal under the Serious Crime Act 2015 (England and Wales). 

### Controlling Behaviour
Controlling behaviour includes acts designed to make a person subordinate, inferior, or dependent on the abuser. This can include:
* Isolating the person from sources of support, such as family and friends.
* Preventing independence, such as not allowing them to get a job.
* Controlling their money.
* Monitoring daily behaviour, including where they go or who they message.

### Coercive Behaviour
Coercive behaviour involves the use of assault, threats, humiliation, and intimidation to harm, punish, or frighten the victim. This creates a constant state of fear that limits the victim's freedom and ability to escape.

## Physical Abuse
Physical abuse occurs when someone physically hurts you in any way. Examples include:
* Hitting, slapping, or punching.
* Female genital mutilation (FGM).

## Sexual Abuse
Sexual abuse is when someone forces you into sexual activity you do not want or threatens you if you do not comply. 
* Sexual contact includes unwanted touching, oral sex, vaginal sex, and anal sex. 
* Consent is vital: even if you have consented to sexual activity in the past, it does not give another person the right to touch you without your current consent. 
* You never have to do anything sexual that you do not want to do.

## Financial Abuse
Financial abuse refers to the control of another person through money. This may include:
* Withholding money or telling someone what they can and cannot buy.
* Buying the victim items to make them feel they "owe" the abuser.
* Forcing the victim into sexual activity or illegal acts (such as robbery or selling drugs) for financial reasons.

## Getting Help
If you have experienced any of these types of abuse, are worried that you might be at risk, or are worried about someone else, it is important to seek support and find resources on how to get help.
