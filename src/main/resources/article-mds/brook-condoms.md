# Condoms

Condoms are made of very thin latex (rubber) and are designed to cover the penis in order to stop fluids being transferred between partners. They are the only method of contraception that protects against both pregnancy and sexually transmitted infections (STIs).

## Key Facts

* **Effectiveness:** More than 98% effective if used correctly without mistakes.
* **Hormones:** No hormones involved.
* **Mood/Emotions:** No effect on mood.
* **STIs:** Protects against STIs.
* **Visibility:** Only people involved in the sexual activity will see it.
* **Periods:** No effect; periods will stay the same.
* **Duration:** Single use only.
* **Acne/Skin:** No effect; skin will stay the same.

## How Condoms Work

Condoms protect against unwanted pregnancy by stopping the sperm contained in semen from coming into contact with the vagina. An external condom covers the penis and acts as a barrier between it and the mouth, vagina, or anus. This prevents sexual fluids from being transferred between partners, providing protection against STIs.

If you are allergic to latex, latex-free (polyurethane) condoms are available. Biodegradable condoms are also available for those concerned about single-use plastics.

## Pros and Cons

### Pros
* The only method that protects against both pregnancy and STIs.
* Only need to be used during sexual activity.
* No serious side effects.
* Available in different shapes, sizes, textures, colours, and flavours.
* Easily accessible.
* Can be used in addition to other methods (e.g., the pill, implant, or injection) for extra protection.
* Can be used on sex toys.

### Cons
* They can occasionally split or slip off.
* Some people are allergic to latex (though latex-free options exist).
* Some people find talking about condoms awkward.
* They are single-use only.

## Where to Get Condoms

You can get condoms for free from:
* Brook services and young people’s services.
* Contraception clinics and GUM clinics.
* Some GP surgeries.
* The **C-Card scheme**, which allows young people to pick up free condoms from local outlets like pharmacies, youth services, and shops.

You can also buy condoms from pharmacies, supermarkets, vending machines in public toilets, and online. There is **no age restriction** on buying or accessing free condoms.

## How to Use a Condom Correctly

When used correctly, condoms are 98% effective. Follow these steps:

1. **Check the package:** Feel for the air bubble/rib of the condom and push it to the side so you don't tear it while opening.
2. **Check the direction:** Unroll the condom slightly to ensure it is the right way round before it touches the penis.
3. **Pinch the tip:** Pinch the tip between your thumb and forefinger to remove any trapped air.
4. **Apply:** Put it on the penis as soon as it is erect (hard) or on a sex toy before it comes into contact with a partner.
5. **Roll down:** Roll the condom all the way to the base of the penis or sex toy.
6. **Use lubricant:** If having anal sex, use additional water-based lubricant on the outside of the condom.
7. **Check during sex:** Ensure the condom stays in place.
8. **Withdrawal:** After ejaculation, hold the condom at the base while withdrawing the penis.
9. **Disposal:** Take the condom off, wrap it in tissue, and throw it in the bin (do not flush it).

## Dos and Don’ts

### DO
* Check the expiry date.
* Look for the European CE mark or the UKCA mark on the packet.
* Use a brand new condom every time you have sex (including when moving between anal and vaginal sex).
* Use water-based lubricants (like KY Jelly).
* Be careful with sharp nails or jewellery.

### DON'T
* Don't use a condom if the pack is torn or damaged.
* Don't use two condoms at the same time (this increases friction and causes tearing).
* Don't use oil-based products (like Vaseline or hand cream) with latex condoms, as they damage the material.
* Don't reuse a condom if you accidentally tried putting it on the wrong way; throw it away and start again.

## Accidents and Emergencies

If a condom breaks, slips off, or is used incorrectly:
* **Emergency Contraception:** You may need emergency contraception if there is a risk of pregnancy.
* **STI Testing:** Visit a sexual health clinic for a quick and painless test.
* **Support:** Sexual health services are non-judgmental and are there to help you enjoy sex safely.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I use condoms and internal condoms together?
No. Using two barrier methods together increases the likelihood of them splitting. You can, however, use condoms alongside non-barrier methods like the pill or implant.

### How do I find the right fit?
If a condom won't go all the way down or feels uncomfortable, you may need a different size. Condoms come in many sizes and textures; trialling different types can help you find the best fit.

### How long do they last?
Always check the expiry date on the individual wrapper. Replace any condoms that have been kept in a wallet for over a month, as friction and heat can degrade the material.
