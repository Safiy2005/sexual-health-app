# Premature ejaculation – coming too soon

Premature ejaculation is when a person with a penis ejaculates, or ‘comes’, before they or their partner would like. When talking about ejaculation in relation to sex, people are generally referring to when semen (usually containing sperm) is discharged from the penis as the result of an orgasm.

Most people with penises will experience premature ejaculation at some point in their life, and this is nothing to be ashamed of or worried about. However, if you find it is happening frequently and you or your partner are unhappy about it, you may want to visit your GP or a sexual health clinic for help and support.

## Is it only people with penises who ejaculate?

People with vulvas can also ejaculate, though it’s not something that everyone with a vulva will necessarily experience and it isn't always linked to an orgasm. This is commonly referred to as “female ejaculation” or “squirting”.

## Redefining the end of sex

Premature ejaculation is often considered a problem because people think it signals the end of sex. However, sex is not just about penetration or orgasms. There are no rules that say sex must finish once a person with a penis has ejaculated; there are many other activities you can still enjoy.

The question of how long sex should last depends on what each person wants. Instead of focusing on time, try to focus on pleasure and enjoyment. Sex should last as long as everyone involved is happy, comfortable, and having a good time.

## Ejaculation and porn

Porn is generally not representative of real sexual experiences or typical penis function. It often gives the impression that people can last a long time before orgasming. In reality, most porn actors are not engaging in sexual activity for long periods of time. Like all film productions, there are breaks, re-takes, and clever editing tricks.

Furthermore, porn actors are working. People tend to reach orgasm faster when they are turned on, but the set of a porn shoot—with lights, a production crew, and pressure to perform—might make it difficult for actors to feel naturally aroused. It is not an intimate moment between people who are physically or emotionally attracted to each other.

## Techniques to delay ejaculation

If you want to delay ejaculation, there are several techniques you can try:

### Knowing your body: The stop-start technique
An important part of delaying ejaculation is recognising the signs that you are about to come. You can use the ‘stop-start technique’ to help identify these feelings:

1. Start by stimulating your penis (either alone or with a partner).
2. Focus on how your body and your penis feel.
3. When you become very aroused, stop all stimulation.
4. When your arousal level has subsided, begin stimulating your penis again.
5. Repeat this method a couple of times, and on the final time, continue until you come.

### Reduce anxiety, worry, and pressure
Feeling worried, anxious, or pressured to please another person can make it harder to control ejaculation. Remember that there are many ways to provide pleasure that don’t involve penetration. Sex should be enjoyable for everyone involved, and you should never feel forced to do anything that makes you uncomfortable.

### Slow down
Many people speed up sex as it becomes more enjoyable, which can lead to a quicker orgasm. If you feel you are getting close and want to last longer, try slowing down the pace.

### Switch activities
If you feel you are close to ejaculation, try changing what you are doing. If you are having penetrative sex, you could change positions or withdraw and focus on other things, such as oral sex or kissing.

### Breathing
Taking deep breaths can help shut down the reflex that creates ejaculation.

### The squeeze technique
Either you or your partner can gently but firmly squeeze the penis just under the head using the thumb and forefinger (avoid squeezing so hard that it hurts). This decreases the sensation and can help delay ejaculation.

### Use a thicker condom
Using a thicker condom can decrease sensation and help delay ejaculation. Some condoms also contain special lubricant with a numbing agent. Always ensure that neither you nor your partner are sensitive to the ingredients.

## Seeking help and support

If you want further help or support with premature ejaculation, you can visit a sexual health service such as Brook or your GP. Condoms are also available for free or at low cost from pharmacies, supermarkets, and sexual health clinics, and they remain the only method of contraception that also protects against STIs.
