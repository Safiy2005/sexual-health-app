# HIV

HIV stands for Human Immunodeficiency Virus. It is a virus that attacks the immune system and, if left untreated, can lead to AIDS (Acquired Immune Deficiency Syndrome). With effective treatment, most people living with HIV can lead long and healthy lives.

---

## What is HIV?

HIV attacks and weakens the immune system, making it harder for the body to fight off infections and diseases. The virus targets CD4 cells, a type of white blood cell that plays a crucial role in the immune response.

### HIV vs AIDS

AIDS is the most advanced stage of HIV infection. It develops when the immune system is severely damaged and can no longer fight off infections. With modern treatment, many people with HIV never develop AIDS.

### Living with HIV

Thanks to advances in treatment, people with HIV who take their medication as prescribed can have an undetectable viral load. This means the level of virus in their blood is so low it cannot be detected by standard tests, and they cannot pass HIV on to sexual partners.

---

## Signs and Symptoms

Many people with HIV do not experience symptoms for years after infection. However, some people may experience symptoms within 2-6 weeks of infection.

### Early Symptoms

Early symptoms of HIV infection can include:

- Flu-like symptoms
- Fever and high temperature
- Sore throat
- Body rash
- Tiredness and fatigue
- Joint pain and muscle aches
- Swollen glands (lymph nodes)

### Why Testing is Important

These symptoms are common to many illnesses, so the only way to know if you have HIV is to get tested. Many people with HIV have no symptoms at all, which is why regular testing is so important if you are sexually active.

---

## How HIV is Transmitted

HIV is passed on through infected blood, semen, vaginal fluids, rectal secretions and breast milk.

### Common Ways HIV is Transmitted

- Having unprotected vaginal, anal or oral sex with someone who has HIV
- Sharing needles or syringes with someone who has HIV
- From mother to child during pregnancy, childbirth or breastfeeding (without treatment)

### How HIV is NOT Spread

HIV cannot be transmitted through:

- Kissing, hugging or shaking hands
- Sharing food, drinks or utensils
- Toilet seats
- Mosquito bites
- Coughing or sneezing
- Sweat or tears

---

## Who is at Risk?

Anyone who is sexually active or shares needles can be at risk of HIV. However, certain groups have higher rates of HIV.

### Higher Risk Groups

- Men who have sex with men
- People who inject drugs
- People from countries with high HIV prevalence
- Sexual partners of people in high-risk groups

### Reducing Your Risk

You can reduce your risk of HIV by:

- Using condoms correctly every time you have sex
- Getting tested regularly for HIV and other STIs
- Taking PrEP (pre-exposure prophylaxis) if you are at high risk
- Never sharing needles or injecting equipment
- Using PEP (post-exposure prophylaxis) if you think you may have been exposed

---

## Testing for HIV

Getting tested for HIV is the only way to know if you have the virus. Testing is quick, easy and confidential.

### Types of HIV Tests

- **Rapid tests** – These can give results in minutes using a finger-prick blood sample or oral swab
- **Laboratory tests** – Blood samples are sent to a lab for analysis and can detect HIV earlier than rapid tests
- **Self-testing kits** – You can order these online or buy from pharmacies to test yourself at home

### Window Period

The window period is the time between potential exposure to HIV and when a test can accurately detect the virus. This can be up to 45 days for some tests, though some newer tests can detect HIV earlier. If you test negative during the window period, you should test again after the window period has passed.

### Where to Get Tested

You can get tested for HIV at:

- Sexual health clinics (GUM clinics)
- GP surgeries
- Some pharmacies
- Community testing services
- At home using a self-test kit

---

## Treatment for HIV

There is no cure for HIV, but there are very effective treatments that enable most people living with HIV to live long and healthy lives.

### Antiretroviral Treatment (ART)

HIV is treated with antiretroviral medicines. These work by stopping the virus from replicating in the body. Treatment usually involves taking tablets every day.

### Undetectable = Untransmittable (U=U)

When someone with HIV takes their medication as prescribed and achieves an undetectable viral load, they cannot pass HIV on to their sexual partners. This is known as U=U (Undetectable = Untransmittable).

### Starting Treatment Early

It is recommended that people start HIV treatment as soon as possible after diagnosis. Starting treatment early helps to protect the immune system and reduces the risk of passing HIV on to others.

---

## Living Well with HIV

With the right treatment and support, people living with HIV can lead full and healthy lives.

### Regular Monitoring

People living with HIV will have regular check-ups to monitor their viral load and CD4 count. This helps to ensure the treatment is working effectively.

### Support Available

Being diagnosed with HIV can be a lot to take in. There is lots of support available, including:

- HIV support groups and charities
- Counselling services
- Sexual health advisers
- Online communities and resources

### Telling Partners

If you are diagnosed with HIV, it is important to tell your current and recent sexual partners so they can get tested too. Your sexual health clinic can help you with this through partner notification services.

---

## Prevention

There are several ways to prevent HIV transmission.

### Condoms

Using condoms correctly every time you have vaginal, anal or oral sex is one of the most effective ways to prevent HIV and other STIs.

### PrEP

PrEP (pre-exposure prophylaxis) is a medication that can be taken by people who do not have HIV to reduce their risk of getting it. When taken as prescribed, PrEP is highly effective at preventing HIV.

### PEP

PEP (post-exposure prophylaxis) is an emergency treatment that can be taken within 72 hours of potential exposure to HIV. It is not 100% effective but can significantly reduce the risk of infection if taken quickly.

### Regular Testing

Getting tested regularly for HIV and other STIs means you can get treatment early if needed and avoid passing infections on to others.
