# Miscarriage at 7 weeks: Holly-mae's story

At the age of 18, Holly-mae experienced a miscarriage seven weeks into her pregnancy. Sharing her story through the Miscarriage Association, she discusses the emotional challenges of loss, the impact on her relationships, and how she is coping with feelings of guilt.

## The Discovery of Loss

Holly-mae first suspected something was wrong when she went for an emergency scan at 11 weeks. Although she should have been further along, the scan confirmed that the pregnancy had ended several weeks prior.

* **The Scan:** Upon seeing the screen, Holly-mae realized the embryo was not developing as expected.
* **The Timeline:** Medical confirmation showed that the pregnancy had stopped at approximately 7 weeks.
* **Physical Intuition:** Reflecting on the weeks leading up to the scan, Holly-mae noted that she had stopped "feeling pregnant," suggesting an internal awareness that something had changed.

## The Emotional Impact

The aftermath of a miscarriage involves a complex grieving process that is often non-linear. Holly-mae describes a range of emotional states:

* **Fluctuating Grief:** She experiences "good days" where the loss is only a secondary thought, and "bad days" characterized by persistent crying and a sense that time has stopped.
* **Social Isolation:** A feeling that the rest of the world is moving forward while she remains stuck in her grief.
* **Unmet Expectations:** The loss represented the disappearance of a planned future and the roles she had envisioned for herself and her family.

## Relationships and Guilt

The experience of miscarriage was compounded by difficulties in Holly-mae's personal life and the reactions of those around her.

* **Lack of Support:** Following the miscarriage, Holly-mae’s partner at the time left her, placing the blame for the loss on her.
* **Self-Blame:** At 18, Holly-mae struggled with the feeling that her body had failed her. She felt guilty that her family members would not be able to fulfill their new roles as grandparents or aunts.
* **Misunderstood Comfort:** She noted that while people tried to be supportive by saying it was a "lucky escape" due to her age or relationship status, such comments offered no consolation for the loss of a wanted pregnancy.

## Finding Hope and Support

Holly-mae emphasizes the importance of moving past self-imposed guilt to avoid further emotional destruction.

* **Holding onto Hope:** She believes that there are brighter times ahead and encourages others in similar situations to maintain hope.
* **Seeking Help:** Holly-mae shared her story in collaboration with The Miscarriage Association. 
* **External Resources:** Young people looking for support, information, or advice regarding pregnancy loss can find resources through The Miscarriage Association and Brook.
