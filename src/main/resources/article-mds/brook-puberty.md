# Puberty

As you get older, your body goes through a number of changes called puberty. Between the ages of 8 and 18, most people will go through this stage of development. During this time, you may experience a number of physical and emotional changes, though exactly what these are and when they happen will vary from person to person.

Puberty is when a person’s body starts to change physically and mentally from that of a child to an adult. These changes prepare our bodies to be able to reproduce (create babies). There is no "normal" when it comes to puberty; everyone experiences it differently. Some might start young, some later, and for some, the process may stop and start with gaps in between.

## Understanding Sex and Gender

Puberty affects people differently depending on their biological sex. Sex is determined by chromosomes and reproductive organs. 
* **Female:** Usually assigned to babies born with a vulva and a vagina.
* **Male:** Usually assigned to babies born with a penis and testicles.
* **Intersex:** People whose external body parts, chromosomes, or reproductive organs do not fit typical binary definitions of male or female.

It is important to remember that not everyone’s biological sex will be the same as their gender identity. People who identify with a gender different from their biological sex are known as transgender.

## What are Hormones?

Hormones are chemicals produced naturally within the body and released into the bloodstream to send messages to other parts of the body. During puberty, sex hormones trigger physical changes:
* **Testosterone:** The "male" hormone, mostly produced in the testes. It is usually higher in people with penises and testicles.
* **Oestrogen:** The "female" hormone, mostly produced in the ovaries. It is usually higher in people with vaginas and vulvas.

Most people have at least a small amount of both hormones in their bodies.

## General Changes for Everyone

Most people will experience the following changes regardless of their sex:
* Growing taller (growth spurts).
* Growth of body hair on legs, armpits, genitals, and sometimes the face.
* More active sweat glands under the arms and between the legs.
* Spots on the face and body (acne).
* Greasier hair.
* A deepening voice.
* Increased nipple sensitivity.
* Muscle development in the arms, legs, chest, and stomach.

## Hygiene and Self-Care

Because of hormonal changes, you may sweat more and your skin and hair may become greasier. It is important to stay clean and wash regularly with soap and water. Many people also choose to use deodorant to manage body odor.

## Puberty and Mental Health

Puberty is a significant transition between childhood and adulthood. While physical changes are often the focus, the emotional impact is just as important. The hormonal shifts can lead to significant emotional changes, and it is vital to prioritize mental health and emotional wellbeing during this time.

## Changes for People with a Penis and Testicles

For those with a penis and testicles, testosterone levels rise significantly between the ages of 10 and 18. Specific changes include:
* **Genitals:** The penis and testicles grow larger and begin to produce semen.
* **Erections:** The penis may become hard when sexually excited, or sometimes for no apparent reason.
* **Wet Dreams:** Ejaculating (releasing semen) while asleep.
* **Breast Tissue Growth:** It is common for oestrogen levels to fluctuate, which can cause temporary breast tissue growth or tenderness in the chest.
* **Muscle Development:** The chest develops more muscle (pectoral muscles).
* **Hair Growth:** Hair typically grows on the face, chest, armpits, legs, and around the genitals.

## Changes for People with a Vulva and Vagina

For those with a vulva and vagina, oestrogen levels rise significantly between the ages of 8 and 18. Specific changes include:
* **Vaginal Discharge:** A clear or milky fluid that keeps the vagina healthy. If it changes color, smells bad, or causes itching, it may indicate an infection.
* **Periods (Menstruation):** The monthly release of unneeded blood and tissue from the womb. Periods usually happen every four to five weeks and can be irregular when they first start.
* **Breast Development:** Nipples and breasts will begin to swell and fill out. It is common for breasts to grow at different speeds or be different sizes.
* **Hair Growth:** Hair grows on the armpits, legs, and around the genitals. Some may also notice hair around the nipples, under the belly button, or on the face.

## Everyone is Different

Puberty can be overwhelming, especially if you feel you are changing at a different rate than your friends. There is no single "normal" experience. If you have concerns about the changes happening to your body, you can talk to a trusted adult, a doctor, or a professional healthcare service.
