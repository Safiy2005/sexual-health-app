# Breasts and Chests

Breasts and chests come in all shapes, sizes, and colors. It is common to worry about whether yours are "normal," but everyone has breast tissue regardless of their gender or sex assigned at birth. This tissue presents differently depending on the dominant sex hormones in your body.

Brook teamed up with CoppaFeel! to provide information to help you get to know your body and understand what is normal for you.

## Understanding the Terminology

While the way people talk about their bodies is personal and often relates to their gender identity, this guide uses the following definitions:

* **Breasts:** Refers to the muscles, fat, and breast tissue of people whose dominant sex hormone is oestrogen.
* **Chests:** Refers to the muscles, fat, and breast tissue of people whose dominant sex hormone is testosterone, or used more generally to describe the chest area.

## Puberty and Hormones

Most people notice changes in their chest between the ages of 10 and 14, though it can happen earlier or later. During puberty, sex hormones are released, causing physical changes:

* **Oestrogen:** Often called the "female" hormone, produced mainly in the ovaries.
* **Testosterone:** Often called the "male" hormone, produced mainly in the testes.
* **Hormone Balance:** Most people have at least a small amount of both hormones.
* **Intersex Variations:** Some people have variations in sex characteristics that do not align with traditional male/female categories.
* **Gender Transition:** Some people may undergo hormone therapy as part of their transition, which will affect how their chest or breasts develop.

## Breast Development

For those whose bodies produce mostly oestrogen, it can take up to five years for breasts to become fully grown. They may continue to change shape and size throughout a person's life.

Common experiences include:
* One breast developing faster than the other.
* Breasts continuing to develop until age 18 or beyond.
* Asymmetry (one breast being larger than the other).
* Changes related to the menstrual cycle, such as sudden growth, swelling, or pain before a period.
* A wide variety of shapes: breasts may be far apart, close together, pointy, or round.

If pregnancy occurs, breasts begin to produce milk to feed a child.

## Chest Development

For those whose bodies produce mostly testosterone, the chest typically develops by growing pectoral muscles ("pecs").

Common experiences include:
* Broadening of the chest.
* Growth of pectoral muscles (size varies significantly between individuals).
* Growth of chest hair (ranging from no hair to a lot of hair).
* Temporary breast tissue growth: During puberty, testosterone levels can drop, causing oestrogen to stimulate breast tissue growth. This can cause tenderness or asymmetry, which is a normal part of development.

## Diversity and Normalcy

There is no such thing as a "normal" body. No two people's chests or breasts look or feel exactly the same. 

* **Bare Reality Project:** Shows the vast variety of breasts and chests across different ages and sizes.
* **CoppaFeel! A-Z of Boobs:** A resource for understanding how varied bodies are and how to look after your health.
* **Health Checks:** It is important to get to know your own body so you can identify any changes that are unusual for you.

## Nipples

Nipples are often sensitive during the early stages of puberty. This sensitivity can fluctuate throughout life due to hormonal changes or the menstrual cycle.

* **Appearance:** Nipples may be darker or paler than the surrounding skin.
* **Areola:** The area around the nipple can be large or small.
* **Hair:** It is very common to have hair growing around the nipples.
* **Shape:** Nipples can be flat, pointy, bumpy, or inverted (looking like a dimple).
* **Response:** Nipples may become erect when cold or sexually excited.

## Pleasure and Erogenous Zones

Breasts and chests are erogenous zones, meaning they are sensitive areas that can produce a sexual response when touched. 

Discovering which parts of your body feel good is an important part of understanding your sexuality. While sensitivity varies, many people find that nipple stimulation provides pleasure. Other common erogenous zones include the neck, ears, back, hips, and genitals.
