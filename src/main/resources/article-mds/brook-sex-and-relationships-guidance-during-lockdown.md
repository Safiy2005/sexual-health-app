# Sex and Relationships: Guidance During Lockdown

Brook education experts have answered questions from young people regarding sex, relationships, and wellbeing during the COVID-19 lockdown periods.

## Coronavirus and Sexual Transmission

There is no evidence that coronavirus is sexually transmitted through vaginal or anal sex. However, it is passed through drops of saliva and by touching infected surfaces. Because sex involves close physical contact, there is a high risk of transmission. It is advised not to have sex if you or your partner are showing symptoms.

## Having Sex During Lockdown

Lockdown restrictions vary based on government guidelines and local tiers. Generally:

* **Living together:** If you live with your partner, have no symptoms, and have not been exposed to COVID-19, having sex is fine.
* **Living apart:** If you do not live with your partner, meeting them may not be allowed under government guidelines. 
* **Support Bubbles:** If both you and your partner live alone, you may be able to form a ‘support bubble,’ allowing you to have physical contact.
* **Established Relationships:** Government guidance (as of September 2020) suggested that people in "established relationships" do not need to socially distance. This requires mutual trust and honest conversation about safety steps.
* **New Relationships and Hook-ups:** If you are single or in the early stages of a relationship, social distancing should be maintained. This is not a safe time for hook-ups or meeting new people for sex.

## Masturbation and Solo Sex

Masturbation is the safest way to enjoy sexual pleasure during lockdown. It is a healthy way to explore your body and relieve stress. While finding privacy may be difficult in shared households, it is important to remember there is no shame in masturbating.

## Accessing Sexual Health Services

If you need sexual health services, do not delay seeking help.
* **Brook Clinics:** Many clinics remain open by appointment only, often providing phone consultations first.
* **Service Capacity:** Some services may be running at reduced capacity. Always ring ahead before traveling.
* **Confidentiality:** You will not be judged or questioned about your lockdown activities when seeking medical care.

## Contraception and Safer Sex

It is essential to keep up with your regular contraception.
* **Access:** Check with your GP or local clinic to ensure you can still access your supplies.
* **Condoms:** Use condoms to reduce the risk of STIs and as a backup method. They are 98% effective when used correctly.
* **Emergency Contraception:** If contraception fails or wasn't used, the emergency contraceptive pill (morning-after pill) is available from clinics, pharmacies, or online (e.g., Fettle or Superdrug).

## Maintaining Relationships Remotely

Being apart can put a strain on relationships. To stay connected, you can:
* Talk openly about how you feel.
* Watch the same TV shows or read the same books together and discuss them.
* Play online games together.
* Use video calls for "get to know you" quizzes or conversation prompts.
* Send letters or care packages.
* Plan fun activities for the future.

## Boundaries and Pressure

If you have decided to follow social distancing rules, your partners and friends should respect that boundary. You should never feel pressured to meet up or do anything that makes you uncomfortable. Pressuring someone is never acceptable.

## Relationship Breakups

Breakups are difficult, especially during isolation. If a relationship ends:
* Acknowledge that maintaining long-distance relationships is challenging.
* Keep in touch with friends and family online.
* Practice self-care through exercise or meditation.
* Access support through services like **Kooth** for online counselling.

## Tension and Safety at Home

Increased time at home can lead to household tension. 
* Practice patience and try to walk away from arguments before they escalate.
* **Abuse:** Abuse is never okay. Lockdown rules do not apply if you are feeling unsafe or experiencing violence. You are allowed to leave your home to seek refuge.
* **Support:** Call the National Domestic Violence hotline at **0808 2000 247**.
* **Emergencies:** Call **999**. If you cannot speak, press **"55"** on a mobile to be put through to the police.

## Online Safety and Sexting

All normal laws regarding online safety and consent apply during lockdown.
* **Sexting (Under 18):** If you are under 18, sharing sexual images or videos is illegal. Once an image is sent, you lose control over who sees it.
* **Sexting (Over 18):** While legal for adults, it should always be consensual and based on clear boundaries.
* **Unknown Contacts:** Be cautious when talking to strangers online and use reporting/blocking tools if you feel uncomfortable.

## Pornography

If you watch porn during lockdown, be mindful of the following:
* **Legality:** Ensure the content is legal (no people under 18).
* **Realism:** Porn is often a fantasy and does not usually reflect realistic sex or consent.
* **Impact:** If watching porn is interfering with your mental health or relationships, seek advice.
* **Respect:** Be mindful of others in your household when viewing porn.
