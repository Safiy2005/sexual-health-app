# Becoming parents with no warning – Hannah and Kieran’s story

Hannah and Kieran share their experience of becoming first-time parents with no warning after a "cryptic pregnancy" when Hannah was 22.

## The Unexpected Discovery

The couple’s journey into parenthood began abruptly. Hannah and Kieran were unaware of the pregnancy until they arrived at the hospital for what they thought were severe stomach pains. 

Hannah recalls telling Kieran the pains felt like giving birth, but she initially dismissed the idea. At the Accident & Emergency department, staff first suggested she was 25 weeks pregnant and nearly sent her home. However, it was soon discovered that she was at full term and already in active labour. Within 24 hours of arriving at the hospital, they became parents to their daughter, Elsie.

## Understanding Cryptic Pregnancy

A cryptic pregnancy occurs when a person does not look or feel typically pregnant, often resulting in the pregnancy going undetected until the final weeks or even birth. Several factors contributed to Hannah’s experience:

* **Medication Interference:** Hannah was on a new medication that counteracted her contraceptive pill. It is vital to consult healthcare professionals about potential drug interactions with hormonal contraception.
* **Overlapping Symptoms:** The side effects of her new medication (such as achy joints) mirrored early pregnancy symptoms.
* **Irregular Menstrual Cycles:** As Hannah had never had regular periods (ranging from 2-6 months apart), the absence of a period did not signal a change.
* **Timing of Pregnancy Tests:** An early pregnancy test came back negative, likely because it was taken too soon for the hormones to be detectable. Pregnancy tests should generally be taken at least three weeks after unprotected sex or suspected contraception failure.
* **Lifestyle Factors:** High stress and a busy work schedule led Hannah to disconnect from her body’s signals, attributing any physical changes to work-related exhaustion or medication.

## Decision Making and Support

The initial shock led the couple to consider adoption, as they felt unprepared for the sudden responsibility. However, the support of their families provided a "blanket of comfort" that helped them realize they could manage the transition.

While the original plan was for Elsie to stay with Hannah’s father temporarily, the couple quickly adapted to caring for her themselves. They emphasize that a strong support system of friends and family was essential to their success.

## Challenges of Being a Young Parent

Hannah and Kieran highlight several difficulties faced by young, working parents:

* **Financial Pressure:** Both parents often need to work full-time to support the family, leading to exhaustion and a difficult work-life balance.
* **Childcare Costs:** In the UK, nursery costs can be prohibitively expensive, and government subsidies often do not begin until a child is two or three years old.
* **Parental Leave:** Kieran noted that statutory paternity leave (often only 2-3 weeks) is insufficient for supporting a partner and bonding with a new child.
* **Social Isolation:** Because they did not attend prenatal classes, it was difficult to connect with other parents. Hannah often felt out of place in parenting groups where other parents were significantly older or in different life stages.

## Societal Stigma and Identity

The couple noted a disparity in how young mothers and fathers are perceived. While Kieran felt he received praise for "sticking around," Hannah felt she faced judgment and "looks in the street" from people who assumed she was too young to be a mother. 

Hannah also described the "trauma" of the sudden transition, noting that it takes time to rediscover one's identity after the initial pressure of proving oneself as a capable parent has passed.

## Advice for New Parents

Kieran and Hannah offer the following advice for those facing unexpected parenthood:

* **Have faith in yourself:** Humans are highly adaptable. While it may feel impossible at first, you will find the strength to manage.
* **Don't strive for perfection:** A baby primarily needs love and comfort. Trying to "put on a show" for others will only lead to burnout.
* **Ignore judgment:** Focus on your child and your own well-being rather than the opinions of strangers.
* **Accept that there is no "right time":** Every child is different, and most parents are learning as they go, regardless of their age or level of preparation.
