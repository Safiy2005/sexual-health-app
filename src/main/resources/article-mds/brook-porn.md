# Porn: Important Things to Know

## What is Pornography?

Pornography, or 'porn' for short, refers to explicit images or videos that show sexual activity or sexual images designed to arouse the viewer. It has existed for thousands of years in various forms.

## Why People Watch Porn

* **Arousal:** The primary reason is to become sexually excited, often during masturbation or with a partner.
* **Education and Exploration:** To learn about particular sexual acts, discover new aspects of sex and sexuality.
* **Social Reasons:** To show off or have a laugh with friends.

## Accidental Exposure

It's possible to come across porn by accident, especially online, when searching for factual information or unrelated topics. If this happens and you feel uncomfortable:

* Close the browser immediately.
* Talk to a trusted adult to discuss any confusion, worry, or upset.

## Not Everyone Enjoys Porn

* It is completely normal not to be interested in or enjoy watching porn.
* Reasons for not watching include religious, social, or cultural beliefs, or simply finding it unappealing.
* Some individuals, like those who are asexual, may have less desire for sex.
* You should never feel pressured to watch or enjoy porn.

## Staying Safe and Ethical Considerations

* Only engage with content you are comfortable with.
* Never pressure others to watch porn.
* It's okay to enjoy things in porn that you wouldn't do in real life; your porn use does not define your sexuality.
* Be aware that some fetishes and kinks depicted in porn can be harmful or promote negative stereotypes.
* Illegal content should never be shown or viewed.

## Porn vs. Real Life Sex

Porn is created for entertainment and is often scripted and not reflective of real-life sexual experiences. Key differences include:

* **Consent:** While actors in porn consent beforehand, porn may not always explicitly show this, nor the agreement for specific acts. Never pressure someone into sex; that is not consent.
* **Types of Sex:** Porn often overrepresents penetrative sex and may normalize aggressive acts like choking or slapping. Anal sex is also common, and specific considerations are important if you choose to engage in it.
* **Condoms and Safe Sex:** Condoms are rarely shown in porn. Actors use effective contraception and are tested for STIs, but withdrawal alone is not reliable contraception. Condoms are essential for STI protection regardless of pregnancy risk.
* **Orgasms:** Orgasms can be faked in porn. Real-life orgasms, particularly for people with vaginas, often require direct clitoral stimulation and rarely occur simultaneously for both partners.
* **Body Types:** The bodies depicted in porn are often not representative of the general population and may be enhanced through cosmetic surgery.
* **Arousal:** The time it takes to become aroused is often downplayed in porn.
* **Lube:** While not always shown, lube can enhance the experience of penetrative sex.
* **Breaks:** Porn is edited to appear continuous, but real sex involves breaks.
* **Awkwardness:** Real-life sex can be awkward, clumsy, and involve fumbling, laughter, and talking, which is completely normal.

## Unhealthy Porn Use

Watching porn and being interested in sex is natural. However, excessive or unhealthy use can impact self-esteem, relationships, and overall well-being. Consider if you are:

* Comparing your body or sexual performance to those in porn.
* Struggling to become aroused or enjoy intimacy with a partner.
* Seeking increasingly extreme content.
* Copying pornographic behavior without partner consent.
* Watching content that disgusts you or conflicts with your morals.
* Experiencing anxiety or shame.
* Letting porn dominate your thoughts or take time away from other activities.
* Altering your expectations of real-life sex.

If you answered "yes" to any of these, you may benefit from limiting your porn use or seeking support.

## Tips for Healthy Porn Use

* Talk to friends and partners about porn.
* Avoid seeking increasingly extreme content.
* If you typically masturbate with porn, try without it.
* Consider using internet controls to limit access.
* If over 18, explore ethical porn, which is legally produced, respects performers' safety and diversity, and often focuses on female pleasure.

## Seeking Support

If porn causes distress, impacts your well-being, or harms others, reaching out for help is a positive step. You can speak to a trusted adult or contact organizations like:

* Brook or other sexual health services
* Childline
* The Mix

Resources like BISH and The Mix offer information about porn and sex without requiring you to watch explicit content.
