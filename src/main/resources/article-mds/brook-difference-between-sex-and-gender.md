# What is the Difference Between Sex and Gender?

Gender and sex are two separate terms, despite people commonly thinking they mean the same thing. Understanding the distinction is important for understanding identity and social expectations.

## Sex

‘Sex’ refers to our biological sexual characteristics, which include external genitals, internal reproductive organs, chromosomes, and hormones. Although all these factors contribute to someone’s sex, people are typically defined as male or female at birth based on the observation of their external genitals.

* **AMAB (Assigned Male at Birth):** Recorded as male based on being born with a penis and testicles.
* **AFAB (Assigned Female at Birth):** Recorded as female based on being born with a vulva and vagina.

### Variations in Sex Development
Some babies are born with external genitalia that do not fit traditional definitions of male or female. This is referred to as:
* **VSD/VSC:** Differences or variations in sex development / variations in sex characteristics.
* **Intersex:** A term used by many people with VSDs/VSCs.

While sex has historically been understood as a binary (male and female), many people now view sex as being on a spectrum, with individuals being more or less male or female across various biological factors.

## Gender Identity

The word gender is often used to refer to how a person sees or feels about themselves. It describes the personal sense of feeling or knowing that you are a man, a woman, or a non-binary person. This personal experience is referred to as gender identity.

* Someone’s gender identity is not necessarily connected to their sex assigned at birth.
* Some people feel their identity is rooted in their body, while others feel their body and gender sense are distinct.
* Feelings about the relationship between body and gender can be fluid or change over time.

## Gender as a Social Construct

Gender also refers to a collection of ideas about human behavior, actions, and roles in relation to ‘masculinity’ and ‘femininity’. Society "constructs" these ideas by developing cultural expectations over time regarding what is considered "natural" for men and women.

### Gender Stereotypes
A stereotype is a widely held and overly simplified idea of how a person should behave, what they should be interested in, or how they should look. 

* **Masculinity:** A set of ideas and behaviors associated with men due to a society’s culture.
* **Femininity:** A set of ideas and behaviors associated with women due to a society’s culture.

When expectations about gender are strict or inflexible, they can limit opportunities, damage confidence, and lead to discrimination. Many people do not conform to these stereotypes simply because they do not enjoy the clothes, hobbies, or behaviors expected of their gender.

## Gender Expression

Gender expression describes the outward ways people choose to express aspects of their gender. This is separate from gender identity.

* **Elements of Expression:** This includes clothing, hairstyles, behavior, and mannerisms.
* **Autonomy:** People can choose to express themselves however they wish. Gender expression does not have to "match" gender identity or biological sex.
* **Variations:** Expression can be masculine, feminine, both, or neither.
