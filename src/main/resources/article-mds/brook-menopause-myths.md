# Menopause Myths: Get the Facts

The menopause can be a challenging topic to talk about for some people. This can lead to a lack of understanding and accurate knowledge about what the menopause is and how it affects women and people with uteruses. Here we explore some of the most common myths and explain why they are not accurate.

## Myth: Menopause only happens in your 50s

Although the average age for reaching the menopause is between 44 and 56, there are many reasons why someone may experience it earlier:

* **Natural onset:** Some people begin experiencing perimenopause symptoms as early as their 30s or 40s.
* **Surgical menopause:** This occurs if someone has their ovaries removed. Without the hormones the ovaries produce, they experience a sudden menopause.
* **Chemical menopause:** Certain medications used to treat conditions like endometriosis deliberately cause an artificial menopause. In most cases, this is temporary and reversible.

## Myth: Hot flushes are the only symptom

Hot flushes are the most visible and common symptom of the perimenopause, but they are by no means the only one. 

* **Mental and emotional impact:** For many people, the psychological effects are just as significant as the physical ones.
* **Severity of flushes:** Hot flushes can occur at any time, day or night. They are often accompanied by mood changes and a lack of concentration. 
* **Frequency:** Some people experience hot flushes as frequently as every hour, lasting around four minutes, which can be highly disruptive to daily life.

## Myth: Your sex life is over

It is true that hormonal changes during the perimenopause and menopause can cause tiredness, lower libido (desire), loss of sensation in the clitoris, and vaginal dryness. However, for many people, the menopause can actually lead to an increase in libido because they:

* No longer worry about becoming pregnant.
* Feel more confident and less concerned about external judgments.
* Prioritize their own pleasure and self-care.

**Tips for managing changes to your sex life:**
* **Communicate:** Talk to your partner(s) about the difficulties you are experiencing.
* **Use lubricant:** Lube is essential for helping with vaginal dryness.
* **Explore different types of sex:** Intimacy isn't just about penetration; you can explore arousal through different techniques and sex toys.
* **Oestrogen creams:** Your GP can prescribe these to help with vaginal dryness.
* **Manage stress:** Libido is often linked to stress; exercise and relaxation can help.

## Myth: You can’t get pregnant during the menopause

You can still get pregnant until you reach the menopause or the age of 55, whichever comes first.

* **Contraception:** You need to continue using contraception for two years after your periods stop. If you are in perimenopause and do not want to get pregnant, it is important to use contraception even if your periods are irregular.
* **Testing:** Doctors can use a follicle-stimulating hormone (FSH) test to help determine if you have reached menopause. However, this is not 100% reliable and is not accurate if you are using the combined pill.

## Myth: Menopause is something to be embarrassed about

Menopause is still a taboo topic for many, despite half the population experiencing it. The historical stigma—often involving jokes or labeling people as "hormonal"—can make it uncomfortable to talk about. However, menopause is a natural stage of life. Talking about it openly helps you get the support you need at home and in the workplace.

## Myth: You just have to put up with symptoms

Many people feel they are "making a fuss" by seeing a doctor, but there is no reason to suffer in silence. If symptoms impact your daily life, work, or social life, you should seek medical advice.

* **HRT (Hormone Replacement Therapy):** This is the most effective treatment for menopause symptoms, including hot flushes, night sweats, and mood swings. It is very safe and also supports bone health.
* **HRT Risks:** While combined HRT (oestrogen and progesterone) can be associated with a very small increased risk of breast cancer, for most people, the benefits of symptom relief far outweigh the risks.
* **Support Groups:** Talking to others at the same stage of life can provide valuable support and advice.
