# How Porn Affected My First Sexual Experience: Sian’s Story

Sian, 25, shares how her partner’s use of pornography shaped her first sexual experience as a teenager. Her story highlights the impact of limited sex education and the unrealistic expectations created by explicit online content.

## The Gap in Sex Education

At age 14, Sian’s understanding of sex and intimacy was extremely limited. Her previous experiences had not gone beyond holding hands or kissing. When she began a new relationship, her primary sources of information were:

* **School Sessions:** Focus was heavily placed on sexually transmitted infections (STIs), with little explanation regarding how they are contracted or the nuances of intimacy.
* **Home Life:** A lack of open conversation regarding "the birds and the bees."
* **Peers and Partners:** Information was filtered through friends and partners who were openly consuming pornography online.

## Expectations Shaped by Pornography

Sian realized in hindsight that her partner's approach to sexual experimentation came directly from the pornography he watched. Because pornography was easily accessible on sites like Pornhub, it became a primary reference point for young people lacking formal education on healthy relationships.

## Body Image and Personal Boundaries

A significant turning point occurred when Sian's partner made a comment about her pubic hair, stating he "wasn’t expecting her to be so hairy." This expectation, likely fueled by the groomed aesthetics common in pornography, led to:

* **Immediate Shame:** Sian felt mortified and immediately changed her physical appearance.
* **Compliance:** A sense of shame stayed with her, leading her to allow her partner to guide their sexual activities because she believed his "knowledge" (derived from porn) was superior to her own.
* **Imitation:** The couple engaged in acts like oral sex and explicit texting, essentially "copying" the scenes seen in videos.

## Pressure and the Complexity of Consent

Understanding and maintaining consent was complicated by several external factors:

* **Fear of Loss:** Sian worried that saying "no" would end the relationship.
* **Personal Vulnerability:** At the time, Sian was seeking comfort due to a family bereavement (her grandfather’s terminal illness).
* **Peer Pressure:** In her school environment, there was no privacy. Classmates compared notes on sexual experiences and porn consumption, creating a "strange sense of status" mixed with disgust and fear of family discovery.

## Conclusion: The Need for Better Education

The relationship eventually ended when Sian began withdrawing and setting boundaries, such as refusing penetrative sex. Reflecting on the experience, Sian notes that both she and her partner were failed by a lack of proper education.

Key takeaways for improving sexual health outcomes for young people include:

* **Crucial Sex Education:** Comprehensive education is necessary to prevent young people from relying on pornography and social media for information.
* **Open Conversations:** Teaching about consent and having honest discussions can reduce misinformation.
* **Delaying Experiences:** Proper support can help young people delay sexual experiences until they feel truly ready and informed.

Sian now works with Brook to help ensure other young people have access to the education and support she lacked.
