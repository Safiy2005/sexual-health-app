# PrEP and PEP

PrEP and PEP are two medical methods used to prevent HIV transmission. While condoms remain the most effective way to prevent the transmission of HIV and other sexually transmitted infections (STIs), these medical interventions offer significant protection for those at risk.

## PrEP (Pre-exposure Prophylaxis)

PrEP is a medication for people who do not have HIV. It is a pill taken before sex that offers near-complete protection from HIV (99% effective). It is a highly effective tool for individuals at risk of HIV, particularly those who may find it difficult to use condoms every time.

* **Composition:** PrEP consists of two antiretroviral medicines, Tenofovir and Emtricitabine. These drugs have been used in HIV treatment for many years and are often known by the brand name Truvada, though generic versions with the same active ingredients are also available.
* **Protection Limits:** PrEP is not a vaccine. It only provides protection against HIV as long as it is taken as prescribed.
* **Other STIs:** PrEP does not protect against other STIs. To prevent other infections, you should use condoms during oral, anal, or vaginal sex and attend regular sexual health screenings.

## Who should take PrEP?

You may want to consider taking PrEP if you are HIV-negative and any of the following apply:

* You are at high risk of HIV and do not always use condoms.
* You have recently been diagnosed with an STI (specifically syphilis or a rectal infection).
* You use recreational drugs for "chemsex" (such as crystal meth, mephedrone, or GHB).
* Your partner is HIV-positive and is not taking their HIV treatment regularly.
* You have recently needed to take PEP (post-exposure prophylaxis).

## Where can I get PrEP?

PrEP is available for free via the NHS at sexual health clinics throughout the UK. It is accessible to everyone, regardless of immigration status.

* **Sexual Health Clinics:** If your local clinic does not offer PrEP, they should be able to direct you to a service that does.
* **LVNDR:** This service, built for the LGBT+ community, also provides free access to PrEP.
* **Buying Online:** While it is possible to buy PrEP online, it is not recommended due to the risks associated with unregulated medication. If you choose to purchase PrEP online, you should ensure it is from a reliable source and seek support from a sexual health clinic for essential health checks and monitoring.

## PrEP Frequently Asked Questions

### Will PrEP affect my contraception?
PrEP is safe to use with all hormonal contraception, including the pill, patch, ring, or implant. There is no interaction between PrEP and hormonal contraceptives that would reduce the effectiveness of either.

### Can I take PrEP if I am pregnant or breastfeeding?
Yes, PrEP is safe to use if you are pregnant or if you are breast/chestfeeding.

## PEP (Post-exposure Prophylaxis)

PEP is a course of medication taken after a potential exposure to HIV.

* **Timing:** PEP must be started within 72 hours (three days) of the potential exposure to be effective. It is most effective when started within the first 24 hours.
* **Treatment:** It involves a 28-day course of powerful antiretroviral drugs. It is not guaranteed to work and is usually only recommended after high-risk exposure (for example, if a partner is known to be HIV-positive).
* **Access:** You can obtain PEP from sexual health clinics or hospital A&E (Emergency) departments.
