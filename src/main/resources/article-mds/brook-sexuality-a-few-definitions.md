# Sexuality: a few definitions

The language we use when talking about sexuality is very important because it can have an effect on people’s wellbeing. It can be daunting; it might seem like you will never know it all, or like it changes without you realising, or you might be worried about ‘getting it wrong’. 

What is important to remember is that language is personal; your meanings or use of these words may be different to how other people use them. Not everyone will want to be known by the same terms so the most important thing is to be respectful of someone else’s identity and the labels they choose to use. In turn, you deserve that respect back.

## A

* **Allosexual**: Someone who experiences sexual attraction, desire or sexual interest directed at other people. The opposite of asexual.
* **Ally**: Someone who supports the LGBT+ community and challenges discrimination against LGBT+ people. This can be a person who is not LGBT+, and it can also be someone who is a member of one part of the LGBT+ community supporting members of other parts of the community.
* **Aromantic**: Someone who experiences limited or no romantic attraction to others, or has little or no interest in forming or pursuing romantic relationships.
* **Asexual/ace**: Someone who experiences limited or no sexual attraction, interest or desire.

## B

* **Bi erasure**: The ignoring or questioning of the validity of bisexuality, as well as the use of terminology that excludes bisexuality, such as ‘gay marriage’, ‘gay/straight relationship’. This can come from both outside of and within LGBT+ communities.
* **Biphobia**: Prejudice and discrimination towards, and/or fear and dislike or someone who is bisexual, based on their sexual orientation.
* **Bisexual/bi**: Someone who is romantically or sexually attracted to people of more than one gender. Sometimes this word is used more specifically to describe people who are attracted to people of their own gender and people of other genders. Bisexual people don’t necessarily experience attraction to different genders in the same way, at the same time, or to the same degree.

## C

* **Closeted/in the closet**: A term used to describe someone who identifies as LGBT+ in some way, but has not openly told anyone about this aspect of their identity.
* **Coming out**: When someone tells someone else about their LGBT+ identity. Coming out is rarely a once-in-a-lifetime event, as many LGBT+ people may want or need to come out to each new person they meet.
* **Chemsex**: Sexual activity whilst under the influence of drugs. It is most common among gay or bisexual men.
* **Cisgender**: Someone whose gender identity is the same as the sex they were assigned at birth.

## D

* **Demisexual**: Someone who experiences limited or no feelings of sexual attraction to anyone they have not already formed a deep romantic connection with.
* **Dead name**: The name a transgender person is given at birth but no longer uses.

## G

* **Gay**: Someone who is largely or exclusively romantically and/or sexually attracted to people of the same gender. The term can be used to describe anyone regardless of gender identity but is more commonly used to describe men.

## H

* **Heterosexual (straight)**: Someone who is largely or exclusively romantically and/or sexually attracted to people of a different gender, typically a man who is only attracted to women or a woman who is only attracted to men.
* **Heterosexism**: The belief that non-heterosexual people are inferior to heterosexual people based on the assumption that heterosexuality is ‘normal’ and preferable.
* **Heteronormativity**: The assumption that all people are heterosexual, and the lack of consideration that people with other sexualities exist.
* **Homonormativity**: The privileging of a particular idea of what it means to be LGBT+, based on conflating wider LGBT+ experiences with a dominant gay, male, cisgendered (and usually white) experience.
* **Homosexual**: A term used to describe someone who is almost exclusively attracted to people of the same gender. Some people find this term too medical and prefer ‘gay’, ‘lesbian’ or ‘queer’.
* **Homophobia**: Prejudice and discrimination towards someone who is attracted to people of the same gender as themselves, based on their sexual orientation.

## I

* **Intersectionality**: An approach thinking about intersecting or overlapping areas of power, discrimination, and identity (e.g., how race, gender, and sexual orientation interact).

## L

* **Lesbian**: Generally considered to be a woman who is largely or exclusively romantically and/or sexually attracted to other women. This identity may also be used by non-binary people.
* **Lesbophobia**: Prejudice and discrimination towards someone who is a lesbian, based on their sexual orientation. This is the specific intersection of homophobia and misogyny.
* **LGBT+**: The acronym for lesbian, gay, bisexual, transgender and queer. The ‘+’ is used to be more inclusive of other identities.

## M

* **Monogamy**: A relationship dynamic where both partners are expected to be romantically and sexually exclusive to each other.
* **Monosexuality**: An overarching term to describe sexual orientations where attraction is directed towards only one gender, such as lesbian, gay or straight.
* **MSM/MLM**: Stands for ‘men who have sex with men’/’men loving men’. Used in healthcare to be inclusive of men who may not identify as LGBT+ but engage in same-sex activity.

## N

* **Non-monogamy**: A relationship dynamic where one, both or all partners are consensually not romantically and/or sexually exclusive.

## O

* **Outing/Out**: Telling someone about someone else’s sexual orientation or gender identity without their consent.

## P

* **Pansexual/pan**: Describes someone who is romantically and/or sexually attracted to others regardless of their gender identity.
* **Polyamorous/poly**: Describes someone who has or is open to having more than one sexual and/or romantic relationship at a time with the full awareness and consent of everyone involved.
* **Pride**: Having a positive view of being part of the LGBT+ community; also refers to worldwide events celebrating LGBT+ culture and protesting discrimination.

## Q

* **Queer**: An umbrella term used by some to describe members of the LGBT+ community or an identity that rejects specific labels. While reclaimed by many, it has a history as a slur.
* **QPOC/QTIPOC**: Stands for ‘Queer People of Colour’ or ‘Queer, Transgender and Intersex People of Colour’.
* **Questioning**: A term used to describe people who are in the process of exploring their sexual orientation or gender identity.

## R

* **Romantic attraction**: An emotional connection to someone that is separate from sexual attraction.
* **Romantic orientation**: How a person feels romantically about people of various genders.

## S

* **Sexual attraction**: Desiring sexual contact with a specific other person or group of people.
* **Sexual orientation**: How a person feels sexually about people of various genders. Sexual activity is not always representative of sexual orientation.
* **Sexuality**: A holistic term for someone’s sexual behaviours, attractions, likes, dislikes, kinks and preferences.

## W

* **WSW/WLW**: Stands for ‘women who have sex with women’/’women loving women’. Used in sexual health services to be inclusive of women who have sex with other women but may not identify as LGBT+.
