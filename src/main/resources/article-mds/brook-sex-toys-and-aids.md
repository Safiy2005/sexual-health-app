# Sex toys and aids

Sex toys can increase pleasure and make sex easier for some people. Sex toys or aids are objects used for arousal, pleasure, or to make sex more accessible. They can be used alone or with a partner and come in many forms to suit different bodies and preferences.

## Personal choice and legality

Sex toys are more accessible than ever, frequently appearing in mainstream media and social media. However, nobody should feel pressured to use them; many people have fulfilling sex lives without them.

In the UK, it is not illegal to buy sex toys if you are under 18, but it is illegal to enter premises that exclusively sell sex toys (sex shops).

## Safety and STI prevention

When purchasing sex toys, ensure they meet safety standards. Look for the **CE mark**, which indicates the device is safe for use. 

Sexually transmitted infections (STIs) can be passed on through shared sex toys. To reduce risks, you should:
* Avoid sharing sex toys with other people.
* Clean them thoroughly after every use according to the packaging instructions.
* Use the correct type of lubricant (following the toy's instructions to avoid damaging the material).
* Use a new condom each time a toy is used on a different person or moved between different parts of the body (e.g., moving from the vagina to the anus).
* Regularly check for scratches, tears, or cracks, as these can harbor germs.

## Accessibility and disability

Disabilities can influence how someone experiences sex and pleasure. Sex toys and aids come in various shapes and functions to help make sex more accessible and fulfilling. For example:
* Remote-controlled toys can assist those with limited hand movement.
* Specific aids are designed to support different physical needs, ensuring that pleasure is inclusive.

## Supporting sexual health and recovery

Sex toys can play a supportive role in various life stages and health conditions:
* **Recovery from trauma:** Toys can help survivors of sexual assault or rape reconnect with their bodies and pleasure in a safe, controlled way. Some toys are designed to look like everyday objects or include features that monitor breathing to encourage relaxation.
* **Physical conditions:** For those with conditions like vulvodynia or vaginismus, sex toys can help explore non-penetrative pleasure or assist in gradual therapy.
* **Life changes:** Toys can be useful during or after menopause, cancer treatment, childbirth, or a hysterectomy.
* **Erectile function:** Aids can assist those with penises experiencing erectile problems.

## LGBT+ inclusivity

Sex toys can be a significant part of sex for LGBT+ people. Many toys are now designed specifically for transgender bodies or to facilitate diverse ways of giving and receiving pleasure beyond traditional penetration.

## Consent and communication

Like any other sexual activity, using sex toys with a partner requires mutual, clearly given consent. 

If a partner suggests using a sex toy, it is usually a reflection of their personal preference or curiosity rather than a reflection on your ability to provide pleasure. It is helpful to talk openly about your feelings, likes, and dislikes to ensure both partners feel comfortable and respected.
