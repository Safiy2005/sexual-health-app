# Foreskins and Circumcision

The foreskin is the fold of skin that covers the head of the penis when it is soft. When the penis is hard, the foreskin typically moves back so the head of the penis is exposed. Some people have a foreskin long enough to cover the head of their penis even during an erection.

## Circumcision

Circumcision is the surgical removal of some or all of the foreskin. There are two main reasons why circumcisions are carried out:

* **Religious or cultural reasons:** This is a common practice in Jewish and Islamic communities, many African communities, and in the USA. Most cultural circumcisions are carried out on young boys.
* **Medical reasons:** Circumcision may be used as a treatment of last resort for conditions such as a tight foreskin (phimosis) or recurrent infection of the foreskin and head of the penis (balanitis).

## Tight Foreskin (Phimosis)

Phimosis is a condition where the foreskin is too tight to easily roll back over the head of the penis during an erection. This can be uncomfortable, but unless it causes symptoms, it is not usually a problem. 

Symptoms of a problematic tight foreskin include:
* Redness
* Swelling
* Discharge
* **Balanitis:** Inflammation of the head of the penis (the glans).
* **Balanoposthitis:** Inflammation of both the glans and the foreskin.

A tight foreskin can also be associated with sexually transmitted infections (STIs) or skin conditions such as eczema or psoriasis.

## Treatment and Hygiene

Balanitis and balanoposthitis can be treated with a combination of good hygiene and medical interventions:
* Antibiotics or antifungal creams (depending on the cause of the infection).
* Prescribed steroid creams to help with a tight foreskin.
* In some cases, surgery may be considered to remove the foreskin.

## When to Seek Help

If you have any swelling, discharge, or redness, or if you are worried about the health of your penis, you should seek advice from a doctor or nurse.

* **Seek medical advice:** If you are unable to pull your foreskin back.
* **Go to A&E (Emergency):** If you pull your foreskin back and it gets stuck and will not retract. This condition is called **paraphimosis** and requires immediate medical attention.
