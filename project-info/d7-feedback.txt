TESTING

I have judged that the most appropriate descriptions from the mark scheme for your work are:

[Testing] (70 - 80%) Good automated unit tests are presented with clear evidence. A range of techniques (component, integration, boundary, regression) are used. Acceptance criteria and scenarios are tested.

I have awarded a mark of 79%

TESTING COMMENTS

The testing section is very strong and well-developed overall. It clearly explains multiple testing strategies (unit, component, integration, boundary, partition, regression), and most follow a consistent what / why / examples structure (pages 7–12) , which is very well presented.

In terms of strengths:
The testing strategy is very clearly explained, with strong justification for each method. For example, integration testing is well motivated in terms of cross-service workflows (e.g. search + preferences + ranking), showing good depth.

The unit and integration testing coverage is strong, with a detailed test directory (pages 8–9) and concrete examples such as ContentPreferencesServiceTest and NotificationServiceTest, indicating systematic testing.

The scenario testing is particularly strong (from page 12 onwards) . It includes detailed user scenarios, broken down into specific test points (A1, A2, etc.), and each is explicitly linked to acceptance criteria (e.g. D7-11-03, D7-2-03), which is excellent.

Additionally, scenario testing is supported by references to unit and regression tests, making it more robust than simple step descriptions.

However, there are some issues:
First, there is little to no presentation of actual testing results. The section focuses on what is tested and how, but does not clearly show outcomes (e.g. pass/fail or observed results).

Second, scenario testing is partly written as test planning. Phrases like “what we will test” (page 13) make it feel more like design rather than executed testing.

Overall, this is a very strong testing section in terms of structure, strategy, and alignment with acceptance criteria, but it would benefit from clearer presentation of actual results and supporting evidence.
